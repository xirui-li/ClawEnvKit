EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This report evaluates the current software platform landscape deployed across Meridian Financial Services Group, a mid-sized financial services firm headquartered in Charlotte, North Carolina, with approximately 2,400 employees across 11 regional offices. The assessment covers the period from January 2023 through August 2024 and examines three primary platforms: Salesforce Sales Cloud (Enterprise Edition), ServiceNow IT Service Management (Tokyo release), and Microsoft Azure DevOps. The goal is to identify active use cases, measure platform utilization against licensed capacity, and document functional gaps requiring remediation or supplemental tooling.

Key findings indicate that Meridian is utilizing only 61% of its licensed Salesforce capacity, ServiceNow ticket resolution times exceed the industry benchmark of 4.2 hours by an average of 2.8 hours, and Azure DevOps pipeline automation covers just 44% of eligible development workflows. Estimated annual cost of underutilization across all three platforms is $1.34 million.

---

SECTION 1: SCOPE AND METHODOLOGY

The assessment was conducted between July 15 and August 30, 2024, by a six-person team including two senior platform architects, two business analysts, and two data analysts. Methodology included:

- Structured interviews with 47 stakeholders across IT, Operations, Sales, and Compliance departments
- Platform telemetry extraction from Salesforce usage dashboards (data pulled August 5, 2024)
- ServiceNow Performance Analytics reports covering Q1 and Q2 2024
- Azure DevOps pipeline run logs from January 1 through July 31, 2024
- Benchmarking against Gartner's 2024 CRM Utilization Index and the HDI 2023 Technical Support Benchmark Report

All monetary figures are expressed in USD. Platform licensing costs were sourced directly from Meridian's IT procurement records provided by Chief Information Officer Dana Kowalski on July 18, 2024.

SECTION 3: SALESFORCE SALES CLOUD — USE CASE ANALYSIS

3.1 Active Use Cases

The following use cases are confirmed active as of August 2024:

- Lead and opportunity management for the Commercial Banking division (approximately 1,100 active opportunities tracked)
- Contact management for 87,000 client records across the Southeast region
- Email integration via Salesforce Inbox for 142 sales representatives
- Quarterly pipeline reporting using 14 standard dashboards
- Case management for the Wealth Management team (averaging 340 cases per month)

3.2 Identified Gaps

Gap 1 — Marketing Cloud Integration Not Deployed
Meridian purchased a Marketing Cloud Growth Edition add-on in April 2023 at an additional $96,000 annually. As of the assessment date, zero campaigns have been launched through the platform. The primary barrier cited by Marketing Director Priya Nambiar is insufficient internal training resources and a lack of a dedicated Marketing Cloud administrator.

Gap 2 — Einstein Activity Capture Disabled
Einstein Activity Capture, included in the Enterprise Edition license, is disabled for all 340 users. Sales Operations Manager Tom Reardon confirmed that the feature was evaluated in Q3 2023 but deactivated due to concerns about data residency compliance. A compliant configuration path was identified by Salesforce Technical Account Manager Lisa Chung in February 2024 but has not been implemented.

Gap 3 — CPQ Module Underused
Salesforce CPQ was activated for the Commercial Banking team in June 2023. Only 19 of 78 eligible users have completed onboarding. Quote generation through CPQ accounts for 24% of total quotes produced, with the remaining 76% still generated via manual Excel templates.

SECTION 4: SERVICENOW ITSM — USE CASE ANALYSIS

4.1 Active Use Cases

- Incident management: 4,200 tickets processed per month on average (Q1–Q2 2024)
- Service Catalog: 38 active catalog items covering hardware requests, software provisioning, and access management
- SLA tracking for Tier 1 and Tier 2 support (SLA compliance rate: 71.4% against a target of 90%)

4.2 Performance Metrics vs. Benchmarks

Metric | Meridian Actual | HDI 2023 Benchmark | Variance
Mean Time to Resolve (MTTR) | 7.0 hours | 4.2 hours | +2.8 hours
First Contact Resolution Rate | 58% | 74% | -16 points
Ticket Reopen Rate | 12.3% | 6.1% | +6.2 points
Self-Service Deflection Rate | 9% | 31% | -22 points

4.3 Identified Gaps

Gap 4 — Problem Management Module Inactive
The Problem Management module, included in Meridian's license since the Tokyo upgrade in November 2023, has never been configured. Root cause analysis is currently performed ad hoc via email threads, contributing to a recurring incident rate of approximately 18% (incidents with no documented root cause that recur within 60 days).

Gap 5 — CMDB Accuracy Below Threshold
The Configuration Management Database contains 14,200 configuration items, but an audit conducted by IT Asset Manager Carlos Vega in May 2024 found that 38% of records were outdated or inaccurate. This directly impairs Change Management workflows and is a contributing factor to the 71.4% SLA compliance rate.

Gap 6 — Virtual Agent Not Deployed
ServiceNow Virtual Agent, licensed as part of the Tokyo release, has not been deployed. Deployment is estimated to reduce Tier 1 ticket volume by 20–25% based on comparable implementations at firms of similar size, according to ServiceNow's 2024 Customer Outcomes Report.

SECTION 5: AZURE DEVOPS — USE CASE ANALYSIS

5.1 Active Use Cases

- CI/CD pipelines for 12 production applications across the Core Banking and Digital Channels teams
- Sprint planning and backlog management for 6 active Agile teams (approximately 74 developers)
- Artifact repository hosting for 3 internal shared libraries
- Test plan management for the QA team (14 testers, 1,800 test cases documented)

5.2 Identified Gaps

Gap 7 — Manual Deployment Persists for Legacy Systems
Of 22 applications in Meridian's production portfolio, 10 are still deployed manually. These 10 applications represent 63% of total deployment incidents in 2023 (source: Meridian Incident Report IR-2023-Annual, published February 2024). Pipeline migration for these systems has been deferred twice, most recently in March 2024, due to resource constraints in the Platform Engineering team.

Gap 8 — Security Scanning Not Integrated
Azure DevOps Advanced Security, available to Meridian under its existing Microsoft Enterprise Agreement signed January 2024, has not been enabled. Static application security testing (SAST) is currently performed manually by a two-person security team, resulting in an average scan cycle of 18 days versus an industry standard of 3–5 days for automated tooling.

Gap 9 — Boards Adoption Fragmented
Three of the six Agile teams use Azure Boards for sprint tracking. The remaining three teams use a combination of Jira Software (self-hosted, version 9.4) and physical Kanban boards. This fragmentation prevents unified velocity reporting and was cited by VP of Engineering Marcus Delacroix as a top-three operational pain point.

SECTION 7: RECOMMENDATIONS AND ESTIMATED IMPACT

The assessment team recommends a 90-day remediation sprint beginning October 1, 2024, targeting the four P1 gaps. Closing these gaps is projected to:

- Reduce annual platform waste by approximately $410,000 through improved utilization
- Improve ServiceNow SLA compliance from 71.4% to an estimated 84% within two quarters
- Reduce deployment-related incidents by 40% based on historical data from comparable pipeline migrations
- Bring CPQ adoption from 24% to a target of 70% by Q1 2025, reducing quote preparation time from an average of 3.4 hours to 45 minutes per quote

A follow-up assessment is recommended for March 2025 to measure progress against these targets.
