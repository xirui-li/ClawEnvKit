EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This report evaluates the current software platform landscape deployed across Meridian Financial Services' operational divisions, assessing active use cases, adoption rates, and capability gaps identified between Q1 and Q3 2024. The analysis covers four primary platforms: Salesforce Sales Cloud (Enterprise Edition), Microsoft Azure DevOps, ServiceNow ITSM (Tokyo release), and Tableau Server 2023.3. Data was collected through 214 structured interviews, system telemetry exports, and a 47-question survey distributed to 1,840 employees across six regional offices.

Overall platform utilization stands at 61.4% of licensed capacity, representing a significant underperformance against the industry benchmark of 78% reported by Gartner's 2024 Software Optimization Index. Annual licensing expenditure across the four platforms totals $4.7 million, meaning approximately $1.8 million in licensed capability is currently unused or underutilized.

SECTION 2: ACTIVE USE CASES BY PLATFORM

2.1 Salesforce Sales Cloud

The platform supports 11 documented use cases across Meridian's commercial banking and wealth management divisions. The three highest-adoption use cases are:

- Opportunity Pipeline Management: Used by 389 of 431 active users (90.3% adoption among active users). Average deal cycle tracked is 47 days, down from 63 days in 2022 following CRM standardization.
- Contact and Account Management: 412 active users maintain account records; data quality score averages 74/100 based on Salesforce's built-in Einstein Data Quality module.
- Activity Logging and Call Reporting: Compliance-mandated for all client-facing staff. Completion rate is 81.2%, below the 95% target set by the Chief Compliance Officer in January 2024.

Underutilized features include Salesforce Flow automation (adopted by only 12% of eligible users), Einstein Lead Scoring (licensed but not configured), and the Revenue Intelligence module, which has been active since March 2024 but accessed by fewer than 30 users.

2.2 Microsoft Azure DevOps

Azure DevOps supports the Technology and Digital Products division, primarily for sprint planning, CI/CD pipeline management, and repository hosting. Key findings:

- 14 active development teams use Azure Boards for sprint tracking, averaging 22 work items per sprint per team.
- Pipeline automation covers 68% of deployment workflows; the remaining 32% still rely on manual deployment scripts maintained by individual developers.
- The Artifacts module, which enables internal package management, is used by only 3 of 14 teams despite being included in all 310 licenses.
- Average build time across monitored pipelines is 11.4 minutes, compared to a 7-minute target established in the 2024 DevOps Maturity Roadmap.

2.3 ServiceNow ITSM (Tokyo Release)

ServiceNow was upgraded from the San Diego release to Tokyo in October 2023. The platform manages IT service requests, incident management, and change advisory board (CAB) workflows. Current metrics:

- Mean Time to Resolution (MTTR) for Priority 2 incidents: 4.2 hours, against an SLA target of 3.5 hours.
- Change success rate: 91.7%, above the industry average of 87% cited in the HDI 2024 Support Center Practices Report.
- The HR Service Delivery module was purchased in January 2024 at an additional cost of $210,000 annually but has not been deployed. The HR department has cited integration complexity with Workday HCM as the primary blocker.
- Knowledge Management articles: 1,247 published, but only 38% have been accessed in the past 90 days, suggesting significant content staleness.

2.4 Tableau Server 2023.3

Tableau is the primary business intelligence platform, supporting 22 active dashboard workbooks across Finance, Risk, and Operations.

- The Finance division accounts for 61% of all Tableau usage by session count.
- Average dashboard load time is 8.3 seconds, exceeding Tableau's recommended threshold of 5 seconds for optimal user experience.
- 94 published data sources exist on the server; 31 (32.9%) have not been refreshed in more than 60 days.
- Tableau Prep Builder is licensed for all 430 seats but is actively used by only 19 analysts, representing a 4.4% adoption rate.

3.1 Critical Gap Detail: ServiceNow HR Service Delivery

The failure to deploy the HR Service Delivery module represents the single largest dollar-value gap identified in this assessment. At $210,000 in annual licensing cost with zero return, and with the HR team processing an estimated 340 service requests per month manually, the labor cost of manual processing is estimated at $127,000 annually based on average HR coordinator compensation of $58,000 per year and time-allocation analysis. The combined cost of the unused license plus manual processing overhead totals approximately $337,000 per year.

The integration blocker with Workday HCM is addressable through ServiceNow's pre-built Workday spoke, available in the Integration Hub. This was not identified during the October 2023 upgrade project, representing a project management oversight.

3.2 Revenue Opportunity: Salesforce Automation

Configuring Salesforce Flow for automated lead routing and enabling Einstein Lead Scoring is projected to reduce average lead response time from 6.2 hours to under 30 minutes, based on comparable implementations at two peer institutions (First Horizon Bank in 2023 and Glacier Bancorp in Q1 2024). Industry data from Salesforce's 2024 State of Sales Report indicates that leads contacted within 5 minutes are 21 times more likely to convert than those contacted after 30 minutes.

SECTION 4: RECOMMENDATIONS AND PRIORITIZATION

Priority 1 (Complete by December 31, 2024):
- Deploy ServiceNow HR Service Delivery module using Integration Hub Workday spoke. Estimated implementation effort: 6 weeks, $45,000 in professional services.
- Configure Salesforce Einstein Lead Scoring and automated routing workflows. Estimated effort: 3 weeks, internal resource only.
- Optimize Tableau data source extracts and implement extract acceleration to reduce dashboard load times below 5 seconds.

Priority 2 (Complete by March 31, 2025):
- Develop Azure Artifacts adoption program across all 14 development teams, targeting 80% adoption by Q1 2025.
- Launch ServiceNow Knowledge Management audit to retire or update the 770 articles not accessed in 90 days.
- Conduct Tableau Prep training program for the 60 analysts currently performing manual data preparation.

Priority 3 (Evaluate in Q2 2025):
- Assess whether 140 dormant Salesforce seats should be retained pending the November 2024 onboarding or renegotiated with Salesforce for a temporary reduction.
- Review Azure DevOps seat count against actual team headcount; 132 seats (42.6%) are unassigned.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

SECTION 5: CONCLUSION

Meridian Financial Services is spending $4.7 million annually on four enterprise platforms while realizing value from only 61.4% of licensed capacity. The gaps identified in this report are not primarily technology failures but adoption, configuration, and project execution failures. The highest-priority remediation actions carry a combined implementation cost of approximately $45,000 in external services and are projected to recover $337,000 in wasted expenditure within 12 months, representing a 7.5x return on remediation investment.
