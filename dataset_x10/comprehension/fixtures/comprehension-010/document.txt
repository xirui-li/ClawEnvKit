SOFTWARE PLATFORM ANALYSIS REPORT
Use Cases and Gap Assessment
Prepared by: Enterprise Technology Advisory Group
Date: September 12, 2024
Version: 2.3 | Classification: Internal Use Only

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This report evaluates the current software platform landscape deployed across Meridian Financial Services, a mid-sized wealth management firm headquartered in Charlotte, North Carolina, with approximately 1,340 employees across 14 regional offices. The assessment was conducted between June 3 and August 29, 2024, covering six core platforms currently licensed by the organization. The goal is to identify active use cases, measure adoption rates, and document capability gaps that are creating operational friction or compliance risk.

The analysis found that 3 of the 6 platforms are operating below 60% of their licensed capacity, representing an estimated $2.1 million in annual waste. Additionally, 11 distinct workflow gaps were identified that are currently being addressed through manual workarounds, shadow IT tools, or disconnected spreadsheet processes.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

SECTION 1: PLATFORMS UNDER REVIEW

The following six platforms were included in the scope of this assessment:

1. Salesforce Financial Services Cloud (FSC) — CRM and client lifecycle management
2. Microsoft 365 E5 — Productivity, collaboration, and security suite
3. Addepar — Portfolio reporting and performance analytics
4. Orion Advisor Tech — Portfolio management and billing
5. Riskalyze (now Nitrogen) — Risk tolerance assessment and proposal generation
6. DocuSign CLM — Contract lifecycle management

Data was gathered through 47 structured interviews with department heads and end users, system usage logs pulled from IT administration consoles, and a 22-question survey distributed to 312 employees, of which 241 responded (a 77.2% response rate).

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

SECTION 2: USE CASE INVENTORY BY PLATFORM

SALESFORCE FINANCIAL SERVICES CLOUD

Active Use Cases:
- Client onboarding workflow (used by 89% of advisors)
- Opportunity tracking for new AUM (assets under management)
- Household relationship mapping
- Activity logging for compliance documentation

Inactive or Underutilized Features:
- Einstein Analytics dashboards: activated but used by fewer than 12 employees
- Referral tracking module: configured in 2022 but never adopted
- Automated email journeys: 0% utilization as of August 2024

Current license count: 210 seats at $150 per user per month = $378,000 annually
Estimated active users: 174 (82.9% seat utilization)

MICROSOFT 365 E5

Active Use Cases:
- Email and calendar (Outlook): 98% utilization
- Teams for internal communication: 91% utilization
- SharePoint for document storage: 67% utilization
- Microsoft Defender for endpoint security: fully deployed

Inactive or Underutilized Features:
- Microsoft Purview compliance tools: less than 5% of compliance team using available features
- Power BI Pro (included in E5): only 18 licenses actively used out of 1,340
- Microsoft Viva Insights: purchased as part of E5 bundle, zero active sessions recorded since deployment in January 2024

License cost: $57 per user per month × 1,340 users = $916,560 annually

ADDEPAR

Active Use Cases:
- Quarterly performance reporting for 2,800+ client accounts
- Multi-custodian data aggregation (currently pulling from 9 custodians including Schwab, Fidelity, and Pershing)
- Custom benchmark comparisons

Inactive or Underutilized Features:
- Addepar Marketplace integrations: only 2 of 14 available integrations enabled
- Client portal (Addepar View): offered to clients but only 312 of 2,800 accounts (11.1%) have activated access

License cost: $180,000 annually (enterprise flat-rate agreement signed March 2023)

ORION ADVISOR TECH

Active Use Cases:
- Billing and fee calculation for all managed accounts
- Rebalancing workflows for 6 model portfolios
- Performance reporting (partially redundant with Addepar)

Inactive or Underutilized Features:
- Orion Risk Intelligence: licensed but not configured
- Client portal: not deployed, creating duplication with Addepar View gap noted above

License cost: $96,000 annually

RISKALYZE / NITROGEN

Active Use Cases:
- Risk number generation for new client proposals
- Stress testing for existing portfolios during annual reviews

Inactive or Underutilized Features:
- Autopilot model marketplace: not utilized
- Check-In feature for ongoing client engagement: 3% adoption rate among advisors

License cost: $42,000 annually (firm-wide agreement)

DOCUSIGN CLM

Active Use Cases:
- New account agreement execution
- Advisory agreement renewals

Inactive or Underutilized Features:
- Contract repository and search: documents are being stored in SharePoint instead, creating a duplicate repository
- Workflow automation for multi-party approvals: not configured despite being a core CLM feature
- Obligation tracking: 0% utilization

License cost: $64,800 annually (108 seats at $600 per seat per year)

SECTION 3: GAP ASSESSMENT

The following 11 gaps were identified as high-priority based on frequency of mention in interviews, estimated time cost, and compliance risk rating (scored 1–5 by the advisory team).

GAP TABLE

Gap ID | Description | Platforms Affected | Compliance Risk | Est. Annual Hours Lost
G-01 | No automated client onboarding status notifications | Salesforce FSC | 3 | 1,200
G-02 | Duplicate performance reporting in Addepar and Orion | Addepar, Orion | 2 | 840
G-03 | Manual fee reconciliation between Orion billing and custodian statements | Orion | 4 | 2,100
G-04 | No integrated risk-to-proposal workflow (Nitrogen to Salesforce) | Nitrogen, Salesforce | 3 | 680
G-05 | Contract obligations not tracked post-signature | DocuSign CLM | 5 | 960
G-06 | Client portal fragmentation (Addepar View vs. Orion portal) | Addepar, Orion | 2 | 420
G-07 | Power BI underutilized for advisor performance dashboards | Microsoft 365 | 1 | 1,800
G-08 | No centralized document version control for compliance filings | SharePoint, DocuSign | 5 | 1,440
G-09 | Referral source attribution not tracked in CRM | Salesforce FSC | 2 | 560
G-10 | Nitrogen risk assessments not archived in Salesforce client records | Nitrogen, Salesforce | 4 | 730
G-11 | Microsoft Purview not configured for SEC recordkeeping requirements | Microsoft 365 | 5 | N/A (regulatory exposure)

Total estimated annual hours lost to manual workarounds: 10,730 hours
At an average fully-loaded cost of $68 per hour for affected staff, this represents approximately $729,640 in annual productivity loss.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

SECTION 4: SHADOW IT AND WORKAROUND INVENTORY

During interviews, 34 employees across 8 departments disclosed use of tools not officially sanctioned by IT. The most common shadow tools identified were:

- Google Sheets: used by 19 advisors for client tracking outside Salesforce
- Dropbox: used by the compliance team for sharing documents with external auditors
- Calendly: used by 11 advisors for client scheduling, not integrated with Outlook
- ChatGPT (free tier): used by 7 employees for drafting client communications, raising data privacy concerns flagged to the CISO on August 14, 2024

These tools represent both security risk and data integrity risk, particularly given Meridian's obligations under SEC Regulation S-P and the firm's upcoming FINRA examination scheduled for Q1 2025.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

SECTION 5: RECOMMENDATIONS AND PRIORITY RANKING

Based on the gap analysis and cost modeling, the advisory team recommends the following actions, sequenced by impact and implementation complexity:

IMMEDIATE ACTIONS (Q4 2024)
- Configure Microsoft Purview for SEC recordkeeping (addresses G-08 and G-11; estimated implementation cost: $18,000 in consulting fees)
- Establish DocuSign CLM obligation tracking workflow (addresses G-05; estimated 40 hours of internal configuration)
- Archive Nitrogen risk assessments to Salesforce via existing API connector (addresses G-10; no additional licensing required)

SHORT-TERM ACTIONS (Q1–Q2 2025)
- Consolidate client portal strategy: decommission Orion portal and drive adoption of Addepar View to 40% of accounts by June 30, 2025 (addresses G-06)
- Deploy Power BI dashboards for advisor performance reporting using existing E5 licenses (addresses G-07; estimated 120 hours of development)
- Implement Salesforce-to-Nitrogen integration using MuleSoft connector (addresses G-04; estimated cost: $22,500)

LONGER-TERM ACTIONS (H2 2025)
- Evaluate whether Orion and Addepar reporting functions can be consolidated to eliminate redundancy (addresses G-02; potential savings of $48,000–$96,000 annually depending on outcome)
- Conduct formal RFP for automated fee reconciliation solution to address G-03

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

SECTION 6: CONCLUSION

Meridian Financial Services is carrying significant unused platform capacity while simultaneously absorbing productivity losses from manual workarounds. The six platforms reviewed represent a combined annual spend of approximately $1,696,560. Of this, an estimated $2.1 million in total value (including productivity loss and unused license capacity) is not being realized.

The highest-urgency items are those with compliance risk scores of 4 or 5, particularly G-03, G-05, G-08, G-10, and G-11, given the firm's FINRA examination timeline and ongoing SEC recordkeeping obligations. Addressing these five gaps alone is projected to reduce regulatory exposure materially and recover approximately 5,230 staff hours annually.

A follow-up review is recommended for March 2025 to assess progress against the recommendations in this report.
