EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This report presents comprehensive commodity price statistics for the United States fiscal year ending September 30, 2023. Data has been compiled from the CFTC's market surveillance division, the BLS Producer Price Index program, the U.S. Energy Information Administration (EIA), and the U.S. Department of Agriculture (USDA) National Agricultural Statistics Service. The findings reflect significant volatility across energy, agricultural, and metals markets during the reporting period, with overall commodity price indices declining 8.3 percent from fiscal year 2022 levels following the historic highs recorded in the aftermath of the Russian invasion of Ukraine.

SECTION 1: ENERGY COMMODITIES

1.1 Crude Oil

West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude oil averaged $77.58 per barrel during fiscal year 2023, representing a decline of 17.4 percent compared to the fiscal year 2022 average of $93.92 per barrel. The annual high of $93.68 per barrel was recorded on September 27, 2023, driven by OPEC+ production cuts announced by Saudi Arabia and Russia totaling 1.3 million barrels per day. The annual low of $66.74 per barrel was recorded on March 17, 2023, amid banking sector instability following the collapse of Silicon Valley Bank.

Brent crude oil, the international benchmark, averaged $82.14 per barrel for the fiscal year, down 15.9 percent from the prior year average of $97.65 per barrel.

U.S. crude oil production reached a record 12.9 million barrels per day in October 2023, surpassing the previous record of 12.8 million barrels per day set in November 2019.

1.2 Natural Gas

Henry Hub natural gas spot prices averaged $2.57 per million British thermal units (MMBtu) during fiscal year 2023, a dramatic decline of 58.6 percent from the fiscal year 2022 average of $6.21 per MMBtu. This decline was primarily attributable to above-average storage inventories, mild winter temperatures across the continental United States, and reduced export demand from European markets as liquefied natural gas (LNG) infrastructure constraints limited throughput capacity.

The annual high of $3.47 per MMBtu was recorded on October 5, 2022. The annual low of $1.97 per MMBtu was recorded on April 14, 2023.

1.3 Refined Products

Retail gasoline prices averaged $3.53 per gallon nationally during fiscal year 2023, compared to $4.14 per gallon in fiscal year 2022, a decrease of 14.7 percent. The national average peaked at $3.98 per gallon on September 18, 2023. Diesel fuel averaged $4.01 per gallon for the fiscal year, down 18.3 percent from the prior year average of $4.91 per gallon.

Wheat prices experienced the most significant decline among major grains, falling 30.5 percent as global supply concerns eased following record harvests in Australia and Argentina. The Black Sea Grain Initiative, which permitted Ukrainian grain exports through July 17, 2023, contributed to price moderation during the first three quarters of the fiscal year. Following Russia's withdrawal from the agreement on July 17, 2023, wheat futures rose 8.2 percent over the subsequent two-week trading period.

U.S. corn production for the 2023 crop year was estimated at 15.342 billion bushels by the USDA, up 10.1 percent from the 2022 crop of 13.930 billion bushels.

2.2 Livestock and Meat

Live cattle futures averaged $172.84 per hundredweight (cwt) during fiscal year 2023, an increase of 14.6 percent from the fiscal year 2022 average of $150.82 per cwt. This represented the highest annual average price on record for live cattle futures. The record high of $188.25 per cwt was reached on August 4, 2023, driven by tight cattle supplies resulting from prolonged drought conditions in the Southern Plains.

Lean hog futures averaged $82.47 per cwt, down 11.3 percent from the prior year average of $92.97 per cwt, reflecting improved domestic pork supplies and reduced export demand from China.

2.3 Soft Commodities

Coffee (Arabica) futures averaged $1.627 per pound during fiscal year 2023, down 22.4 percent from the fiscal year 2022 average of $2.096 per pound, as Brazilian production recovered from the 2021 frost damage. Cocoa futures averaged $2,987 per metric ton, up 28.6 percent from the prior year average of $2,323 per metric ton, driven by adverse weather conditions in West Africa, particularly in Ghana and Côte d'Ivoire, which together account for approximately 60 percent of global cocoa production. Cotton futures averaged $0.832 per pound, down 24.1 percent from the prior year.

SECTION 3: METALS AND MINERALS

3.1 Precious Metals

Gold futures on the COMEX division of the New York Mercantile Exchange averaged $1,941.20 per troy ounce during fiscal year 2023, an increase of 5.8 percent from the fiscal year 2022 average of $1,835.40 per troy ounce. Gold reached an all-time intraday high of $2,081.90 per troy ounce on May 4, 2023, before retreating as Federal Reserve officials signaled continued monetary tightening. The annual low of $1,628.30 per troy ounce was recorded on October 28, 2022.

Silver futures averaged $23.14 per troy ounce, up 2.1 percent from the prior year average of $22.67 per troy ounce. The gold-to-silver ratio averaged 83.8 during the fiscal year, compared to 81.0 in fiscal year 2022.

Nickel experienced the most dramatic decline among base metals, falling 33.8 percent as Indonesian production surged and the London Metal Exchange continued to manage market disruptions stemming from the March 2022 short squeeze that had temporarily pushed prices above $100,000 per metric ton. Aluminum declined 27.2 percent as European smelters resumed production following the easing of energy price pressures.

Copper, often cited as a leading economic indicator, averaged $3.85 per pound, down 9.8 percent from fiscal year 2022. Analysts at the CFTC's Office of the Chief Economist noted that copper's relative resilience compared to other base metals reflected sustained demand from electric vehicle manufacturing and renewable energy infrastructure investment.

SECTION 4: PRODUCER PRICE INDEX — COMMODITY COMPONENTS

The BLS Producer Price Index for All Commodities (Series WPU00000000) declined 4.6 percent on a year-over-year basis as of September 2023, following an increase of 14.3 percent recorded in September 2022. The energy component of the PPI declined 22.7 percent year-over-year, while the food component declined 3.1 percent. The industrial commodities component, excluding food and energy, declined 1.8 percent.

The PPI for crude goods — the most upstream measure of commodity prices — declined 12.4 percent year-over-year in September 2023, compared to an increase of 22.1 percent in September 2022.

SECTION 5: MARKET PARTICIPATION AND OPEN INTEREST

Total open interest across all CFTC-regulated commodity futures markets averaged 28.4 million contracts during fiscal year 2023, down 6.2 percent from the fiscal year 2022 average of 30.3 million contracts. Energy markets accounted for 38.7 percent of total open interest, agricultural markets accounted for 29.4 percent, and financial commodity markets (including metals) accounted for the remaining 31.9 percent.

Managed money net long positions in WTI crude oil futures declined from a peak of 412,000 contracts in January 2023 to a low of 98,000 contracts in May 2023, reflecting shifting sentiment among speculative traders regarding global demand prospects.

SECTION 6: OUTLOOK AND RISK FACTORS

The CFTC's Office of the Chief Economist, led by Dr. Andrew Busch, identifies the following primary risk factors for commodity markets in fiscal year 2024:

- Continued OPEC+ production management, with the current voluntary cut agreement extending through December 2024
- El Niño weather pattern impacts on agricultural production in South America and Southeast Asia, with NOAA forecasting a 71 percent probability of El Niño conditions persisting through the first quarter of 2024
- Federal Reserve monetary policy trajectory and its impact on the U.S. dollar, which maintains a historically significant negative correlation of approximately -0.62 with the Bloomberg Commodity Index
- Geopolitical risks in the Middle East following the October 7, 2023 Hamas attack on Israel and subsequent regional escalation
- Chinese economic recovery pace and its implications for base metal and energy demand

DATA SOURCES AND METHODOLOGY

Price data for energy commodities was sourced from the U.S. Energy Information Administration's Short-Term Energy Outlook database and NYMEX settlement prices as reported by CME Group. Agricultural commodity prices were sourced from CBOT settlement data and USDA Agricultural Marketing Service cash price reports. Metal prices were sourced from London Metal Exchange official settlement prices and COMEX settlement data. Producer Price Index data was sourced from the Bureau of Labor Statistics at bls.gov. All annual averages represent arithmetic means of daily settlement prices for the period October 1, 2022 through September 30, 2023, unless otherwise noted.
