EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The Commonwealth of Pennsylvania's General Fund revenues for the second quarter of Fiscal Year 2023-2024 (October 1 through December 31, 2023) totaled $10.84 billion, representing a decrease of 3.2 percent compared to the first quarter of the same fiscal year, which recorded $11.20 billion. Year-over-year, second quarter revenues declined by $312 million, or 2.8 percent, compared to the second quarter of Fiscal Year 2022-2023.

Total General Fund expenditures for Q2 FY2023-2024 reached $11.67 billion, an increase of $890 million, or 8.3 percent, over Q1 FY2023-2024 expenditures of $10.78 billion. The resulting quarterly deficit of $830 million was offset by the Commonwealth's General Fund balance carried forward from the prior fiscal year, which stood at $3.14 billion as of July 1, 2023.

Secretary of the Budget Uri Monson noted that the quarter-over-quarter revenue decline reflects anticipated seasonal patterns in personal income tax withholding and corporate net income tax collections, and does not indicate structural deterioration in the Commonwealth's fiscal position.

Key Revenue Observations:

Personal Income Tax (PIT): The $320.6 million quarter-over-quarter decline in PIT collections is consistent with historical seasonal patterns. Q1 typically benefits from elevated quarterly estimated payments due September 15, while Q2 reflects standard payroll withholding without a major estimated payment deadline. Withholding collections alone totaled $3.87 billion in Q2, down from $3.94 billion in Q1, a modest decline of 1.8 percent. The larger drop occurred in estimated and final payments, which fell from $872.3 million in Q1 to $621.7 million in Q2.

Sales and Use Tax (SUT): The 7.6 percent quarter-over-quarter increase in SUT collections reflects strong holiday retail activity in November and December 2023. December 2023 alone generated $1.24 billion in SUT receipts, the highest single-month SUT collection since December 2021. Online marketplace facilitator collections, which became fully enforceable under Act 13 of 2019, contributed $387.4 million in Q2, up from $341.2 million in Q1.

Corporate Net Income Tax (CNIT): The 15.5 percent decline in CNIT collections represents the most significant quarter-over-quarter drop among major revenue categories. The Q1 figure was elevated by $198.4 million in large corporate settlements finalized in September 2023. Excluding these one-time items, the adjusted Q1 CNIT total would have been $1,445.8 million, making the Q2 decline a more modest 3.9 percent on a normalized basis.

Key Expenditure Observations:

Medical Assistance: The $478.7 million quarter-over-quarter increase in Medicaid spending is the largest absolute increase among all expenditure categories. This reflects the conclusion of the federal continuous enrollment requirement under the Families First Coronavirus Response Act, which ended Pennsylvania's Medicaid redetermination pause. The Department of Human Services processed 487,000 eligibility redeterminations during Q2, resulting in approximately 94,000 individuals transitioning off the program. However, increased utilization among remaining enrollees and a 4.2 percent rate increase for managed care organizations effective October 1, 2023, drove overall spending higher.

Education: The $244.8 million increase in PreK-12 education spending reflects the disbursement of the second quarterly installment of the Basic Education Funding subsidy to the Commonwealth's 500 school districts. The Q2 disbursement included a supplemental allocation of $127.3 million pursuant to the Governor's Executive Order 2023-14, signed October 12, 2023, directing accelerated distribution of funds to the 100 lowest-wealth districts as identified by the Basic Education Funding Commission.

Debt Service: The 25.8 percent quarter-over-quarter increase in debt service payments reflects the November 1 and December 1 semi-annual interest payment dates on the Commonwealth's outstanding general obligation bonds. Total outstanding GO bond debt as of December 31, 2023, stood at $11.43 billion, with a weighted average interest rate of 3.87 percent.

SECTION III: FUND BALANCE AND LIQUIDITY POSITION

The Commonwealth's General Fund unappropriated balance as of December 31, 2023, was $2.31 billion, compared to $3.14 billion at the start of the fiscal year on July 1, 2023. The $830 million reduction reflects the net of Q1 and Q2 combined operations.

The Budget Stabilization Reserve Fund (Rainy Day Fund) remained unchanged at $1.83 billion as of December 31, 2023. No transfers into or out of the Rainy Day Fund occurred during Q2. The combined General Fund balance and Rainy Day Fund total of $4.14 billion represents 9.1 percent of annualized General Fund expenditures, which remains within the range recommended by the Government Finance Officers Association of 8 to 12 percent.

Short-term borrowing: The Commonwealth did not issue Tax Anticipation Notes (TANs) during Q2 FY2023-2024, consistent with the prior year. Cash flow projections prepared by the Treasury Department under Treasurer Stacy Garrity indicate sufficient liquidity through March 31, 2024, without recourse to short-term borrowing.

SECTION IV: SPECIAL FUNDS OVERVIEW

Motor License Fund: Q2 revenues totaled $1.12 billion, up 2.1 percent from Q1's $1.097 billion. Liquid fuels tax collections of $487.3 million were essentially flat quarter-over-quarter (+0.4%). Vehicle registration fees generated $312.8 million in Q2.

Lottery Fund: Net revenues available for senior programs totaled $284.6 million in Q2, a decrease of $18.3 million, or 6.0 percent, from Q1's $302.9 million. iLottery sales of $94.7 million in Q2 represented a 12.4 percent increase over Q1's $84.2 million, partially offsetting declines in traditional instant ticket sales.

SECTION V: OUTLOOK FOR Q3 FY2023-2024

Q3 (January 1 through March 31, 2024) revenue projections are $10.62 billion, reflecting the March 15 corporate estimated payment deadline and the absence of a major personal income tax deadline until April 15. Expenditures are projected at $11.41 billion, driven by the third quarterly Basic Education Funding disbursement and continued Medicaid spending pressure.

The Independent Fiscal Office, in its December 2023 Revenue Estimate Update, projected full-year FY2023-2024 General Fund revenues of $43.1 billion, a reduction of $780 million from the enacted budget estimate of $43.88 billion. The Office of the Budget concurs with this revised projection and is working with agency secretaries to identify $400 million in expenditure management actions to reduce the projected year-end structural gap.
