EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The State of Minnesota collected $31.4 billion in total tax revenue during Fiscal Year 2023 (July 1, 2022 through June 30, 2023), representing a 3.2 percent increase over the $30.4 billion collected in Fiscal Year 2022. This growth, while positive, fell below the 5.7 percent growth rate projected in the February 2022 forecast and reflects broader national trends of moderating consumer spending and a cooling labor market in the second half of calendar year 2022. This report provides a comprehensive breakdown of revenue by tax category, year-over-year comparisons, notable variances from projections, and forward-looking observations for the FY2024 budget cycle.

---

SECTION 1: TOTAL REVENUE OVERVIEW

Minnesota's $31.4 billion in FY2023 tax revenue was distributed across five primary categories. The individual income tax remained the dominant source, contributing $17.1 billion, or 54.5 percent of total collections. The sales and use tax generated $8.6 billion, representing 27.4 percent of total revenue. Corporate franchise taxes contributed $2.9 billion, or 9.2 percent. The remaining 8.9 percent was split between property-related taxes ($1.6 billion) and miscellaneous taxes and fees ($1.2 billion), which include motor vehicle excise taxes, estate taxes, deed transfer taxes, and tobacco and alcohol levies.

Compared to FY2022, the most significant growth occurred in corporate franchise tax collections, which rose 11.4 percent from $2.6 billion. Individual income tax collections grew by 2.8 percent from $16.6 billion, while sales tax revenue increased by 1.9 percent from $8.44 billion. Property-related taxes declined by 0.6 percent, the only category to post a year-over-year decrease.

---

SECTION 2: INDIVIDUAL INCOME TAX ANALYSIS

Individual income tax collections of $17.1 billion in FY2023 were driven primarily by strong wage growth in the first three quarters of the fiscal year. Withholding payments, which account for approximately 78 percent of individual income tax receipts, totaled $13.3 billion, up 3.1 percent from $12.9 billion in FY2022.

Estimated payments from self-employed individuals and investors totaled $2.4 billion, a decrease of 4.8 percent from $2.52 billion in FY2022. This decline is attributed to reduced capital gains realizations as equity markets contracted during calendar year 2022. The S&P 500 declined 19.4 percent in calendar year 2022, and Minnesota taxpayers reported approximately $4.1 billion in net capital gains on FY2023 returns, down from $6.3 billion the prior year.

Final payments and refunds netted to $1.4 billion in additional collections. The Department issued $2.87 billion in individual income tax refunds during FY2023, compared to $2.61 billion in FY2022, an increase of 10 percent driven in part by legislative changes enacted under the 2023 Tax Bill (HF 1938) that expanded the Child Tax Credit and increased the Working Family Credit.

Minnesota's top individual income tax rate of 9.85 percent, applicable to taxable income above $304,970 for married joint filers, generated disproportionate revenue from high-income households. Taxpayers with adjusted gross income above $500,000 represented 1.3 percent of all filers but contributed 28.7 percent of total individual income tax revenue, or approximately $4.9 billion.

---

SECTION 3: SALES AND USE TAX ANALYSIS

Sales and use tax collections totaled $8.6 billion in FY2023, reflecting modest growth of 1.9 percent over FY2022. The statewide general sales tax rate of 6.875 percent remained unchanged throughout the fiscal year. Collections were supported by continued consumer spending on services and durable goods in the first half of the fiscal year but softened notably in the January through June 2023 period.

Monthly collections peaked in August 2022 at $812 million, reflecting strong summer retail activity and back-to-school spending. The lowest monthly total was recorded in February 2023 at $618 million. The average monthly collection for FY2023 was $716.7 million.

Remote seller collections, governed by the state's economic nexus law implemented following the 2018 South Dakota v. Wayfair Supreme Court decision, contributed $1.14 billion in FY2023, up 18.3 percent from $963 million in FY2022. Remote seller revenue now represents 13.3 percent of total sales tax collections, compared to 11.4 percent in FY2022 and just 2.1 percent in FY2019.

By sector, general merchandise and food stores accounted for $2.1 billion in sales tax collections. Motor vehicle sales generated $1.3 billion. Restaurants and food service contributed $890 million. Construction materials and contractors paid $760 million. The remaining $3.55 billion came from a broad range of retail, service, and digital goods categories.

---

SECTION 4: CORPORATE FRANCHISE TAX ANALYSIS

Corporate franchise tax collections of $2.9 billion represented the strongest growth of any major category at 11.4 percent year-over-year. Minnesota's corporate tax rate of 9.8 percent, one of the highest in the nation, applied to corporate taxable income apportioned to Minnesota using a single-sales-factor formula.

The increase in collections was driven by strong corporate profitability in calendar year 2021 and early 2022, which flowed into FY2023 estimated and final payments. The top 50 corporate taxpayers by Minnesota liability contributed an estimated $1.1 billion, representing 37.9 percent of total corporate collections. Industries with the largest contributions included financial services ($620 million), manufacturing ($540 million), retail trade ($390 million), and health care ($310 million).

Corporate estimated payments totaled $2.3 billion, while final payments net of refunds added $600 million. Corporate refunds issued during FY2023 totaled $487 million, up from $341 million in FY2022, as several large multistate corporations successfully litigated apportionment disputes resulting in refund awards.

---

SECTION 5: MISCELLANEOUS TAXES AND FEES

The $1.2 billion in miscellaneous tax collections included the following components:

Motor Vehicle Excise Tax: $487 million (collected at 6.5 percent of vehicle purchase price)
Tobacco Products Tax: $214 million (cigarettes taxed at $3.04 per pack; other tobacco at 95 percent of wholesale price)
Alcohol Excise Tax: $98 million
Estate and Gift Tax: $176 million (applicable to estates exceeding $3 million)
Deed Transfer Tax: $143 million
Other Fees and Assessments: $82 million

The estate tax generated $176 million, a decrease of 22.1 percent from $226 million in FY2022, reflecting fewer large estates filed during the period and a decline in asset valuations affecting taxable estate sizes.

---

SECTION 6: VARIANCE FROM FORECAST

The February 2022 forecast projected total FY2023 tax revenue of $32.6 billion. Actual collections of $31.4 billion fell short by $1.2 billion, or 3.7 percent below forecast. The largest variance occurred in individual income tax, which came in $780 million below the $17.88 billion projection, primarily due to lower-than-anticipated capital gains realizations. Sales tax collections were $340 million below the projected $8.94 billion. Corporate franchise tax exceeded its $2.75 billion projection by $150 million.

The November 2022 revised forecast, which incorporated updated economic data, projected $31.7 billion in total collections. Actual FY2023 revenue came in $300 million, or 0.9 percent, below this revised estimate, indicating that the Department's mid-year adjustments largely captured the economic slowdown.

---

SECTION 7: FORWARD OUTLOOK

The February 2024 forecast projects FY2024 total tax revenue of $32.1 billion, representing growth of 2.2 percent over FY2023 actuals. Individual income tax is projected to reach $17.6 billion, supported by continued wage growth and a partial recovery in capital gains activity. Sales tax is projected at $8.85 billion. Corporate franchise tax is expected to moderate to $2.7 billion as corporate profit margins compress under higher interest rates.

Key risks to the FY2024 forecast include potential federal policy changes affecting the state tax base, continued uncertainty in commercial real estate valuations affecting property-related revenues, and the impact of the expanded Child Tax Credit enacted in 2023 on net individual income tax collections.

---

SECTION 8: METHODOLOGY NOTES

All figures in this report are presented on a cash basis consistent with the State of Minnesota's accounting standards under Minnesota Statutes Section 16A.011. Revenue is recorded in the fiscal year in which payment is received by the Department of Revenue. Comparative FY2022 figures have been restated in two instances to reflect audit adjustments totaling $43 million identified during the FY2023 close process. All percentage calculations are based on restated FY2022 figures. This report was reviewed by the Legislative Budget Office and the Office of the State Auditor prior to publication.
