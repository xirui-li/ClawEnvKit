EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This report presents a comprehensive comparison of departmental appropriations and actual expenditures for the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania across fiscal year 2023–2024 (July 1, 2023 through June 30, 2024) and fiscal year 2022–2023 (July 1, 2022 through June 30, 2023). The Commonwealth's total enacted general fund budget for FY 2023–2024 was $47.6 billion, representing an increase of $2.1 billion, or 4.6 percent, over the FY 2022–2023 enacted budget of $45.5 billion. Actual general fund expenditures for FY 2023–2024 totaled $46.8 billion, leaving an unspent balance of approximately $812 million across all departments.

This document is intended for use by the General Assembly, the Governor's Office, department secretaries, and the public in evaluating departmental fiscal performance, identifying areas of over- or under-expenditure, and informing future budget planning cycles.

SECTION 1: METHODOLOGY AND DATA SOURCES

All figures in this report are drawn from the Commonwealth's Integrated Enterprise System (IES), the official financial management platform administered by the Office of the Comptroller Operations. Data was extracted on September 30, 2024, following the close of all FY 2023–2024 fiscal year-end transactions. Appropriation figures reflect enacted budgets as signed by Governor Josh Shapiro on July 7, 2023, for FY 2023–2024, and as signed by Governor Tom Wolf on June 30, 2022, for FY 2022–2023.

Expenditure figures include all obligations liquidated within the fiscal year. Encumbrances carried forward are noted separately where applicable. Federal fund expenditures and special fund expenditures are excluded from the general fund comparisons in Section 2 but are addressed in Section 4.

SECTION 3: NOTABLE FINDINGS AND ANALYSIS

3.1 Department of Education — Largest Proportional Increase Among Major Departments

The Department of Education recorded the largest year-over-year percentage increase in actual expenditures among the twelve major departments, rising 6.99 percent from $8,089.3 million in FY 2022–2023 to $8,655.1 million in FY 2023–2024. This increase is attributable primarily to the $567 million increase in the Basic Education Funding subsidy, which was the largest single-year increase in that line item in the Commonwealth's history. The enacted appropriation for Basic Education Funding alone in FY 2023–2024 was $7,073.4 million. The department spent 99.45 percent of its enacted appropriation, the highest utilization rate among all major departments.

3.2 Department of Health — Significant Expansion

The Department of Health experienced an 11.76 percent increase in actual expenditures, the highest percentage growth rate of any major department. Actual spending rose from $987.6 million to $1,103.7 million. This growth reflects the full-year implementation of the Opioid Use Disorder Treatment Expansion Program, which accounted for $68.4 million in new expenditures, as well as a $47.2 million increase in the Medical Assistance — Behavioral Health line item. The department's enacted appropriation of $1,148.3 million was not fully utilized, with actual spending representing 96.1 percent of the appropriation.

3.3 Department of Corrections — Expenditure Exceeds Appropriation

The Department of Corrections is the only major department in which actual FY 2022–2023 expenditures exceeded the enacted appropriation, with actual spending of $3,201.8 million against an enacted budget of $3,187.2 million, a deficit of $14.6 million. This overrun was covered through a supplemental appropriation approved by the General Assembly on March 14, 2023, in the amount of $18.0 million. In FY 2023–2024, the department returned to within-appropriation spending, with actual expenditures of $3,318.4 million against an enacted budget of $3,344.6 million, leaving an unspent balance of $26.2 million. The department attributed the prior year overrun to unanticipated increases in pharmaceutical costs and a 2.3 percent increase in the average daily inmate population during the second half of FY 2022–2023.

3.4 Department of Human Services — Largest Absolute Expenditure

The Department of Human Services remains the Commonwealth's largest spending department by a substantial margin, accounting for 37.4 percent of total general fund actual expenditures in FY 2023–2024. The department's actual spending of $17,488.2 million represents an increase of $786.8 million over the prior fiscal year. The Medical Assistance program, which provides health coverage to approximately 3.4 million Pennsylvanians, constituted $11,214.6 million of the department's total expenditures. The Children and Youth line item increased by $112.3 million, or 8.9 percent, reflecting expanded foster care services and child protective services staffing.

3.5 Pennsylvania State Police — Personnel Cost Pressures

The Pennsylvania State Police spent $1,507.3 million in FY 2023–2024, representing 99.65 percent of its enacted appropriation. Personnel costs, including salaries, benefits, and overtime, accounted for $1,341.2 million, or 88.99 percent of total expenditures. Overtime expenditures alone totaled $87.4 million, an increase of $11.6 million over the prior year, driven by staffing shortages in rural barracks and increased deployment for special events.

SECTION 4: FEDERAL AND SPECIAL FUND OVERVIEW

In addition to general fund expenditures, Commonwealth departments expended $38.2 billion in federal funds during FY 2023–2024, compared to $36.7 billion in FY 2022–2023, an increase of 4.1 percent. The Department of Human Services accounted for $24.6 billion of federal fund expenditures, primarily through Medicaid matching funds received from the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services. Special fund expenditures totaled $14.9 billion in FY 2023–2024, with the Department of Transportation's Motor License Fund representing the largest special fund at $4.3 billion.

SECTION 5: BUDGET UTILIZATION RATES

The following departments achieved the highest and lowest general fund appropriation utilization rates in FY 2023–2024:

Highest Utilization Rates:
- Department of Education: 99.45%
- Pennsylvania State Police: 99.65%
- Department of Human Services: 99.28%

Lowest Utilization Rates:
- Department of Agriculture: 96.79%
- Department of Health: 96.12%
- Department of Environmental Protection: 97.38%

The Commonwealth-wide general fund utilization rate for FY 2023–2024 was 98.32 percent, compared to 98.07 percent in FY 2022–2023.

SECTION 6: RECOMMENDATIONS

The Bureau of Financial Management offers the following recommendations for the FY 2024–2025 budget cycle:

1. The Department of Corrections should submit a revised personnel cost projection by December 1, 2024, incorporating updated pharmaceutical contract pricing and projected inmate population trends to prevent a recurrence of the FY 2022–2023 appropriation overrun.

2. The Department of Health should provide a detailed spending plan for the Opioid Use Disorder Treatment Expansion Program no later than November 15, 2024, given the $44.6 million unspent balance in FY 2023–2024.

3. Departments with utilization rates below 97 percent for two consecutive fiscal years should be required to submit quarterly expenditure projections to the Office of the Budget beginning in the first quarter of FY 2024–2025.
