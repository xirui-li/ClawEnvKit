EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The Commonwealth of Pennsylvania's General Fund revenues for the third quarter of fiscal year 2023-2024 totaled $11.84 billion, representing an increase of $1.27 billion, or 12.0 percent, compared to the second quarter total of $10.57 billion. This quarter-over-quarter improvement reflects stronger-than-anticipated personal income tax collections driven by year-end bonus distributions, improved corporate net income tax receipts, and a seasonal uptick in sales and use tax activity associated with the post-holiday retail period. General Fund expenditures for Q3 reached $10.93 billion, up from $10.41 billion in Q2, an increase of $520 million or 5.0 percent. The resulting quarterly surplus of $910 million compares favorably to the Q2 surplus of $160 million, a quarter-over-quarter improvement of $750 million.

1.2 Personal Income Tax

Personal income tax (PIT) collections of $5.108 billion in Q3 represent the largest single-quarter PIT collection in Pennsylvania history, surpassing the previous record of $4.89 billion set in Q3 of fiscal year 2021-2022. The 18.5 percent quarter-over-quarter increase is attributable primarily to the January 31, 2024 withholding deadline for year-end employer bonuses and supplemental wage payments. Employer withholding alone accounted for $3.76 billion of Q3 PIT collections, up from $3.21 billion in Q2. Estimated payments contributed $892.4 million, reflecting strong fourth-quarter 2023 investment income among high-net-worth filers.

1.3 Sales and Use Tax

Sales and use tax (SUT) collections of $3.045 billion increased 5.3 percent from Q2. January collections of $1.089 billion were the strongest month of the quarter, reflecting December 2023 holiday retail activity remitted in January. February collections of $967.3 million and March collections of $988.4 million were broadly consistent with seasonal norms. Online marketplace facilitator remittances, which Pennsylvania began collecting under Act 43 of 2017, contributed $412.6 million in Q3, up 8.7 percent from $379.6 million in Q2, reflecting continued growth in e-commerce activity.

1.4 Corporate Net Income Tax

Corporate net income tax (CNIT) collections of $1.389 billion increased 15.3 percent from Q2's $1.205 billion. This increase reflects the March 15, 2024 estimated payment deadline for calendar-year S-corporations and partnerships that elected corporate treatment. Pennsylvania's CNIT rate declined from 8.99 percent to 8.49 percent effective January 1, 2024, as part of the phased reduction schedule established under Act 53 of 2022. Despite the rate reduction, collections increased due to higher corporate profitability reported for tax year 2023.

1.5 Declining Revenue Categories

Realty transfer tax collections fell $14.3 million, or 9.9 percent, to $129.4 million in Q3, consistent with the continued softening of Pennsylvania's residential real estate market. The number of recorded deed transfers statewide declined 11.2 percent compared to Q2, according to data from the Pennsylvania Department of State. Cigarette and tobacco tax revenues declined 3.3 percent to $191.7 million, continuing a long-term trend of declining cigarette consumption. Taxable cigarette packs sold in Q3 totaled approximately 47.3 million, down from 48.9 million in Q2.

2.2 Education Expenditures

Education spending of $4.103 billion in Q3 increased $261.4 million, or 6.8 percent, from Q2. This increase reflects the disbursement of the second installment of the Basic Education Funding subsidy to Pennsylvania's 500 school districts, totaling $2.14 billion, which was distributed on February 15, 2024. The Philadelphia School District received the largest single allocation at $312.7 million. Special Education funding disbursements of $441.3 million were also processed in Q3, compared to $389.6 million in Q2.

2.3 Debt Service

Debt service expenditures of $612.7 million in Q3 increased $123.4 million, or 25.2 percent, from Q2. This significant increase reflects the February 1, 2024 semi-annual principal and interest payment on the Commonwealth's General Obligation bonds. As of March 31, 2024, Pennsylvania's total outstanding General Obligation debt stood at $10.84 billion, a decrease of $287.3 million from the June 30, 2023 balance of $11.13 billion, reflecting scheduled principal retirements.

2.4 Medical Assistance

Medical Assistance (Medicaid) expenditures of $2.298 billion increased 3.8 percent from Q2. Pennsylvania's Medicaid enrollment as of March 31, 2024 stood at 3,412,600 individuals, a decrease of 87,400 from the Q2 enrollment of 3,500,000, reflecting the ongoing federal unwinding of continuous enrollment provisions that expired in March 2023. The Department of Human Services processed 214,300 eligibility redeterminations during Q3, of which 31.7 percent resulted in disenrollment.

SECTION 3: FUND BALANCE AND RESERVES

As of March 31, 2024, the General Fund unappropriated balance stood at $3.218 billion, compared to $2.308 billion at December 31, 2023, an increase of $910 million reflecting the Q3 operating surplus. The Budget Stabilization Reserve Fund (Rainy Day Fund) balance remained unchanged at $1.437 billion during Q3, as no transfers were authorized by the General Assembly during the quarter.

Year-to-date General Fund revenues through March 31, 2024 total $32.614 billion against year-to-date expenditures of $31.704 billion, producing a year-to-date surplus of $910 million. The Governor's Executive Budget, submitted February 6, 2024, projects full-year revenues of $43.27 billion and expenditures of $44.42 billion, implying a projected year-end deficit of $1.15 billion that would require appropriation from reserve balances.

SECTION 4: SPECIAL FUNDS SUMMARY

The Motor License Fund received $1.084 billion in Q3, up 3.1 percent from $1.051 billion in Q2, driven by higher liquid fuels tax collections of $487.3 million. The Lottery Fund generated net revenue of $312.6 million in Q3, an increase of $18.4 million from Q2's $294.2 million, with instant ticket sales of $891.4 million representing the strongest quarterly performance since the Pennsylvania Lottery's fiscal year 2021-2022 record. The Unemployment Compensation Fund maintained a positive balance of $2.76 billion as of March 31, 2024, with Q3 benefit payments of $387.4 million against contributions of $441.2 million.

SECTION 5: OUTLOOK FOR FOURTH QUARTER

The fourth quarter (April 1 – June 30, 2024) will be shaped significantly by the April 15, 2024 personal income tax filing deadline. The Independent Fiscal Office's March 2024 revenue estimate projects Q4 PIT collections of $4.87 billion, which would represent a 4.7 percent decline from Q3 as the seasonal bonus withholding effect dissipates. Sales and use tax collections are projected at $3.01 billion for Q4, broadly flat with Q3. Corporate net income tax collections are expected to decline to approximately $890 million in Q4, as the March estimated payment deadline shifts collections forward. The Bureau of Financial Management will publish the Q4 report on or before July 25, 2024.
