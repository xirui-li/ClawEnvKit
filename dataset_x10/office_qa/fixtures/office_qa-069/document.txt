EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This report presents comprehensive commodity price statistics for the United States fiscal year ending September 30, 2023. Data compiled herein reflects pricing trends, volatility indices, and year-over-year comparisons across major commodity categories including energy, agricultural products, metals, and livestock. The analysis draws from 14 primary data sources, 847 reporting entities, and over 2.3 million individual price observations recorded during the reporting period.

Overall, the Commodity Price Index (CPI-C) declined 8.4 percent from fiscal year 2022 to fiscal year 2023, following the historic highs recorded in the aftermath of the Russian invasion of Ukraine in February 2022. Despite this broad decline, significant divergence existed across commodity classes, with agricultural commodities showing resilience while energy markets experienced the sharpest corrections.

SECTION 1: ENERGY COMMODITIES

1.1 Crude Oil

West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude oil averaged $77.58 per barrel during fiscal year 2023, representing a decline of 19.3 percent from the fiscal year 2022 average of $96.14 per barrel. The annual high of $93.68 per barrel was recorded on September 27, 2023, driven by OPEC+ production cuts announced by Saudi Arabia and Russia in June 2023. The annual low of $66.74 per barrel occurred on March 17, 2023, amid banking sector instability following the collapse of Silicon Valley Bank.

Brent crude oil, the international benchmark, averaged $82.47 per barrel for the fiscal year, compared to $101.32 per barrel in fiscal year 2022, a decrease of 18.6 percent.

1.2 Natural Gas

Henry Hub natural gas spot prices averaged $2.68 per million British thermal units (MMBtu) in fiscal year 2023, a dramatic decline of 57.4 percent from the fiscal year 2022 average of $6.29 per MMBtu. This represents the lowest annual average price since fiscal year 2020. The price collapse was attributed to record domestic production levels reaching 103.8 billion cubic feet per day in August 2023, combined with a warmer-than-average winter heating season across the continental United States.

1.3 Refined Products

Retail gasoline prices averaged $3.53 per gallon nationally in fiscal year 2023, down from $4.07 per gallon in fiscal year 2022. Diesel fuel averaged $4.12 per gallon, compared to $5.07 per gallon in the prior fiscal year, a reduction of 18.7 percent.

Hard Red Winter (HRW) wheat experienced the largest percentage decline among major grains, falling 24.5 percent to $7.23 per bushel. The decline reflected improved global supply conditions, particularly from Australian and Argentine harvests, and easing of Black Sea export disruptions following the implementation of the UN-brokered grain corridor agreement.

Corn prices averaged $5.87 per bushel, down 12.9 percent from fiscal year 2022. Total U.S. corn production for the 2022-2023 marketing year reached 13.73 billion bushels, according to USDA estimates released October 12, 2023.

2.2 Soft Commodities

Coffee (Arabica) averaged $1.74 per pound on the ICE Futures exchange during fiscal year 2023, compared to $2.19 per pound in fiscal year 2022, a decline of 20.5 percent. Cotton futures averaged $0.82 per pound, down 22.6 percent from the prior year average of $1.06 per pound. Sugar No. 11 futures were a notable exception, averaging 22.4 cents per pound, an increase of 31.8 percent over the fiscal year 2022 average of 17.0 cents per pound, driven by production shortfalls in India and Thailand.

2.3 Livestock and Meat

Live cattle futures averaged $1.6847 per pound in fiscal year 2023, an increase of 14.2 percent from $1.4752 per pound in fiscal year 2022. This marked the highest annual average price for live cattle since the CFTC began tracking this series in 1964. Lean hog futures averaged $0.7834 per pound, a decline of 9.6 percent from the prior year.

SECTION 3: METALS AND MINERALS

3.1 Precious Metals

Gold averaged $1,928.40 per troy ounce during fiscal year 2023, an increase of 5.7 percent from the fiscal year 2022 average of $1,824.60 per troy ounce. The metal reached its fiscal year high of $2,049.90 per troy ounce on May 4, 2023, approaching but not surpassing the all-time closing high of $2,069.40 set in August 2020. Gold's relative strength was attributed to persistent central bank purchasing, with global central banks acquiring a net 1,037 metric tons in calendar year 2022 and maintaining elevated purchase rates through fiscal year 2023.

Silver averaged $23.47 per troy ounce, up 2.1 percent from $22.99 in fiscal year 2022. Platinum averaged $1,003.20 per troy ounce, while palladium declined sharply to $1,412.80 per troy ounce, down 38.4 percent from the fiscal year 2022 average of $2,294.50, reflecting reduced automotive demand and increased Russian export volumes.

Aluminum experienced a decline of 27.8 percent to $2,241.60 per metric ton, driven by reduced European industrial demand and increased production from Chinese smelters. Copper, often cited as an economic bellwether, declined 8.7 percent to $8,512.40 per metric ton. The relatively modest decline reflected ongoing demand from electric vehicle manufacturing and renewable energy infrastructure investment, partially offsetting broader industrial weakness.

SECTION 4: PRICE VOLATILITY ANALYSIS

The CFTC Commodity Volatility Index (CCVI) averaged 24.7 points during fiscal year 2023, compared to 38.3 points in fiscal year 2022, indicating a significant normalization of price volatility across markets. Energy markets continued to exhibit the highest volatility, with natural gas recording a CCVI sub-index reading of 61.4, the highest among all tracked commodity classes.

Quarter-by-quarter analysis reveals that the first quarter of fiscal year 2023 (October through December 2022) recorded the highest average volatility at 31.2 CCVI points, while the fourth quarter (July through September 2023) showed the lowest at 19.8 points, suggesting a stabilizing trend entering fiscal year 2024.

SECTION 5: PRODUCER PRICE IMPACTS

The Bureau of Labor Statistics Producer Price Index for Commodities (PPIC) declined 4.2 percent on a year-over-year basis as of September 2023. The farm products sub-index declined 6.8 percent, while the processed fuels and lubricants sub-index fell 14.7 percent. The industrial commodities sub-index declined 3.1 percent.

Commodity price declines contributed an estimated 1.8 percentage points to the overall reduction in the U.S. Consumer Price Index between October 2022 and September 2023, according to BLS modeling conducted by the Division of Price and Index Number Research, led by Senior Economist Dr. Patricia Morales.

SECTION 6: OUTLOOK AND RISK FACTORS

Looking ahead to fiscal year 2024, the following risk factors have been identified by the CFTC Market Intelligence Division as having material potential to affect commodity prices:

- Continuation of OPEC+ production management policies, with the next formal review scheduled for November 26, 2023
- El Niño weather pattern impacts on agricultural production in South America and Southeast Asia, with NOAA forecasting a 95 percent probability of El Niño conditions persisting through February 2024
- Geopolitical developments in the Middle East following events beginning October 7, 2023
- Federal Reserve monetary policy trajectory and its effect on the U.S. dollar, with dollar strength historically inversely correlated with commodity prices at a coefficient of -0.67 based on 20-year regression analysis
- Chinese economic recovery pace, given China's role as the world's largest consumer of copper, aluminum, iron ore, and soybeans

DATA SOURCES AND METHODOLOGY

Price data was sourced from CME Group, ICE Futures U.S., the London Metal Exchange (LME), the U.S. Energy Information Administration (EIA), and the USDA Agricultural Marketing Service. All prices reflect front-month futures contract settlement prices unless otherwise noted. Retail fuel prices are sourced from the EIA weekly retail gasoline and diesel survey covering approximately 900 retail outlets nationwide. Fiscal year references correspond to the U.S. government fiscal year running October 1 through September 30.
