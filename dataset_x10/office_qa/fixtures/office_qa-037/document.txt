EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The Commonwealth of Pennsylvania recorded total General Fund revenues of $11.84 billion in the third quarter of fiscal year 2023-2024 (January through March 2024), representing a 6.2 percent increase over the $11.15 billion collected in the second quarter (October through December 2023). Total General Fund expenditures for Q3 FY2024 reached $12.31 billion, up 3.8 percent from $11.86 billion in Q2 FY2024. The resulting quarterly deficit of $470 million reflects seasonal patterns consistent with prior fiscal years, driven primarily by elevated Medicaid disbursements and education funding distributions in the winter quarter.

Year-to-date General Fund revenues through March 31, 2024 stand at $33.97 billion, which is $412 million, or 1.2 percent, above the Administration's official estimate of $33.56 billion published in the Governor's Executive Budget on February 6, 2024.

1.2 Key Revenue Observations

Personal Income Tax (PIT): The $579.3 million quarter-over-quarter increase in PIT collections is the largest single driver of Q3 revenue growth. This surge is attributable to the April 15 estimated payment deadline falling within the Q3 window, as well as stronger-than-anticipated wage withholding. The Pennsylvania Department of Revenue reported that employer withholding alone totaled $3.14 billion in Q3, up from $2.87 billion in Q2, a 9.4 percent increase reflecting continued labor market strength. The Commonwealth's unemployment rate held at 3.4 percent as of February 2024, according to the Pennsylvania Department of Labor and Industry.

Corporate Net Income Tax (CNIT): CNIT collections of $1.622 billion in Q3 represent a 55.4 percent increase over Q2 and exceed the quarterly estimate by $214 million. The Department of Revenue attributes this performance to large one-time settlements from three multinational corporations totaling approximately $387 million, finalized between January 19 and March 4, 2024. Excluding these settlements, underlying CNIT growth was approximately 13.7 percent quarter-over-quarter.

Sales and Use Tax: The $183.4 million decline in Sales and Use Tax revenue reflects typical post-holiday seasonal softening. December 2023 collections, captured in Q2, benefited from holiday retail activity. January and February 2024 collections averaged $978.3 million per month, compared to $1.09 billion per month in October and November 2023.

Non-Tax Revenue: The sharp 54.1 percent decline in non-tax revenue from $563.6 million to $258.8 million is explained by the one-time receipt in Q2 of $298.7 million from the Pennsylvania Liquor Control Board's annual transfer, which does not recur in Q3.

2.2 Key Expenditure Observations

Human Services and Medicaid: Medicaid expenditures of $5.214 billion in Q3 represent the largest single expenditure category and reflect the continued unwinding of the federal continuous enrollment requirement that ended in March 2023. The Pennsylvania Department of Human Services processed 94,300 Medicaid redeterminations in Q3 FY2024, with approximately 18.7 percent of reviewed cases resulting in disenrollment. Despite disenrollments, per-member costs rose 4.1 percent quarter-over-quarter, driven by increased utilization of behavioral health services.

Education: The $243.8 million increase in PreK-12 education spending reflects the Commonwealth's accelerated distribution of Basic Education Funding under Act 55 of 2023. Secretary of Education Dr. Khalid Mumin confirmed on March 14, 2024, that $1.1 billion in supplemental equity funding was distributed to 100 low-wealth school districts during Q3, with the Philadelphia School District receiving the largest single allocation of $187.4 million.

Debt Service: The 25.6 percent increase in debt service payments reflects the maturation of $600 million in General Obligation bonds originally issued in March 2014 and the associated final principal payment processed on February 1, 2024. This is a non-recurring expenditure item.

Transportation: The $57.5 million decline in transportation spending reflects the completion of several major capital projects in Q2, including the I-95 Philadelphia reconstruction project, which drew down $43.2 million in state matching funds in November 2023.

SECTION 3: FUND BALANCE AND CASH POSITION

As of March 31, 2024, the General Fund unappropriated balance stood at $3.87 billion, compared to $4.34 billion at December 31, 2023, a decrease of $470 million consistent with the quarterly operating deficit noted above.

The Commonwealth's Budget Stabilization Reserve Fund (Rainy Day Fund) remained unchanged at $5.07 billion as of March 31, 2024, representing approximately 10.3 percent of annual General Fund appropriations. No transfers into or out of the Rainy Day Fund were authorized during Q3 FY2024.

The Commonwealth Treasurer, Stacy Garrity, reported that the Treasury's short-term investment pool generated $94.6 million in investment income during Q3 FY2024, compared to $101.3 million in Q2 FY2024, a decline of 6.6 percent reflecting lower average daily cash balances.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

SECTION 4: YEAR-TO-DATE PERFORMANCE VERSUS ESTIMATES

Through March 31, 2024, cumulative General Fund revenues of $33.97 billion are running $412 million, or 1.2 percent, above the Administration's official estimate. The largest positive variance is in Corporate Net Income Tax, which is $631 million above estimate year-to-date, partially offset by a $218 million shortfall in Sales and Use Tax collections relative to estimate.

Total year-to-date expenditures of $35.48 billion are $143 million, or 0.4 percent, below the appropriated level, primarily due to $97 million in unspent funds within the Department of Corrections related to delayed facility renovation contracts.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

SECTION 5: OUTLOOK FOR Q4 FY2024

The Office of the Budget projects Q4 FY2024 (April through June 2024) General Fund revenues of approximately $10.9 billion, reflecting the absence of the large CNIT settlements that boosted Q3 performance. Final income tax return payments due April 15, 2024 are expected to contribute approximately $1.3 billion in additional PIT revenue. Total Q4 expenditures are projected at $11.6 billion, resulting in a projected Q4 deficit of approximately $700 million.

The Administration projects the fiscal year will close on June 30, 2024 with a General Fund balance of approximately $3.17 billion, which would represent a $1.17 billion improvement over the $2.0 billion balance recorded at the close of FY2022-2023.
