EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This report presents comprehensive commodity price statistics for the United States fiscal year ending September 30, 2023. Data compiled herein reflects price movements across energy, agricultural, metals, and soft commodity sectors. The aggregate Commodity Price Index (CPI-C) declined 11.4 percent year-over-year, following the historic highs recorded in fiscal year 2022, which were driven primarily by supply chain disruptions and geopolitical instability in Eastern Europe. Despite this broad correction, select agricultural commodities and industrial metals demonstrated resilience, with cocoa futures reaching a 46-year high by the fourth quarter of calendar year 2023.

This report is prepared pursuant to the Commodity Exchange Act (7 U.S.C. § 1 et seq.) and is intended for use by policymakers, market participants, and the general public.

SECTION 1: ENERGY COMMODITIES

1.1 Crude Oil

West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude oil averaged $77.58 per barrel during fiscal year 2023, representing a decline of 18.3 percent from the fiscal year 2022 average of $94.97 per barrel. The annual low of $66.74 per barrel was recorded on June 12, 2023, while the annual high of $93.68 per barrel occurred on September 27, 2023, driven by OPEC+ production cuts announced by Saudi Arabia and Russia totaling 1.3 million barrels per day.

Brent crude oil, the international benchmark, averaged $82.14 per barrel for the same period, maintaining a premium of approximately $4.56 per barrel over WTI, consistent with historical spreads.

1.2 Natural Gas

Henry Hub natural gas spot prices averaged $2.74 per million British thermal units (MMBtu) in fiscal year 2023, a dramatic decline of 57.6 percent from the fiscal year 2022 average of $6.45 per MMBtu. This correction followed the extraordinary price spike attributable to European demand for U.S. liquefied natural gas (LNG) exports following the Russia-Ukraine conflict. The fiscal year low of $1.97 per MMBtu was recorded on March 3, 2023.

1.3 Gasoline and Distillates

Retail regular-grade gasoline averaged $3.53 per gallon nationally during fiscal year 2023, down from $3.97 per gallon in fiscal year 2022. Diesel fuel averaged $4.01 per gallon, reflecting a 14.7 percent year-over-year decline.

SECTION 2: AGRICULTURAL COMMODITIES

2.1 Grains

Corn (per bushel) | $5.47 | $6.81 | -19.7%

Wheat (CBOT, per bushel) | $6.18 | $9.14 | -32.4%

Wheat prices experienced the most significant correction among major grains, falling 32.4 percent as Ukrainian export corridors partially reopened under the Black Sea Grain Initiative, which remained operative through July 17, 2023, before Russia's withdrawal from the agreement. Following Russia's withdrawal, Chicago Board of Trade (CBOT) wheat futures surged 8.5 percent in a single trading session on July 18, 2023.

2.2 Oilseeds and Vegetable Oils

Palm oil, a critical global commodity, averaged $879 per metric ton on the Malaysian Derivatives Exchange during fiscal year 2023, down 31.2 percent from the fiscal year 2022 average of $1,277 per metric ton. Canola futures on the Intercontinental Exchange averaged $614 Canadian dollars per metric ton.

2.3 Livestock

Live cattle futures averaged $1.7842 per pound during fiscal year 2023, an increase of 14.6 percent from the prior year average of $1.5568 per pound. This represents the highest annual average price for live cattle since the CFTC began tracking standardized futures data in 1964. Lean hog futures averaged $0.7831 per pound, a decline of 6.2 percent year-over-year.

2.4 Soft Commodities

Cocoa futures on the Intercontinental Exchange New York averaged $3,107 per metric ton during fiscal year 2023, rising 28.9 percent from the fiscal year 2022 average of $2,410 per metric ton. By December 2023, cocoa futures had surpassed $4,200 per metric ton, levels not seen since 1977, driven by El Niño weather patterns severely impacting production in Ghana and Côte d'Ivoire, which together account for approximately 60 percent of global cocoa supply.

Coffee (Arabica) averaged $1.6842 per pound, while raw sugar averaged $0.2287 per pound, an increase of 31.4 percent year-over-year, reaching its highest level since 2011.

SECTION 3: METALS AND MINERALS

3.1 Precious Metals

Gold averaged $1,941.60 per troy ounce during fiscal year 2023, an increase of 7.3 percent from the fiscal year 2022 average of $1,808.90 per troy ounce. Gold reached an intraday record high of $2,081.82 per troy ounce on May 4, 2023, surpassing the previous record set in August 2020. The metal's performance was supported by persistent central bank purchasing, with global central banks acquiring a net 1,037 metric tons of gold in calendar year 2022, the highest annual total since 1967, according to the World Gold Council.

Silver averaged $23.47 per troy ounce, a modest increase of 2.1 percent year-over-year. The gold-to-silver ratio averaged 82.6:1 during the fiscal year, indicating silver's relative underperformance compared to historical norms of approximately 60:1.

Nickel experienced extreme volatility in the prior fiscal year following the March 2022 short squeeze on the London Metal Exchange, which temporarily pushed prices above $100,000 per metric ton before trading was suspended.

3.3 Industrial Minerals

Lithium carbonate prices, critical to electric vehicle battery manufacturing, declined sharply during fiscal year 2023. Chinese spot prices for battery-grade lithium carbonate fell from a peak of approximately $81,000 per metric ton in November 2022 to approximately $21,500 per metric ton by September 2023, a decline of 73.5 percent, driven by expanded production capacity and moderating EV demand growth in China.

SECTION 4: PRODUCER PRICE INDEX — COMMODITY COMPONENTS

The Bureau of Labor Statistics Producer Price Index for All Commodities (PPI-C) registered 204.6 in September 2023 (1982=100), compared to 228.4 in September 2022, representing a year-over-year decline of 10.4 percent. This marks the largest annual decline in the PPI-C since the 2008-2009 financial crisis, when the index fell 18.7 percent between July 2008 and July 2009.

Key sub-index readings for September 2023:
- Crude petroleum: 189.4 (down 21.3% year-over-year)
- Natural gas: 112.7 (down 61.8% year-over-year)
- Grains: 231.8 (down 17.2% year-over-year)
- Metals and metal products: 278.3 (down 8.9% year-over-year)
- Livestock and poultry: 312.4 (up 9.1% year-over-year)

SECTION 5: MARKET OUTLOOK AND POLICY CONSIDERATIONS

The broad commodity price correction observed in fiscal year 2023 has contributed meaningfully to the Federal Reserve's inflation reduction objectives. The Federal Open Market Committee noted in its September 20, 2023 meeting minutes that commodity price normalization accounted for approximately 1.8 percentage points of the decline in headline Consumer Price Index inflation from its June 2022 peak of 9.1 percent.

Looking forward, the CFTC's Office of Market Intelligence has identified three primary risk factors for fiscal year 2024 commodity markets:

1. El Niño weather disruptions affecting agricultural production in Southeast Asia, West Africa, and South America, with the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA) projecting a moderate-to-strong El Niño event persisting through the first quarter of calendar year 2024.

2. Continued OPEC+ production management, with the cartel's stated commitment to maintaining output restrictions through December 2024.

3. Geopolitical risk premiums in energy markets, particularly related to Middle East tensions following events beginning October 7, 2023.

DATA SOURCES AND METHODOLOGY

Price data in this report is sourced from the following primary databases: CME Group market data, Intercontinental Exchange (ICE) settlement prices, London Metal Exchange official prices, U.S. Energy Information Administration weekly petroleum reports, and USDA Agricultural Marketing Service daily price reports. All averages represent arithmetic means of daily settlement or spot prices unless otherwise noted. Currency conversions use fiscal year average exchange rates published by the U.S. Department of the Treasury.
