EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The Commonwealth of Pennsylvania recorded total General Fund revenues of $11.84 billion in the third quarter of fiscal year 2023-2024 (Q3 FY2023-24), representing an increase of $1.27 billion, or 12.0 percent, compared to the $10.57 billion collected in the second quarter (Q2 FY2023-24). Total General Fund expenditures for Q3 reached $10.93 billion, up from $10.41 billion in Q2, reflecting a quarter-over-quarter increase of $520 million, or 5.0 percent. As a result, the Commonwealth posted a net quarterly surplus of $910 million in Q3, compared to a surplus of $160 million in Q2, an improvement of $750 million.

The General Fund ending balance as of March 31, 2024, stood at $4.63 billion, compared to $3.72 billion as of December 31, 2023. Budget Secretary Uri Monson noted that the Q3 performance reflects stronger-than-anticipated personal income tax collections driven by wage growth and year-end bonus distributions, as well as continued moderation in Medical Assistance spending growth.

1.2 Key Revenue Observations

Personal Income Tax (PIT): PIT collections of $4.512 billion in Q3 exceeded Q2 collections by $621 million. This increase is primarily attributable to the January 15, 2024, quarterly estimated payment deadline, which captured significant fourth-quarter 2023 earnings, including elevated year-end bonuses in the financial services and technology sectors. Withholding receipts also rose by 8.3 percent quarter-over-quarter, consistent with Pennsylvania's seasonally adjusted unemployment rate declining to 3.4 percent in February 2024, as reported by the Pennsylvania Department of Labor and Industry.

Corporate Net Income Tax (CNIT): CNIT collections surged by $342 million, or 32.66 percent, to $1.389 billion. The Commonwealth's CNIT rate decreased from 8.99 percent to 8.49 percent effective January 1, 2024, under Act 53 of 2022's phased reduction schedule. Despite the rate reduction, collections increased due to strong corporate profitability in Q4 2023 filings and accelerated estimated payments by several large multistate corporations headquartered in the Philadelphia metropolitan area.

Sales and Use Tax (SUT): SUT collections declined modestly by $86 million, or 2.68 percent, to $3.118 billion. This reflects typical post-holiday seasonal softening in consumer retail spending following the elevated December 2023 holiday period. Online marketplace facilitator collections, however, increased by $34 million quarter-over-quarter, partially offsetting the decline in brick-and-mortar retail receipts.

Non-Tax Revenue: Non-tax revenue increased by $290 million to $1.799 billion, largely due to $218 million in federal reimbursements received in January and February 2024 related to prior-period Medical Assistance claims adjustments processed by the Department of Human Services.

2.2 Key Expenditure Observations

Debt Service: The most significant quarter-over-quarter expenditure increase occurred in debt service, which rose by $186 million, or 30.39 percent, to $798 million. This spike reflects the February 1, 2024, semi-annual principal and interest payment on the Commonwealth's Series 2019A General Obligation Bonds, totaling $143 million, as well as a $41 million payment on the 2021B GO Bond series. These payments are scheduled obligations and were fully anticipated in the enacted budget.

Education Spending: PreK-12 education expenditures increased by $77 million to $3.204 billion. The increase reflects the January distribution of Basic Education Funding subsidy payments to 500 school districts across the Commonwealth, totaling $2.87 billion on an annualized basis. The Q3 distribution included a supplemental allocation of $62 million to 47 low-wealth districts pursuant to the Commonwealth Court's February 7, 2024, remedial order in William Penn School District v. Pennsylvania Department of Education.

Medical Assistance: Medical Assistance expenditures grew by only $53 million, or 1.83 percent, to $2.944 billion, representing the slowest quarter-over-quarter growth rate in this category in six quarters. The Department of Human Services attributed the moderation to the completion of the federally mandated Medicaid continuous enrollment unwinding process, which reduced the Commonwealth's enrolled Medical Assistance population by approximately 94,000 individuals between October 2023 and March 2024.

SECTION 3: FUND BALANCE AND LIQUIDITY

3.1 General Fund Balance Position

As of March 31, 2024, the General Fund unappropriated surplus stood at $4.63 billion, an increase of $910 million from the December 31, 2023, balance of $3.72 billion. The Commonwealth's Budget Stabilization Reserve Fund (Rainy Day Fund) remained unchanged at $1.47 billion during Q3, as no transfers were authorized by the General Assembly during this period.

The Commonwealth's short-term liquidity position remained strong. The Treasury Department, under Treasurer Stacy Garrity, reported that the Commonwealth's investment pool earned an average annualized yield of 5.31 percent during Q3 FY2023-24, compared to 5.18 percent in Q2, generating approximately $187 million in investment income for the quarter.

3.2 Year-to-Date Position (July 1, 2023 – March 31, 2024)

Year-to-date General Fund revenues through March 31, 2024, totaled $31.96 billion, compared to the nine-month prorated budget estimate of $31.44 billion, resulting in a positive revenue variance of $520 million, or 1.65 percent above estimate. Year-to-date expenditures totaled $31.05 billion against a prorated budget of $31.38 billion, reflecting a favorable spending variance of $330 million.

SECTION 4: NOTABLE FISCAL DEVELOPMENTS AND OUTLOOK

4.1 Legislative and Policy Factors

Governor Josh Shapiro's proposed FY2024-25 budget, submitted to the General Assembly on February 6, 2024, calls for total General Fund spending of $48.3 billion, a 6.9 percent increase over the current enacted budget of $45.2 billion. The proposal includes a $1.1 billion increase in basic education funding and a $525 million investment in the new Pennsylvania Land Bank and Housing Affordability Fund. Negotiations with the Republican-controlled Senate and Democratic-controlled House are ongoing as of the publication date of this report.

4.2 Q4 Outlook

The Office of the Budget projects Q4 FY2023-24 (April 1 – June 30, 2024) General Fund revenues of approximately $12.1 billion, driven primarily by the April 15, 2024, personal income tax filing deadline, which historically represents the single largest revenue event of the fiscal year. Final income tax settlement payments are projected to contribute approximately $1.8 billion in April alone. Q4 expenditures are projected at $11.6 billion, yielding an estimated Q4 surplus of $500 million and a projected fiscal year-end General Fund balance of approximately $5.1 billion.
