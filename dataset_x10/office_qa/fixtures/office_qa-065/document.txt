EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This report presents a comprehensive analysis of U.S. Treasury bond yield movements during the third fiscal quarter of 2024, covering the period from July 1, 2024 through September 30, 2024. The Federal Open Market Committee's decision to reduce the federal funds rate by 50 basis points on September 18, 2024 — the first rate reduction since March 2020 — produced measurable and significant changes across the Treasury yield curve. This document details the methodology used to calculate yield changes, presents the resulting figures across all major maturity segments, and assesses the impact on the outstanding federal debt portfolio valued at approximately $27.9 trillion as of September 30, 2024.

---

SECTION 1: BACKGROUND AND SCOPE

The Bureau of the Fiscal Service monitors Treasury bond yields on a continuous basis as part of its mandate to manage the cost of federal borrowing. Yield changes directly affect the government's interest expense, which reached $1.158 trillion in fiscal year 2024, representing 3.93 percent of gross domestic product and the largest annual interest payment in U.S. history.

This report focuses specifically on the calculation of yield changes for the following benchmark securities:

- 2-Year Treasury Note (CUSIP: 91282CKH4)
- 5-Year Treasury Note (CUSIP: 91282CKJ0)
- 10-Year Treasury Note (CUSIP: 91282CKG6)
- 20-Year Treasury Bond (CUSIP: 912810TZ6)
- 30-Year Treasury Bond (CUSIP: 912810UA9)

All yield data is sourced from the Federal Reserve H.15 Statistical Release and cross-referenced with Bloomberg Terminal data maintained by the Office of Debt Management.

SECTION 2: METHODOLOGY FOR YIELD CHANGE CALCULATION

2.1 Basic Yield Change Formula

The absolute yield change for any given security is calculated as follows:

Yield Change (basis points) = [Yield at End Date - Yield at Start Date] x 10,000

One basis point equals 0.01 percent, or 0.0001 in decimal form. This standardized unit allows for consistent comparison across maturities and time periods.

2.2 Percentage Yield Change

In addition to absolute basis point changes, this office calculates the percentage change in yield level:

Percentage Yield Change = [(Yield at End Date - Yield at Start Date) / Yield at Start Date] x 100

2.3 Price Sensitivity Calculation Using Modified Duration

Because bond prices move inversely to yields, the office calculates the estimated price impact using modified duration:

Estimated Price Change (%) = -Modified Duration x Yield Change (in decimal form)

For large yield movements, convexity adjustment is applied using the following formula:

Adjusted Price Change (%) = (-Modified Duration x Yield Change) + (0.5 x Convexity x Yield Change²)

All duration and convexity figures are sourced from the Treasury's proprietary debt management system, TreasuryDirect Analytics Platform (TDAP), updated daily as of market close at 3:00 PM Eastern Time.

SECTION 3: YIELD DATA AND CALCULATED CHANGES

3.1 Quarterly Yield Summary Table

The following table presents benchmark yields as of July 1, 2024 (quarter open) and September 30, 2024 (quarter close), along with calculated changes:

Maturity | July 1, 2024 Yield | Sept 30, 2024 Yield | Change (bps) | % Change
2-Year   | 4.75%              | 3.64%               | -111 bps     | -23.37%
5-Year   | 4.36%              | 3.57%               | -79 bps      | -18.12%
10-Year  | 4.40%              | 3.78%               | -62 bps      | -14.09%
20-Year  | 4.67%              | 4.17%               | -50 bps      | -10.71%
30-Year  | 4.51%              | 4.12%               | -39 bps      | -8.65%

Key observation: The 2-year Treasury note experienced the largest absolute decline of 111 basis points, consistent with its historically higher sensitivity to changes in the federal funds rate. The yield curve, which had been inverted since July 2022, moved toward normalization during this quarter, with the 10-year/2-year spread narrowing from -35 basis points on July 1 to +14 basis points on September 30, 2024.

3.2 Intra-Quarter Yield Movements

Notable yield inflection points during the quarter included:

- August 5, 2024: The 10-year yield fell to an intra-quarter low of 3.67% following weaker-than-expected July nonfarm payroll data released August 2, which showed 114,000 jobs added versus the consensus estimate of 185,000.

- September 18, 2024: Following the FOMC announcement of a 50 basis point rate cut (reducing the target range from 5.25-5.50% to 4.75-5.00%), the 2-year yield fell 13 basis points in a single session, closing at 3.59%.

- September 30, 2024: Quarter-end positioning pushed the 10-year yield to close at 3.78%, up 17 basis points from the September 18 post-announcement close of 3.61%.

SECTION 4: PRICE IMPACT ANALYSIS

4.1 Duration and Convexity Parameters

The following modified duration and convexity values were applied in price impact calculations as of September 30, 2024:

Security          | Modified Duration | Convexity
2-Year Note       | 1.87 years        | 0.042
5-Year Note       | 4.51 years        | 0.243
10-Year Note      | 8.62 years        | 0.876
20-Year Bond      | 13.44 years       | 2.341
30-Year Bond      | 17.21 years       | 3.892

4.2 Estimated Price Changes

Using the convexity-adjusted formula, estimated price changes for the quarter were:

2-Year Note: -1.87 x (-0.0111) + 0.5 x 0.042 x (0.0111²) = +2.077% + 0.000026% = +2.077%
10-Year Note: -8.62 x (-0.0062) + 0.5 x 0.876 x (0.0062²) = +5.344% + 0.0168% = +5.361%
30-Year Bond: -17.21 x (-0.0039) + 0.5 x 3.892 x (0.0039²) = +6.712% + 0.0296% = +6.742%

The 30-year bond's price appreciation of approximately 6.74% represented the largest gain in absolute percentage terms, reflecting its greater duration sensitivity.

SECTION 5: PORTFOLIO IMPACT ON FEDERAL DEBT

5.1 Outstanding Debt Composition

As of September 30, 2024, the total marketable Treasury debt outstanding was $27.9 trillion, distributed as follows:

- Treasury Bills (maturity under 1 year): $6.1 trillion (21.9%)
- Treasury Notes (1-10 year maturity): $14.8 trillion (53.0%)
- Treasury Bonds (over 10 year maturity): $4.6 trillion (16.5%)
- Treasury Inflation-Protected Securities (TIPS): $2.1 trillion (7.5%)
- Floating Rate Notes: $0.3 trillion (1.1%)

5.2 Projected Interest Cost Savings

The decline in yields across the curve is projected to reduce new issuance costs on debt maturing and being refinanced over the next 12 months. The Office of Debt Management estimates that the 79 basis point average decline across the 2-year through 5-year segment will reduce annualized interest expense on approximately $3.2 trillion in notes scheduled for refinancing by approximately $25.3 billion, assuming yields remain near September 30, 2024 levels.

This estimate is calculated as:
$3.2 trillion x 0.0079 (weighted average yield decline) = $25.28 billion in annualized savings

SECTION 6: YIELD CURVE NORMALIZATION ASSESSMENT

The re-steepening of the yield curve during Q3 2024 is a significant structural development. The 2-year/10-year spread moved from -35 basis points on July 1, 2024 to +14 basis points on September 30, 2024, a shift of 49 basis points. This represents the first sustained positive spread since the curve initially inverted on July 5, 2022.

Historical analysis conducted by the Office of Debt Management indicates that following the end of yield curve inversions in the post-1980 period, the 10-year yield has on average declined an additional 87 basis points over the subsequent 12 months. If this historical pattern holds, the 10-year yield could approach 2.91% by September 30, 2025, though the office cautions that current fiscal dynamics — including a projected fiscal year 2025 deficit of approximately $1.9 trillion — may exert upward pressure on longer-dated yields, limiting the extent of any further decline.

---

SECTION 7: LIMITATIONS AND DISCLAIMERS

Yield change calculations in this report are based on constant-maturity Treasury rates published by the Federal Reserve and may differ marginally from on-the-run security yields due to liquidity premiums. Price impact calculations assume parallel yield curve shifts and do not account for changes in yield curve shape or credit spread dynamics. Duration and convexity figures are point-in-time estimates and change continuously as market conditions evolve.

This report is prepared for internal planning purposes and does not constitute investment advice. Projections of future yield levels and interest cost savings are subject to material uncertainty.
