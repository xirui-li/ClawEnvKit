EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This report presents official commodity price statistics for the third quarter of 2024, covering energy, agricultural, metals, and industrial materials sectors. Data were compiled from the U.S. Energy Information Administration (EIA), the U.S. Department of Agriculture (USDA), the London Metal Exchange (LME), and the Chicago Mercantile Exchange (CME). All price figures are denominated in U.S. dollars unless otherwise noted. This report is prepared in accordance with the Federal Statistical Policy Directive No. 1 and is intended for use by policymakers, economists, and the general public.

SECTION 1: ENERGY COMMODITIES

1.1 Crude Oil

West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude oil averaged $78.42 per barrel during Q3 2024, representing a decline of 6.3 percent from the Q2 2024 average of $83.70 per barrel. This marks the second consecutive quarterly decline following the peak of $91.14 per barrel recorded in Q3 2023.

Brent crude, the international benchmark, averaged $82.17 per barrel in Q3 2024, down 5.8 percent from $87.22 in Q2 2024. The spread between Brent and WTI narrowed to $3.75 per barrel, compared to $3.52 in the prior quarter.

Monthly breakdown for WTI crude oil:
  - July 2024: $82.11 per barrel
  - August 2024: $77.34 per barrel
  - September 2024: $75.81 per barrel

The September 2024 figure represents the lowest monthly average since December 2023, when WTI closed at $73.96 per barrel. The decline is attributed primarily to reduced demand signals from China, whose crude oil imports fell 3.1 percent year-over-year in August 2024 according to Chinese customs data.

1.2 Natural Gas

Henry Hub natural gas spot prices averaged $2.19 per million British thermal units (MMBtu) in Q3 2024, a decrease of 11.7 percent from the Q2 2024 average of $2.48 per MMBtu. This figure remains substantially below the five-year Q3 average of $3.84 per MMBtu, reflecting elevated domestic production levels. U.S. dry natural gas production reached 103.8 billion cubic feet per day in August 2024, a record high for that month.

1.3 Gasoline and Distillates

The U.S. average retail price of regular unleaded gasoline was $3.31 per gallon in Q3 2024, down from $3.67 per gallon in Q3 2023, a year-over-year decrease of 9.8 percent. Diesel fuel averaged $3.84 per gallon during the same period, compared to $4.21 per gallon in Q3 2023, representing a decline of 8.8 percent.

SECTION 2: AGRICULTURAL COMMODITIES

2.1 Grains

Corn futures on the Chicago Board of Trade (CBOT) averaged $4.07 per bushel in Q3 2024, a decline of 14.2 percent from $4.74 per bushel in Q3 2023. This represents the lowest quarterly average since Q1 2021, when corn traded at $5.23 per bushel. The USDA projects total U.S. corn production for the 2024/2025 marketing year at 15.186 billion bushels, up 3.4 percent from the prior year.

Wheat (CBOT soft red winter) averaged $5.52 per bushel in Q3 2024, compared to $6.31 per bushel in Q3 2023, a year-over-year decrease of 12.5 percent. Global wheat production is estimated at 793 million metric tons for the 2024/2025 marketing year, according to the USDA World Agricultural Supply and Demand Estimates (WASDE) report released October 11, 2024.

Soybeans averaged $9.78 per bushel in Q3 2024, down 18.6 percent from $12.01 per bushel in Q3 2023. The sharp decline reflects record South American production, with Brazil's 2023/2024 soybean crop estimated at 153.0 million metric tons.

2.2 Livestock and Dairy

Live cattle futures averaged $185.42 per hundredweight (cwt) in Q3 2024, an increase of 7.3 percent from $172.82 per cwt in Q3 2023, driven by tightening U.S. cattle supplies. The U.S. cattle inventory as of July 1, 2024 stood at 86.7 million head, the lowest July 1 count since 1951.

Class III milk futures averaged $19.24 per hundredweight in Q3 2024, up 4.1 percent from $18.48 per cwt in Q3 2023.

2.3 Soft Commodities

Coffee (arabica, ICE Futures U.S.) averaged $2.31 per pound in Q3 2024, an increase of 22.8 percent from $1.88 per pound in Q3 2023. Drought conditions in Brazil's Minas Gerais state, which produces approximately 50 percent of Brazil's arabica coffee, contributed significantly to the price increase.

Raw sugar (ICE No. 11) averaged 19.4 cents per pound in Q3 2024, down from 24.7 cents per pound in Q3 2023, a decline of 21.5 percent.

SECTION 3: METALS AND MINERALS

3.1 Precious Metals

Gold averaged $2,474.50 per troy ounce in Q3 2024, an increase of 28.4 percent from $1,927.80 per troy ounce in Q3 2023. Gold reached an intraday record high of $2,531.60 per troy ounce on August 20, 2024. The sustained rally is attributed to central bank purchasing activity, with global central banks acquiring a net 483 metric tons in the first half of 2024 according to the World Gold Council.

Silver averaged $29.32 per troy ounce in Q3 2024, up 26.1 percent from $23.25 per troy ounce in Q3 2023. The gold-to-silver ratio stood at 84.4 at the end of September 2024.

3.3 Steel and Iron Ore

U.S. hot-rolled coil (HRC) steel averaged $680 per short ton in Q3 2024, down 8.1 percent from $740 per short ton in Q2 2024. Iron ore (62% Fe, CFR China) averaged $104.20 per dry metric ton in Q3 2024, a decline of 17.3 percent from $126.0 per dry metric ton in Q3 2023.

SECTION 4: COMPOSITE PRICE INDICES

4.1 Producer Price Index — Commodities

The Bureau of Labor Statistics Producer Price Index (PPI) for crude materials for further processing declined 2.1 percent in Q3 2024 on a seasonally adjusted basis, following a 0.4 percent decline in Q2 2024. On a year-over-year basis, the index was down 3.7 percent as of September 2024.

4.2 IMF Primary Commodity Price Index

The International Monetary Fund's Primary Commodity Price Index fell 4.2 percent in Q3 2024 compared to Q2 2024. The energy sub-index declined 5.9 percent, while the food and beverages sub-index fell 2.8 percent. The metals sub-index rose 3.1 percent, driven by gold and copper performance.

SECTION 5: OUTLOOK AND POLICY CONSIDERATIONS

The Federal Reserve's September 18, 2024 decision to reduce the federal funds rate by 50 basis points to a target range of 4.75–5.00 percent is expected to exert modest upward pressure on commodity prices in Q4 2024 through a weaker U.S. dollar and improved risk appetite. The U.S. Dollar Index (DXY) declined 4.8 percent during Q3 2024, closing at 100.77 on September 30, 2024.

Key risk factors for Q4 2024 include:
  - Escalation of Middle East geopolitical tensions affecting crude oil supply routes
  - La Niña weather pattern development, which may affect South American agricultural output
  - Continued slowdown in Chinese industrial activity, particularly in the property sector
  - OPEC+ production policy decisions scheduled for review in December 2024

The EIA projects WTI crude oil to average $76.00 per barrel in Q4 2024, while the USDA forecasts corn prices to remain in the $4.00–$4.25 per bushel range through the end of the 2024/2025 marketing year.

DATA SOURCES AND METHODOLOGY

Price data were sourced from the following primary databases: EIA Weekly Petroleum Status Report, USDA WASDE monthly reports, CME Group DataMine, LME official settlement prices, and ICE Futures U.S. daily settlement data. All quarterly averages represent arithmetic means of daily settlement or spot prices unless otherwise specified. Seasonal adjustments follow the X-13ARIMA-SEATS methodology as implemented by the U.S. Census Bureau.
