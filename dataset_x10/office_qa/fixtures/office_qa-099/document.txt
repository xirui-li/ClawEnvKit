EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This report presents comprehensive commodity price statistics for the United States fiscal year ending September 30, 2023. Data has been compiled from the Chicago Mercantile Exchange (CME), the New York Mercantile Exchange (NYMEX), the Intercontinental Exchange (ICE), and domestic producer price indices maintained by the Bureau of Labor Statistics. The report covers energy commodities, agricultural products, metals, and livestock, providing year-over-year comparisons, quarterly breakdowns, and forward-looking assessments based on current market conditions.

Overall, commodity markets in FY2023 experienced significant volatility driven by geopolitical disruptions, Federal Reserve monetary policy adjustments, and persistent supply chain normalization following the COVID-19 pandemic. The Producer Price Index (PPI) for all commodities declined 4.7 percent from FY2022 to FY2023, representing the first annual decline since FY2019.

SECTION 1: ENERGY COMMODITIES

1.1 Crude Oil

West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude oil averaged $77.58 per barrel during FY2023, representing a decline of 18.3 percent compared to the FY2022 average of $94.97 per barrel. This decline was the most substantial annual drop since FY2020, when pandemic-related demand destruction caused prices to collapse.

Quarterly WTI Price Averages (FY2023):

  Q1 (October–December 2022): $82.64 per barrel
  Q2 (January–March 2023): $76.13 per barrel
  Q3 (April–June 2023): $73.78 per barrel
  Q4 (July–September 2023): $82.26 per barrel

The Q3 trough reflected increased U.S. domestic production, which reached 12.9 million barrels per day in June 2023, the highest monthly output since March 2020. OPEC+ production cuts announced on April 2, 2023, totaling 1.66 million barrels per day, contributed to the Q4 price recovery.

Brent crude oil, the international benchmark, averaged $82.17 per barrel for FY2023, compared to $100.85 per barrel in FY2022, a decline of 18.5 percent.

1.2 Natural Gas

Henry Hub natural gas spot prices averaged $2.74 per million British thermal units (MMBtu) in FY2023, a dramatic decline of 57.4 percent from the FY2022 average of $6.43 per MMBtu. This represented the lowest annual average since FY2020.

Key contributing factors included:
  - Record U.S. natural gas production of 103.8 billion cubic feet per day in August 2023
  - Mild winter temperatures across the continental United States in Q1 FY2023
  - Reduced European demand following successful liquefied natural gas (LNG) import diversification
  - Storage levels reaching 3.64 trillion cubic feet by October 2023, 5.2 percent above the five-year average

1.3 Gasoline and Distillates

The U.S. average retail price for regular unleaded gasoline was $3.53 per gallon in FY2023, down from $3.99 per gallon in FY2022. Ultra-low sulfur diesel averaged $4.12 per gallon, compared to $5.07 per gallon the prior year, a decline of 18.7 percent.

SECTION 2: AGRICULTURAL COMMODITIES

2.1 Grains

Corn futures on the Chicago Board of Trade (CBOT) averaged $5.62 per bushel in FY2023, compared to $6.73 per bushel in FY2022, a year-over-year decline of 16.5 percent. The USDA's October 2023 World Agricultural Supply and Demand Estimates (WASDE) projected total U.S. corn production at 15.234 billion bushels for the 2023/24 marketing year, up 10.1 percent from the prior year.

Wheat prices on the CBOT averaged $6.48 per bushel in FY2023, down from $8.89 per bushel in FY2022, a decline of 27.1 percent. The easing of supply concerns following the Black Sea Grain Initiative, which facilitated Ukrainian grain exports through July 17, 2023, contributed to price normalization during the first three quarters of the fiscal year. Following the initiative's collapse, wheat futures briefly spiked to $7.24 per bushel in late July 2023 before retreating.

Soybean futures averaged $13.71 per bushel in FY2023, compared to $14.38 per bushel in FY2022, a modest decline of 4.7 percent. Record Brazilian soybean production of 162.4 million metric tons in the 2022/23 marketing year exerted sustained downward pressure on prices throughout the fiscal year.

2.2 Soft Commodities

Arabica coffee futures on the ICE averaged $1.68 per pound in FY2023, down 12.5 percent from $1.92 per pound in FY2022. Raw sugar futures averaged 23.4 cents per pound, representing a 14.7 percent increase from 20.4 cents per pound in FY2022, driven by production shortfalls in India and Thailand.

Cotton futures on the ICE averaged 82.6 cents per pound in FY2023, a significant decline of 28.3 percent from the FY2022 average of 115.2 cents per pound, reflecting weakened global textile demand and improved U.S. crop conditions.

SECTION 3: METALS

3.1 Precious Metals

Gold futures on the COMEX division of NYMEX averaged $1,941.70 per troy ounce in FY2023, an increase of 8.2 percent from the FY2022 average of $1,794.30 per troy ounce. Gold reached an intraday high of $2,081.90 per troy ounce on May 4, 2023, approaching the all-time record set in August 2020. Safe-haven demand driven by banking sector stress following the collapse of Silicon Valley Bank on March 10, 2023, and Signature Bank on March 12, 2023, contributed to the Q2 price surge.

Silver futures averaged $23.47 per troy ounce in FY2023, compared to $22.14 per troy ounce in FY2022, an increase of 6.0 percent.

3.2 Industrial Metals

Copper futures on the COMEX averaged $3.84 per pound in FY2023, a decline of 5.4 percent from the FY2022 average of $4.06 per pound. Weaker-than-anticipated Chinese economic recovery following the removal of COVID-19 restrictions in December 2022 suppressed demand expectations throughout the fiscal year.

Aluminum on the London Metal Exchange (LME) averaged $2,247 per metric ton in FY2023, down 23.8 percent from $2,950 per metric ton in FY2022. Reduced European industrial activity and lower energy costs for aluminum smelting contributed to the price decline.

Steel (hot-rolled coil) domestic U.S. prices averaged $1,023 per short ton in FY2023, compared to $1,412 per short ton in FY2022, a decline of 27.5 percent.

SECTION 4: LIVESTOCK AND DAIRY

Live cattle futures on the CME averaged $1.7842 per pound in FY2023, an increase of 14.6 percent from $1.5568 per pound in FY2022. This represented the highest annual average on record, driven by historically tight cattle supplies resulting from prolonged drought conditions across the southern Plains states. The U.S. cattle inventory as of January 1, 2023, stood at 89.3 million head, the lowest since 1962.

Lean hog futures averaged 77.4 cents per pound in FY2023, compared to 96.2 cents per pound in FY2022, a decline of 19.5 percent.

Class III milk futures averaged $16.42 per hundredweight in FY2023, down 28.3 percent from $22.90 per hundredweight in FY2022, reflecting increased domestic milk production and reduced export demand.

SECTION 5: PRODUCER PRICE INDEX — COMMODITY COMPONENTS

The BLS Producer Price Index for finished goods declined 2.1 percent on a year-over-year basis in September 2023. The energy component of the PPI fell 14.6 percent, while the food component declined 1.8 percent. The PPI for industrial commodities decreased 3.9 percent year-over-year.

Selected PPI Commodity Changes, FY2022 to FY2023:

  Crude petroleum: -22.4 percent
  Natural gas: -58.1 percent
  Corn: -16.8 percent
  Wheat: -26.3 percent
  Copper scrap: -8.7 percent
  Lumber and wood products: -31.2 percent
  Beef and veal: +9.4 percent

SECTION 6: POLICY IMPLICATIONS AND OUTLOOK

The broad-based decline in commodity prices during FY2023 contributed materially to the deceleration of U.S. consumer price inflation. The Consumer Price Index (CPI) fell from a peak of 9.1 percent year-over-year in June 2022 to 3.7 percent in September 2023. The Federal Reserve's Federal Open Market Committee raised the federal funds rate target to a range of 5.25 to 5.50 percent by July 26, 2023, the highest level since January 2001, which strengthened the U.S. dollar and exerted additional downward pressure on dollar-denominated commodity prices.

Looking ahead to FY2024, the CFTC and BLS project continued moderation in energy prices absent major geopolitical disruptions, while agricultural commodity prices face upside risk from El Niño weather patterns affecting South American and Southeast Asian production regions. Metals markets will remain sensitive to Chinese industrial policy and infrastructure spending decisions.

DATA SOURCES AND METHODOLOGY

Price data was sourced from CME Group daily settlement prices, ICE Futures U.S. daily settlement reports, the U.S. Energy Information Administration (EIA) Weekly Petroleum Status Report, and the BLS Producer Price Index program. All futures prices represent front-month contract settlements unless otherwise noted. Fiscal year averages are calculated as arithmetic means of daily settlement prices for the period October 1 through September 30. Currency conversions use the Federal Reserve H.10 statistical release average annual exchange rates.
