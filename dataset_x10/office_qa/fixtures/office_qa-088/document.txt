EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The Internal Revenue Service, under the direction of Commissioner Daniel Werfel, presents this comprehensive analysis of federal tax revenue collected during Fiscal Year 2023 (October 1, 2022 through September 30, 2023). Total gross tax revenue collected reached $4.68 trillion, representing a decline of 9.3 percent from the record $5.16 trillion collected in Fiscal Year 2022. This contraction reflects normalization following pandemic-era fiscal stimulus effects, reduced capital gains realizations, and a cooling labor market in the second half of the fiscal year. Net revenue after refunds totaled $4.44 trillion.

SECTION 1: OVERVIEW OF TOTAL TAX REVENUE

Fiscal Year 2023 marked the second-highest total tax collection year on record, despite the year-over-year decline. The following table summarizes gross collections by major revenue category:

The sharp decline in the "Other Taxes and Fees" category is primarily attributable to the expiration of the Federal Reserve earnings remittances that inflated FY2022 figures, as well as reduced collections from the Affordable Care Act net investment income tax due to lower investment returns across equity markets.

SECTION 2: INDIVIDUAL INCOME TAX ANALYSIS

Individual income taxes remain the single largest source of federal revenue, accounting for 46.5 percent of total gross collections in FY2023. The $455.3 billion decline from FY2022 is the largest single-year nominal drop in individual income tax receipts since the 2008-2009 financial crisis.

Key contributing factors include:

- Capital gains realizations fell by an estimated 34 percent compared to FY2022, driven by the S&P 500's 19.4 percent decline in calendar year 2022, which reduced taxable gains recognized in early FY2023 filings.

- Wage and salary income grew modestly at 4.2 percent, reflecting continued employment growth offset by slowing wage gains in the second half of calendar year 2022.

- Estimated tax payments declined by $312 billion year-over-year, as high-income filers adjusted quarterly payments downward following investment losses.

- The IRS processed 162.0 million individual income tax returns during FY2023, compared to 164.3 million in FY2022.

- Average refund amounts increased to $2,753 per return, up from $2,201 in FY2022, reflecting over-withholding by employees who anticipated higher income that did not materialize.

Quarterly Breakdown of Individual Income Tax Receipts (FY2023):

Q1 (Oct-Dec 2022):    $487.2 billion
Q2 (Jan-Mar 2023):    $623.4 billion
Q3 (Apr-Jun 2023):    $714.9 billion
Q4 (Jul-Sep 2023):    $351.3 billion

The April-June quarter consistently represents the highest collection period due to the April 18, 2023 filing deadline for calendar year 2022 returns and the June 15 estimated tax payment date.

SECTION 3: PAYROLL TAX REVENUE

Payroll taxes, comprising Federal Insurance Contributions Act (FICA) taxes for Social Security and Medicare and Self-Employment Contributions Act (SECA) taxes, grew by 8.8 percent to $1.614 trillion in FY2023. This category demonstrated the most consistent growth of any major revenue source and now represents 34.5 percent of total gross collections, up from 28.7 percent in FY2022.

Social Security payroll tax receipts totaled $1,143.2 billion, reflecting the increase in the Social Security wage base from $147,000 in 2022 to $160,200 in 2023, a 8.98 percent increase. Medicare payroll tax receipts reached $370.8 billion, with the additional 0.9 percent Medicare surtax on wages above $200,000 (single filers) contributing approximately $18.4 billion.

Self-employment tax collections grew by 6.1 percent to $100.0 billion, consistent with the continued expansion of gig economy participation. The IRS estimates that approximately 27 million taxpayers reported self-employment income in FY2023.

SECTION 4: CORPORATE INCOME TAX ANALYSIS

Corporate income tax collections surged 24.7 percent to $529.9 billion in FY2023, the highest level since FY2015 when adjusted for inflation. This increase is partially attributable to the new 15 percent Corporate Alternative Minimum Tax (CAMT) established under the Inflation Reduction Act of 2022, which became effective for tax years beginning after December 31, 2022.

The CAMT generated an estimated $4.8 billion in its first partial year of collections, below the Joint Committee on Taxation's initial projection of $9.0 billion, as many corporations utilized available credits and deductions to reduce their adjusted financial statement income.

The 1 percent excise tax on corporate stock buybacks, also enacted under the Inflation Reduction Act, generated $8.6 billion in FY2023, its first full year of implementation. The financial services sector accounted for the largest share of buyback tax payments at approximately 31 percent of total collections.

Top corporate tax-paying sectors by estimated contribution:

1. Financial Services and Insurance:    $118.4 billion (22.3% of corporate total)
2. Technology and Communications:       $94.2 billion (17.8%)
3. Healthcare and Pharmaceuticals:      $71.6 billion (13.5%)
4. Energy and Natural Resources:        $63.5 billion (12.0%)
5. Manufacturing:                       $58.8 billion (11.1%)
6. All Other Sectors:                   $123.4 billion (23.3%)

SECTION 5: EXCISE TAXES AND ESTATE AND GIFT TAXES

Federal excise tax collections totaled $86.6 billion in FY2023, a modest decline of 3.5 percent from FY2022. Highway motor fuel taxes remained the largest excise tax category at $36.2 billion, though collections declined 4.1 percent as fuel efficiency improvements and electric vehicle adoption reduced taxable fuel consumption. Tobacco excise taxes continued their long-term structural decline, falling 7.8 percent to $12.1 billion.

Estate and gift tax collections of $34.1 billion reflected the impact of the elevated unified credit exemption of $12.92 million per individual in 2023, up from $12.06 million in 2022. The IRS received 4,157 taxable estate tax returns in FY2023, with an average tax liability of $7.2 million per taxable estate.

SECTION 6: ENFORCEMENT AND COMPLIANCE REVENUE

The IRS collected $63.6 billion through enforcement activities in FY2023, including audits, automated underreporter notices, and collection actions. This represents a 6.2 percent increase from $59.9 billion in FY2022 and reflects the early impact of the $80 billion in additional IRS funding authorized under the Inflation Reduction Act.

The audit rate for individual returns with income above $1 million increased to 1.1 percent in FY2023, up from 0.7 percent in FY2022. The overall individual audit rate remained at 0.38 percent. The IRS Criminal Investigation division initiated 2,676 criminal investigations in FY2023 and obtained 1,986 convictions, with a conviction rate of 90.4 percent.

SECTION 8: OUTLOOK FOR FISCAL YEAR 2024

The Office of Tax Analysis projects FY2024 gross collections of approximately $4.92 trillion, representing a recovery of 5.1 percent from FY2023 levels. Key assumptions underlying this projection include:

- Nominal wage growth of 4.8 percent driving payroll and individual income tax gains
- Partial recovery in capital gains realizations as equity markets stabilized in calendar year 2023
- Full-year CAMT collections approaching the $9.0 billion annual projection
- Continued growth in enforcement revenue as IRS staffing investments mature
