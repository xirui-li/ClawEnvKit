EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The Commonwealth of Pennsylvania recorded total General Fund revenues of $11.84 billion in the third quarter of fiscal year 2023-2024 (Q3 FY2023-24), representing an increase of $1.27 billion, or 12.0 percent, compared to the $10.57 billion collected in the second quarter (Q2 FY2023-24). Total General Fund expenditures for Q3 reached $10.93 billion, up from $10.41 billion in Q2, reflecting a quarter-over-quarter increase of $520 million, or 5.0 percent. The resulting quarterly surplus of $910 million in Q3 compares favorably to the $160 million surplus recorded in Q2, representing a $750 million improvement.

Budget Secretary Uri Monson noted that the Q3 performance reflects stronger-than-anticipated personal income tax collections driven by year-end bonus payments and elevated capital gains realizations, as well as continued growth in sales and use tax receipts tied to consumer spending in the January retail period.

1.2 Personal Income Tax

Personal income tax (PIT) collections of $4.631 billion in Q3 represent the largest single-quarter PIT collection in the Commonwealth's history, surpassing the previous record of $4.418 billion set in Q3 FY2021-22. The 21.5 percent quarter-over-quarter increase is attributable to three primary factors:

- Year-end bonus and deferred compensation payments processed in January 2024, which generated an estimated $340 million in additional withholding compared to the October-December period.
- Estimated tax payments due February 15, 2024, which totaled $612 million, an increase of $88 million over the November 15, 2023 estimated payment deadline.
- Elevated capital gains distributions from mutual funds and investment accounts, which the Department of Revenue estimates contributed approximately $175 million above Q2 levels.

The Commonwealth's flat PIT rate of 3.07 percent remained unchanged during this period.

1.3 Sales and Use Tax

Sales and use tax collections of $3.107 billion in Q3 reflect a 5.5 percent increase over Q2. January 2024 collections were particularly strong at $1.09 billion, driven by post-holiday gift card redemptions and appliance purchases. February collections of $987 million were slightly below the monthly average, consistent with seasonal patterns. March collections of $1.03 billion reflected improving consumer confidence, with the University of Michigan Consumer Sentiment Index averaging 76.9 during the quarter.

1.4 Corporate Net Income Tax

Corporate net income tax (CNIT) collections declined 9.5 percent quarter-over-quarter, from $1.203 billion to $1.089 billion. This decline is consistent with historical patterns, as Q2 typically captures larger estimated payments tied to the September 15 federal deadline cycle. The Department of Revenue notes that the CNIT rate reduction from 8.99 percent to 8.49 percent, effective January 1, 2024, as part of the phased reduction schedule established under Act 53 of 2022, contributed an estimated $62 million to the year-over-year decline in CNIT receipts.

2.2 Human Services

The Department of Human Services (DHS) expenditures of $3.977 billion in Q3 represent the largest expenditure category, comprising 36.4 percent of total General Fund spending. The $136 million increase over Q2 reflects:

- Medical Assistance (Medicaid) payments of $2.614 billion, up $89 million from Q2's $2.525 billion, driven by a 2.1 percent increase in average monthly enrollment, which reached 3.47 million recipients in March 2024.
- Child care subsidy payments increased $31 million to $287 million, reflecting expanded eligibility implemented under Executive Order 2023-08 signed by Governor Josh Shapiro on September 14, 2023.
- Mental health and substance abuse services expenditures rose $16 million to $412 million.

2.3 Transportation

PennDOT expenditures increased $152 million, or 17.1 percent, to $1.043 billion in Q3. This increase reflects the acceleration of capital project spending as weather conditions improved in March 2024, enabling construction activity that had been deferred during the December-January period. Notable projects include the I-95 reconstruction in Philadelphia County ($78 million expended in Q3) and the Route 30 corridor improvements in Lancaster County ($23 million expended in Q3).

2.4 Debt Service

Debt service payments declined $13 million to $621 million in Q3, reflecting the retirement of $450 million in General Obligation bonds that matured on February 1, 2024, partially offset by interest payments on $800 million in new bonds issued November 15, 2023, at a weighted average interest rate of 4.37 percent.

SECTION 3: CASH POSITION AND FUND BALANCE

3.1 General Fund Balance

The General Fund unappropriated balance as of March 31, 2024, stood at $3.84 billion, compared to $2.93 billion as of December 31, 2023, an increase of $910 million. The Commonwealth's Budget Stabilization Reserve Fund (Rainy Day Fund) remained unchanged at $1.83 billion during Q3, as no transfers were authorized by the General Assembly during this period.

3.2 Short-Term Borrowing

The Commonwealth did not issue Tax Anticipation Notes (TANs) during Q3 FY2023-24, consistent with Q2. The last TAN issuance occurred in Q1 FY2022-23, when $500 million was borrowed at a rate of 2.15 percent to manage cash flow timing differences.

SECTION 4: YEAR-TO-DATE PERFORMANCE AND OUTLOOK

Through the first three quarters of FY2023-24 (July 1, 2023 – March 31, 2024), total General Fund revenues of $31.87 billion are running $743 million, or 2.4 percent, above the official estimate established in the Governor's Executive Budget presented February 6, 2024. Total expenditures of $30.96 billion are $218 million, or 0.7 percent, below the budgeted amount for the nine-month period.

The Office of the Budget projects Q4 FY2023-24 revenues of approximately $9.2 billion, which would bring full-year collections to approximately $41.1 billion, compared to the enacted budget estimate of $40.8 billion. Key risks to this projection include potential softening in capital gains realizations if equity markets decline, and uncertainty surrounding federal Medicaid matching rate adjustments expected in June 2024.

SECTION 5: SPECIAL FUNDS SUMMARY

The Motor License Fund received $1.14 billion in Q3, up 3.6 percent from $1.10 billion in Q2, reflecting higher liquid fuels tax collections tied to increased vehicle miles traveled as winter weather subsided. The Lottery Fund transferred $286 million to senior programs in Q3, compared to $271 million in Q2, a 5.5 percent increase driven by strong Powerball and Mega Millions ticket sales following the January 13, 2024 Mega Millions jackpot of $1.13 billion.
