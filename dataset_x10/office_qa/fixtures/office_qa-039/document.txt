EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This report presents comprehensive commodity price statistics for the United States fiscal year ending September 30, 2023. Data was compiled from 47 regulated commodity exchanges, 312 reporting entities, and daily price submissions from 1,840 registered market participants. The Producer Price Index (PPI) for all commodities declined 4.7 percent year-over-year, reversing the 14.2 percent increase recorded in fiscal year 2022. Energy commodities experienced the sharpest corrections, while agricultural products demonstrated relative resilience amid persistent supply chain disruptions.

SECTION 1: ENERGY COMMODITIES

1.1 Crude Oil

West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude oil averaged $77.58 per barrel during fiscal year 2023, representing a decline of 19.3 percent from the fiscal year 2022 average of $96.13 per barrel. The annual low of $66.74 per barrel was recorded on June 12, 2023, while the annual high of $93.68 per barrel occurred on September 27, 2023, driven by OPEC+ production cut announcements.

Brent crude oil, the international benchmark, averaged $82.44 per barrel for the fiscal year, down 17.8 percent from $100.28 per barrel in fiscal year 2022. The spread between Brent and WTI averaged $4.86 per barrel, narrowing from $4.15 in the prior year.

Total U.S. crude oil production averaged 12.9 million barrels per day during the reporting period, a record high surpassing the previous record of 12.8 million barrels per day set in November 2019.

1.2 Natural Gas

Henry Hub natural gas spot prices averaged $2.57 per million British thermal units (MMBtu) in fiscal year 2023, a dramatic decline of 58.4 percent from the $6.18 per MMBtu average recorded in fiscal year 2022. The price collapse was attributed to record domestic production levels, mild winter temperatures across the continental United States, and reduced European demand following energy conservation measures.

The annual low of $1.73 per MMBtu was recorded on March 13, 2023. Storage inventories as of September 30, 2023 stood at 3,556 billion cubic feet, approximately 8.4 percent above the five-year average.

1.3 Refined Products

Retail gasoline prices averaged $3.53 per gallon nationally in fiscal year 2023, compared to $4.07 per gallon in fiscal year 2022, a decrease of 13.3 percent. California maintained the highest state average at $4.89 per gallon, while Mississippi recorded the lowest at $3.11 per gallon.

Ultra-low sulfur diesel averaged $4.12 per gallon at the wholesale level, declining 22.6 percent from $5.32 per gallon in the prior fiscal year.

SECTION 2: AGRICULTURAL COMMODITIES

2.1 Grains and Oilseeds

Soft red winter wheat experienced the most significant price decline among major grains, falling 34.0 percent year-over-year. The decline followed record harvests in Australia and Argentina, which partially offset reduced Black Sea region exports resulting from the ongoing conflict in Ukraine. U.S. wheat exports totaled 19.8 million metric tons for the fiscal year, down 6.2 percent from 21.1 million metric tons in fiscal year 2022.

Corn prices declined 19.8 percent, with the September 30, 2023 closing price on the Chicago Board of Trade (CBOT) settling at $4.88 per bushel. U.S. corn production for the 2023 crop year was estimated at 15.1 billion bushels by the USDA National Agricultural Statistics Service, up 10.4 percent from 13.7 billion bushels in 2022.

Soybean prices showed greater resilience, declining only 7.9 percent, supported by strong Chinese import demand. China purchased 97.3 million metric tons of soybeans globally in calendar year 2023, with the United States supplying approximately 24.8 million metric tons of that total.

2.2 Livestock and Meat

Live cattle futures averaged $175.42 per hundredweight (cwt) in fiscal year 2023, an increase of 12.7 percent from $155.65 per cwt in fiscal year 2022. The cattle market was supported by historically tight domestic herd sizes, with the January 1, 2023 cattle inventory reported at 89.3 million head by the USDA, the lowest level since 1962.

Lean hog futures averaged $79.83 per cwt, declining 8.4 percent from $87.15 per cwt in the prior year. Pork export volumes to Japan and South Korea declined 4.1 percent and 2.7 percent, respectively, contributing to domestic price pressure.

2.3 Soft Commodities

Coffee arabica futures on the Intercontinental Exchange (ICE) averaged $1.68 per pound in fiscal year 2023, down 14.3 percent from $1.96 per pound in fiscal year 2022. Brazilian production recovered to an estimated 54.9 million 60-kilogram bags for the 2023 crop year, representing a 4.5 percent increase from the prior year.

Raw sugar (ICE No. 11 contract) averaged 22.4 cents per pound, an increase of 18.5 percent from 18.9 cents per pound in fiscal year 2022. Indian export restrictions implemented in October 2023 contributed to price strength in the final weeks of the reporting period.

Cotton (ICE No. 2 contract) averaged 82.6 cents per pound, declining 22.8 percent from 107.0 cents per pound in fiscal year 2022, as global textile demand softened amid economic slowdowns in major consuming nations.

SECTION 3: METALS AND MINERALS

3.1 Precious Metals

Gold futures on the COMEX division of the New York Mercantile Exchange (NYMEX) averaged $1,941.80 per troy ounce in fiscal year 2023, an increase of 7.8 percent from $1,800.30 per troy ounce in fiscal year 2022. Gold reached an annual high of $2,063.40 per troy ounce on May 4, 2023, approaching but not exceeding the all-time intraday high of $2,089.20 set on August 7, 2020.

Silver averaged $23.47 per troy ounce, up 3.2 percent from $22.74 in the prior year. The gold-to-silver ratio averaged 82.7:1 during the fiscal year, compared to 79.2:1 in fiscal year 2022.

Nickel experienced the most severe price decline among base metals, falling 36.1 percent year-over-year. The London Metal Exchange (LME) nickel market continued to face structural challenges following the unprecedented short squeeze of March 2022, during which prices briefly exceeded $100,000 per metric ton before trading was suspended. Increased Indonesian nickel production, which grew 26.3 percent in calendar year 2023, contributed substantially to the price decline.

Copper, often cited as an economic indicator due to its broad industrial applications, declined 11.2 percent. Chilean copper production, representing approximately 27 percent of global supply, increased 3.1 percent to 5.7 million metric tons in calendar year 2023.

SECTION 4: PRODUCER PRICE INDEX ANALYSIS

The BLS Producer Price Index for all commodities registered 116.4 in September 2023 (1982=100 baseline), compared to 122.2 in September 2022, representing a 4.7 percent annual decline. This marks the first year-over-year decrease in the all-commodities PPI since fiscal year 2015, when the index fell 6.3 percent.

Key PPI sub-index performance for fiscal year 2023:

- Energy commodities PPI: declined 24.8 percent year-over-year
- Farm products PPI: declined 8.3 percent year-over-year
- Processed foods and feeds PPI: declined 3.1 percent year-over-year
- Metals and metal products PPI: declined 6.7 percent year-over-year
- Chemical products PPI: declined 9.4 percent year-over-year

SECTION 5: MARKET OUTLOOK AND RISK FACTORS

The CFTC's Market Intelligence Division identifies the following principal risk factors for commodity price volatility in fiscal year 2024:

1. Geopolitical disruptions: Continued conflict in the Black Sea region affecting grain and energy export corridors, with Ukrainian grain corridor shipments totaling 32.9 million metric tons since the July 2022 agreement before its termination in July 2023.

2. El Niño weather pattern: The National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA) declared a moderate-to-strong El Niño event in June 2023, with potential adverse impacts on Southeast Asian palm oil production and South American soybean yields in the 2023-2024 growing season.

3. Federal Reserve monetary policy: Interest rate decisions directly influence commodity financing costs and the U.S. dollar index, which averaged 103.4 during fiscal year 2023, up 0.8 percent from 102.6 in fiscal year 2022.

4. Chinese economic recovery: China's GDP growth of 5.2 percent in calendar year 2023, while meeting official targets, fell below historical norms and tempered commodity demand projections.

METHODOLOGY AND DATA SOURCES

Price data was sourced from the following primary exchanges: Chicago Board of Trade (CBOT), Chicago Mercantile Exchange (CME), New York Mercantile Exchange (NYMEX), Intercontinental Exchange (ICE), and London Metal Exchange (LME). Daily settlement prices were used for futures contract calculations. Spot prices were obtained from 312 registered reporting entities submitting data under CFTC Regulation 18.05. All prices are denominated in U.S. dollars unless otherwise noted. Fiscal year references correspond to the U.S. government fiscal year, October 1 through September 30.

For questions regarding this report, contact the CFTC Office of Market Intelligence at (202) 418-5000 or the BLS Division of Industrial Prices and Price Indexes at (202) 691-7705.
