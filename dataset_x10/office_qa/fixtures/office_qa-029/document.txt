EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This report presents comprehensive commodity price statistics for the United States fiscal year ending September 30, 2023. Data was compiled from the CFTC's market surveillance division, the BLS Producer Price Index program, the U.S. Energy Information Administration (EIA), and the U.S. Department of Agriculture (USDA) National Agricultural Statistics Service. The report covers energy commodities, agricultural products, metals, and livestock, providing year-over-year comparisons, quarterly breakdowns, and forward-looking indicators.

Overall, commodity markets in FY2023 experienced significant normalization following the extreme volatility of FY2022, which was driven by the Russian invasion of Ukraine and post-pandemic supply chain disruptions. The aggregate CFTC Commodity Price Index declined 18.4 percent from FY2022 levels, though prices in most categories remained above their five-year pre-pandemic averages.

SECTION 1: ENERGY COMMODITIES

1.1 Crude Oil

West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude oil averaged $77.58 per barrel across FY2023, representing a decline of 19.3 percent from the FY2022 average of $96.14 per barrel. The annual high of $93.68 per barrel was recorded on September 27, 2023, driven by OPEC+ production cuts announced by Saudi Arabia and Russia in June 2023. The annual low of $66.74 per barrel occurred on March 17, 2023, amid banking sector instability following the collapse of Silicon Valley Bank.

Quarterly WTI Crude Oil Averages (FY2023):
  Q1 (Oct–Dec 2022): $85.42 per barrel
  Q2 (Jan–Mar 2023): $75.91 per barrel
  Q3 (Apr–Jun 2023): $73.84 per barrel
  Q4 (Jul–Sep 2023): $82.26 per barrel

Brent crude, the international benchmark, averaged $82.17 per barrel for the fiscal year, maintaining its traditional premium over WTI of approximately $4.59 per barrel.

1.2 Natural Gas

Henry Hub natural gas spot prices averaged $2.74 per million British thermal units (MMBtu) in FY2023, a dramatic decline of 57.8 percent from the FY2022 average of $6.49 per MMBtu. This represented the lowest annual average since FY2020. The price collapse was attributed to record U.S. production levels reaching 103.8 billion cubic feet per day in August 2023, combined with mild winter temperatures across the Northeast and Midwest during the first quarter.

1.3 Gasoline and Distillates

Retail regular-grade gasoline averaged $3.53 per gallon nationally in FY2023, down from $3.99 per gallon in FY2022. Ultra-low sulfur diesel averaged $4.12 per gallon, declining 22.6 percent year-over-year. California maintained the highest state average at $4.89 per gallon for regular gasoline, while Mississippi recorded the lowest at $3.11 per gallon.

SECTION 2: AGRICULTURAL COMMODITIES

2.1 Grains

Corn futures on the Chicago Board of Trade (CBOT) averaged $5.87 per bushel in FY2023, down 9.2 percent from $6.46 per bushel in FY2022. The USDA projected the 2023 corn harvest at 15.1 billion bushels, a 10.2 percent increase over the drought-reduced 2022 harvest of 13.7 billion bushels.

Wheat prices experienced the most significant correction among grain commodities. CBOT soft red winter wheat averaged $6.43 per bushel in FY2023, falling 27.1 percent from the FY2022 average of $8.82 per bushel. The FY2022 peak of $13.64 per bushel, reached on March 7, 2022, following Russia's invasion of Ukraine, remained the highest recorded price in CBOT wheat trading history.

Soybean futures averaged $13.71 per bushel in FY2023, a modest decline of 3.4 percent from $14.19 per bushel in FY2022. Strong export demand from China, which imported 98.6 million metric tons of soybeans globally in calendar year 2023, provided price support.

2.2 Soft Commodities

Coffee arabica futures on the Intercontinental Exchange (ICE) averaged $1.68 per pound in FY2023, down 14.3 percent from $1.96 per pound in FY2022. Brazil's 2023 coffee harvest reached an estimated 54.9 million 60-kilogram bags, a record output that contributed to price softening.

Raw sugar futures (ICE No. 11 contract) averaged 23.4 cents per pound in FY2023, representing a 14.7 percent increase from 20.4 cents per pound in FY2022. This marked the highest annual average for sugar since 2011. Drought conditions in India and Thailand, the world's second and third largest sugar exporters respectively, constrained global supply.

Cotton futures (ICE No. 2 contract) averaged 82.6 cents per pound, declining 22.8 percent from 107.0 cents per pound in FY2022.

SECTION 3: METALS

3.1 Precious Metals

Gold futures on the COMEX division of the New York Mercantile Exchange (NYMEX) averaged $1,941.70 per troy ounce in FY2023, an increase of 8.2 percent from $1,794.50 per troy ounce in FY2022. Gold reached an annual high of $2,009.40 per troy ounce on May 4, 2023, as investors sought safe-haven assets amid regional banking sector stress. The Federal Reserve's eleven consecutive interest rate increases between March 2022 and July 2023, bringing the federal funds rate to a target range of 5.25 to 5.50 percent, created headwinds for gold but were offset by central bank purchasing, which reached a record 1,037 metric tons globally in calendar year 2022 and remained elevated at an estimated 800 metric tons through the first three quarters of calendar year 2023.

Silver futures averaged $23.42 per troy ounce in FY2023, essentially flat compared to $23.56 per troy ounce in FY2022, a decline of just 0.6 percent.

3.2 Industrial Metals

Copper futures on the COMEX averaged $3.84 per pound in FY2023, down 5.4 percent from $4.06 per pound in FY2022. Slower-than-anticipated economic recovery in China, which accounts for approximately 54 percent of global copper consumption, weighed on prices throughout the fiscal year.

Aluminum on the London Metal Exchange (LME) averaged $2,247 per metric ton in FY2023, a decline of 16.8 percent from $2,701 per metric ton in FY2022. Reduced European industrial demand, driven by high energy costs affecting aluminum smelting operations, contributed to the price decline.

3.3 Steel

Hot-rolled coil steel futures averaged $1,012 per short ton in FY2023, down 28.4 percent from $1,414 per short ton in FY2022. U.S. steel mill capacity utilization averaged 74.3 percent for the fiscal year, according to the American Iron and Steel Institute.

SECTION 4: LIVESTOCK AND DAIRY

Live cattle futures on the Chicago Mercantile Exchange (CME) averaged $1.73 per pound in FY2023, an increase of 18.5 percent from $1.46 per pound in FY2022. This represented the highest annual average on record for live cattle futures. Tight cattle supplies, with the U.S. cattle herd declining to 87.2 million head as of January 1, 2023, the smallest herd since 1962, drove prices higher throughout the fiscal year.

Lean hog futures averaged 74.8 cents per pound, declining 12.6 percent from 85.6 cents per pound in FY2022.

Class III milk futures averaged $17.08 per hundredweight in FY2023, down 22.3 percent from $21.98 per hundredweight in FY2022, reflecting improved domestic milk production and softer export demand.

SECTION 5: PRODUCER PRICE INDEX — COMMODITY COMPONENTS

The BLS Producer Price Index for all commodities registered a 12-month change of negative 4.8 percent for September 2023, compared to a 12-month increase of 11.7 percent recorded in September 2022. This represented the most significant year-over-year deceleration in the commodity PPI since the index's restructuring in 2014.

Selected PPI Commodity Category Changes (September 2022 to September 2023):
  Crude petroleum: -22.1 percent
  Natural gas: -61.4 percent
  Grains: -14.7 percent
  Metals and metal products: -8.3 percent
  Livestock: +11.2 percent
  Dairy products: -19.6 percent

SECTION 6: MARKET OUTLOOK AND RISK FACTORS

The CFTC's Office of the Chief Economist identifies the following principal risk factors for commodity markets entering FY2024:

  - Continued OPEC+ production management, with the Saudi-Russian voluntary cut of 1.3 million barrels per day extended through December 2023
  - El Niño weather patterns affecting agricultural production in Southeast Asia, Australia, and South America, with the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA) assigning a 71 percent probability of El Niño conditions persisting through February 2024
  - Geopolitical risk premium in energy markets following the outbreak of conflict in Israel and Gaza on October 7, 2023
  - Federal Reserve monetary policy trajectory and its implications for the U.S. dollar, which maintains an inverse correlation of approximately negative 0.68 with the Bloomberg Commodity Index over rolling 12-month periods
  - Chinese economic stimulus measures and their potential to accelerate industrial metals demand
