FOREWORD

This report presents comprehensive commodity price statistics for the United States fiscal year ending September 30, 2023. Data were compiled by the Economic Research Service in coordination with the National Agricultural Statistics Service, the Bureau of Labor Statistics, and the U.S. Energy Information Administration. All price figures are expressed in nominal U.S. dollars unless otherwise noted. Inflation-adjusted comparisons use the Producer Price Index baseline of January 2020 = 100.

SECTION 1: EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Commodity markets in fiscal year 2023 experienced significant volatility driven by persistent supply chain disruptions, geopolitical tensions stemming from the ongoing conflict in Ukraine, and shifting monetary policy across major economies. The aggregate Commodity Price Index compiled by the ERS recorded an average value of 187.4 for FY2023, representing a 6.2 percent decline from the FY2022 peak of 199.8, though remaining 34.7 percent above the pre-pandemic FY2019 baseline of 139.1.

Agricultural commodities showed mixed performance, with grain prices retreating from historic highs while livestock and dairy sectors demonstrated resilience. Energy commodities experienced the most pronounced correction, with West Texas Intermediate crude oil averaging $78.26 per barrel for the fiscal year, down 18.4 percent from the FY2022 average of $95.88 per barrel. Metals markets were similarly subdued, with copper prices averaging $3.84 per pound compared to $4.21 per pound in FY2022.

SECTION 2: AGRICULTURAL COMMODITY PRICES

2.1 Grain and Oilseed Markets

Corn prices averaged $5.62 per bushel for FY2023, a decrease of 11.3 percent from the FY2022 average of $6.34 per bushel. The decline reflected improved domestic production, with the USDA estimating total U.S. corn production at 15.34 billion bushels for the 2023 crop year, up 9.8 percent from 13.97 billion bushels in 2022. Prices reached their annual low of $4.71 per bushel in August 2023 as harvest pressure mounted.

Soybean prices averaged $13.71 per bushel in FY2023, down 4.6 percent from $14.37 per bushel in FY2022. Brazilian production expansion, with Brazil harvesting a record 162.4 million metric tons in the 2022-23 marketing year, contributed to global supply pressure. U.S. soybean exports totaled 1.76 billion bushels, valued at approximately $24.1 billion.

Wheat markets remained elevated relative to historical norms. Hard Red Winter wheat at the Kansas City Board of Trade averaged $8.14 per bushel for FY2023, compared to $9.47 per bushel in FY2022, representing a 14.0 percent decline. Despite the correction, the FY2023 average remained 28.6 percent above the five-year pre-pandemic average of $6.33 per bushel recorded from FY2015 through FY2019.

2.2 Livestock and Poultry

The livestock sector demonstrated notable strength in FY2023. Choice beef cattle prices averaged $183.42 per hundredweight (cwt), an increase of 12.7 percent from $162.72 per cwt in FY2022. Tight cattle supplies, with the January 2023 cattle inventory at 89.3 million head representing the lowest level since 1962, supported elevated prices throughout the fiscal year.

Hog prices averaged $62.18 per cwt in FY2023, down 8.3 percent from $67.81 per cwt in FY2022, as pork production remained robust at an estimated 27.8 billion pounds. Broiler chicken prices averaged 98.4 cents per pound, up 3.1 percent from 95.4 cents per pound in FY2022, with avian influenza outbreaks affecting approximately 58.8 million birds between January 2022 and September 2023 continuing to influence market dynamics.

2.3 Dairy Products

Class III milk prices, which determine payments for milk used in cheese production, averaged $17.84 per hundredweight in FY2023, a significant decline of 22.6 percent from the FY2022 average of $23.05 per hundredweight. The correction followed record-high cheese prices in 2022 and reflected improved milk production efficiency and moderating export demand. California, the nation's leading dairy state, reported average farm milk prices of $19.12 per cwt for FY2023.

SECTION 3: ENERGY COMMODITY PRICES

3.1 Petroleum Products

West Texas Intermediate crude oil, the U.S. benchmark, averaged $78.26 per barrel for FY2023, with prices ranging from a low of $66.74 per barrel on March 17, 2023, to a high of $93.68 per barrel on September 27, 2023. The September spike reflected production cuts announced by OPEC+ member nations, including Saudi Arabia's unilateral reduction of 1.0 million barrels per day extended through December 2023.

Regular-grade gasoline retail prices averaged $3.53 per gallon nationally in FY2023, compared to $3.98 per gallon in FY2022, a decline of 11.3 percent. The Energy Information Administration reported that the U.S. Strategic Petroleum Reserve stood at 351.3 million barrels as of September 30, 2023, following releases totaling approximately 180 million barrels authorized by the Biden administration between November 2021 and December 2022.

Diesel fuel averaged $4.01 per gallon at retail in FY2023, down from $4.83 per gallon in FY2022. Diesel prices carry particular significance for agricultural producers, as the USDA estimates that fuel represents approximately 14.3 percent of total farm production expenses.

3.2 Natural Gas

Henry Hub natural gas spot prices averaged $2.67 per million British thermal units (MMBtu) in FY2023, a dramatic decline of 56.8 percent from the FY2022 average of $6.18 per MMBtu. Mild winter temperatures across much of the continental United States, combined with record domestic production reaching 103.8 billion cubic feet per day in July 2023, contributed to the price collapse. Prices reached a multi-year low of $1.99 per MMBtu on April 3, 2023.

SECTION 4: METALS AND MINERALS

4.1 Base Metals

Copper prices on the COMEX exchange averaged $3.84 per pound in FY2023, down 8.8 percent from $4.21 per pound in FY2022. Demand concerns related to slower-than-expected economic recovery in China, which accounts for approximately 54 percent of global copper consumption, weighed on prices. The London Metal Exchange copper inventory stood at 178,450 metric tons as of September 29, 2023.

Aluminum averaged $1.07 per pound in FY2023, compared to $1.40 per pound in FY2022, a decline of 23.6 percent. Reduced European smelter curtailments as energy prices moderated contributed to improved supply availability.

4.2 Precious Metals

Gold demonstrated relative stability, averaging $1,943.16 per troy ounce in FY2023, an increase of 5.8 percent from $1,836.40 per troy ounce in FY2022. Safe-haven demand amid banking sector stress in March 2023, which included the failures of Silicon Valley Bank on March 10, 2023, and Signature Bank on March 12, 2023, supported gold prices. The metal reached an intraday record of $2,067.80 per troy ounce on May 4, 2023.

Silver averaged $23.41 per troy ounce in FY2023, compared to $22.18 per troy ounce in FY2022, an increase of 5.5 percent.

SECTION 5: FERTILIZER AND INPUT COSTS

Fertilizer prices, which had reached historic highs in FY2022 following supply disruptions related to sanctions on Russian and Belarusian exports, moderated substantially in FY2023. Urea (44-0-0) averaged $437 per ton at Cornbelt locations in FY2023, down 47.3 percent from the FY2022 average of $829 per ton. Diammonium phosphate (DAP) averaged $521 per ton, a decline of 38.9 percent from $852 per ton in FY2022. Anhydrous ammonia, the most nitrogen-dense fertilizer, averaged $812 per ton in FY2023 compared to $1,487 per ton in FY2022, representing a 45.4 percent reduction.

Despite these corrections, all three fertilizer products remained above their five-year pre-pandemic averages. Urea's FY2023 average exceeded the FY2015-FY2019 average of $312 per ton by 40.1 percent.

SECTION 6: OUTLOOK AND POLICY CONSIDERATIONS

The USDA Economic Research Service projects continued normalization of commodity prices in FY2024, contingent upon resolution of geopolitical uncertainties and continued expansion of global agricultural production. The Federal Reserve's monetary policy trajectory, with the federal funds rate maintained at 5.25-5.50 percent as of September 2023, will continue to influence commodity markets through its effects on the U.S. dollar and global demand.

Farm income projections for calendar year 2023, as published in the USDA's Farm Income and Wealth Statistics release of September 5, 2023, estimate net farm income at $141.3 billion, down 15.9 percent from the record $167.9 billion recorded in 2022 but remaining the third-highest level in inflation-adjusted terms since 1973.
