DEPARTMENT OF THE TREASURY
OFFICE OF FISCAL ANALYSIS

ANNUAL TAX REVENUE ANALYSIS REPORT
Fiscal Year 2023 (October 1, 2022 – September 30, 2023)

Published: December 14, 2023
Report Reference: OFA-TRA-2023-07

─────────────────────────────────────────────

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Total federal tax revenue for Fiscal Year 2023 reached $4.44 trillion, representing a decline of $457 billion, or approximately 9.3 percent, from the record $4.90 trillion collected in Fiscal Year 2022. This contraction marks the first year-over-year decrease in federal tax receipts since Fiscal Year 2020, when revenues fell during the initial phase of the COVID-19 pandemic. Despite the overall decline, collections remained $612 billion above the pre-pandemic baseline established in Fiscal Year 2019, when total receipts stood at $3.46 trillion.

The decline was driven primarily by a sharp reduction in individual income tax withholding and capital gains realizations, partially offset by continued strength in payroll tax collections tied to robust labor market conditions. Corporate income tax receipts also declined modestly from their elevated FY2022 levels but remained historically high.

This report was prepared by the Office of Fiscal Analysis under the direction of Deputy Assistant Secretary for Tax Analysis Dr. Margaret Holloway and reflects data compiled through November 30, 2023.

─────────────────────────────────────────────

SECTION 1: TOTAL REVENUE OVERVIEW

Federal revenues by major category for Fiscal Year 2023 were as follows:

Revenue Source | FY2023 Receipts | FY2022 Receipts | Change | % Change
Individual Income Taxes | $2.18 trillion | $2.63 trillion | -$450B | -17.1%
Payroll Taxes | $1.61 trillion | $1.48 trillion | +$130B | +8.8%
Corporate Income Taxes | $0.47 trillion | $0.43 trillion | +$40B | +9.3%
Excise Taxes | $0.09 trillion | $0.10 trillion | -$10B | -10.0%
Estate and Gift Taxes | $0.034 trillion | $0.033 trillion | +$1B | +3.0%
Customs Duties | $0.073 trillion | $0.099 trillion | -$26B | -26.3%
Miscellaneous Receipts | $0.063 trillion | $0.135 trillion | -$72B | -53.3%

The most significant driver of the overall revenue decline was the $450 billion drop in individual income tax receipts, which fell from $2.63 trillion in FY2022 to $2.18 trillion in FY2023. This reduction reflects two primary factors: a substantial decrease in capital gains realizations as equity markets corrected following the 2021 bull market peak, and a reduction in non-withheld income tax payments, which fell by approximately 32 percent year-over-year.

─────────────────────────────────────────────

SECTION 2: INDIVIDUAL INCOME TAX ANALYSIS

Individual income taxes remain the single largest source of federal revenue, accounting for 49.1 percent of total receipts in FY2023, down from 53.7 percent in FY2022.

Key findings within this category include:

- Withheld income taxes totaled $1.97 trillion in FY2023, a modest decline of 2.4 percent from $2.02 trillion in FY2022, consistent with wage growth of approximately 4.6 percent being partially offset by reduced bonus payments in the financial and technology sectors.

- Non-withheld income taxes, which include estimated quarterly payments and final settlement payments, fell to $0.21 trillion from $0.61 trillion in FY2022, a decline of 65.6 percent. This category is highly sensitive to capital gains activity and reflects the significant market downturn experienced between January and December 2022, during which the S&P 500 declined approximately 19.4 percent.

- Refunds issued to individual taxpayers totaled $0.37 trillion in FY2023, compared to $0.41 trillion in FY2022, a decrease of approximately 9.8 percent. The average refund amount for tax year 2022 returns was $2,903, compared to $3,176 for tax year 2021 returns, reflecting the expiration of expanded Child Tax Credit provisions.

- The top 1 percent of income earners, defined as those with adjusted gross income above $548,336 based on the most recently available Statistics of Income data, accounted for an estimated 42.3 percent of all individual income tax liability.

─────────────────────────────────────────────

SECTION 3: PAYROLL TAX COLLECTIONS

Payroll taxes, which fund Social Security and Medicare programs, were the single bright spot in FY2023 revenue performance, rising to $1.61 trillion from $1.48 trillion in FY2022, an increase of $130 billion or 8.8 percent.

This growth reflects:

- Total nonfarm payroll employment averaged 155.8 million jobs per month during FY2023, compared to 151.2 million in FY2022, representing an average monthly gain of approximately 232,000 positions.

- Average hourly earnings for private-sector workers rose 4.3 percent year-over-year during the fiscal year, contributing to higher taxable wage bases.

- The Social Security taxable wage base increased from $147,000 in calendar year 2022 to $160,200 in calendar year 2023, a 9.0 percent increase, which expanded the payroll tax base for higher-earning workers.

- Medicare Hospital Insurance tax collections, which have no wage cap and include the 0.9 percent Additional Medicare Tax on earnings above $200,000 for single filers, rose to $0.38 trillion, up 7.6 percent from $0.353 trillion in FY2022.

─────────────────────────────────────────────

SECTION 4: CORPORATE INCOME TAX RECEIPTS

Corporate income tax collections totaled $470 billion in FY2023, an increase of $40 billion or 9.3 percent from $430 billion in FY2022. This result was somewhat unexpected given the broader economic slowdown and reflects the lagged nature of corporate tax payments relative to reported earnings.

Notable factors include:

- The 15 percent Corporate Alternative Minimum Tax (CAMT), enacted as part of the Inflation Reduction Act of 2022 and effective for tax years beginning after December 31, 2022, contributed an estimated $9.7 billion in additional corporate receipts during FY2023, below the initial Joint Committee on Taxation estimate of $14.8 billion for the first year of implementation.

- The financial sector, including commercial banks and insurance companies, contributed approximately $87 billion in corporate income taxes, representing 18.5 percent of total corporate receipts.

- S-corporation and partnership income, which flows through to individual returns rather than corporate returns, is not captured in this category but contributed significantly to the decline in individual non-withheld payments.

- The effective corporate tax rate across all C-corporations filing returns is estimated at 18.4 percent for tax year 2022, compared to 17.9 percent for tax year 2021, based on preliminary Statistics of Income data.

─────────────────────────────────────────────

SECTION 5: CUSTOMS DUTIES AND EXCISE TAXES

Customs duties declined sharply to $73 billion in FY2023 from $99 billion in FY2022, a reduction of $26 billion or 26.3 percent. This decline reflects a normalization of import volumes following the post-pandemic surge in goods consumption, as well as a shift in consumer spending toward services. The value of goods imports subject to tariff declined by an estimated 14.2 percent in FY2023.

Excise tax collections totaled $90 billion, down from $100 billion in FY2022. The largest components were:

- Highway and transportation excise taxes: $42 billion
- Tobacco excise taxes: $12 billion
- Alcohol excise taxes: $10 billion
- Air transportation taxes: $16 billion
- Other excise taxes: $10 billion

─────────────────────────────────────────────

SECTION 6: REVENUE AS A SHARE OF GDP

Federal revenues as a percentage of gross domestic product declined to 16.5 percent in FY2023, compared to 19.6 percent in FY2022, which had been the highest revenue-to-GDP ratio recorded since 2000. The FY2023 ratio remains above the 40-year historical average of approximately 17.4 percent when measured against the pre-pandemic trend, though it falls below that average in nominal terms.

Nominal GDP for FY2023 is estimated at approximately $26.9 trillion, based on Bureau of Economic Analysis data through the third quarter of calendar year 2023.

─────────────────────────────────────────────

SECTION 7: OUTLOOK FOR FISCAL YEAR 2024

The Congressional Budget Office's August 2023 baseline projects federal revenues of $4.71 trillion for Fiscal Year 2024, representing a recovery of approximately $270 billion or 6.1 percent from FY2023 levels. This projection assumes:

- Continued labor market strength with unemployment remaining below 4.5 percent through September 2024
- Modest recovery in capital gains realizations as equity markets stabilize
- Full-year impact of the Corporate Alternative Minimum Tax
- No significant legislative changes to the tax code

The Office of Fiscal Analysis notes that revenue projections carry substantial uncertainty, particularly with respect to capital gains realizations, which can vary by hundreds of billions of dollars depending on market conditions and taxpayer behavior.

─────────────────────────────────────────────

SECTION 8: METHODOLOGY AND DATA SOURCES

Revenue data in this report are drawn from the Monthly Treasury Statement published by the Bureau of the Fiscal Service, supplemented by data from the Internal Revenue Service Statistics of Income program. Employment and wage data are sourced from the Bureau of Labor Statistics Current Employment Statistics survey. GDP estimates are derived from Bureau of Economic Analysis National Income and Product Accounts data. All figures are presented on a fiscal year basis unless otherwise noted. Minor discrepancies between totals and component sums are due to rounding.
