EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This report presents comprehensive commodity price statistics for the United States fiscal year ending September 30, 2023. Data was compiled from the CFTC's market surveillance division, the BLS Producer Price Index program, the U.S. Energy Information Administration (EIA), and the U.S. Department of Agriculture (USDA) National Agricultural Statistics Service. The findings reflect significant volatility across energy, agricultural, and metals markets during the reporting period, with notable divergence from the elevated price environment observed in fiscal year 2022.

Overall, the Producer Price Index for commodities declined 4.7 percent on a year-over-year basis, the largest annual decline since fiscal year 2015, when the index fell 6.2 percent. Despite this aggregate decline, select commodities including cocoa, live cattle, and orange juice futures recorded multi-decade price highs during the reporting period.

SECTION 1: ENERGY COMMODITIES

1.1 Crude Oil

West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude oil averaged $77.58 per barrel during fiscal year 2023, representing a decline of 18.3 percent from the fiscal year 2022 average of $94.95 per barrel. The annual average nonetheless remained above the ten-year historical mean of $68.42 per barrel (fiscal years 2013–2022).

WTI crude reached its fiscal year high of $93.68 per barrel on September 27, 2023, driven by production cuts announced by the OPEC+ alliance in June 2023, which reduced collective output by 1.16 million barrels per day. The fiscal year low of $66.74 per barrel was recorded on March 17, 2023, coinciding with financial sector instability following the collapse of Silicon Valley Bank.

Brent crude oil, the international benchmark, averaged $82.17 per barrel for the fiscal year, a decline of 17.1 percent from the prior year average of $99.12 per barrel.

1.2 Natural Gas

Henry Hub natural gas spot prices averaged $2.67 per million British thermal units (MMBtu) in fiscal year 2023, a dramatic decline of 58.4 percent from the fiscal year 2022 average of $6.42 per MMBtu. This represents the lowest annual average since fiscal year 2020, when prices averaged $2.13 per MMBtu.

The fiscal year high of $3.51 per MMBtu was recorded on October 5, 2022. Prices fell to a fiscal year low of $1.97 per MMBtu on April 19, 2023, reflecting elevated storage inventories that stood 22 percent above the five-year seasonal average at that time.

1.3 Gasoline and Distillates

Retail regular-grade gasoline averaged $3.53 per gallon nationally during fiscal year 2023, compared to $4.07 per gallon in fiscal year 2022, a decrease of 13.3 percent. Ultra-low sulfur diesel averaged $4.12 per gallon, down 21.6 percent from the prior year average of $5.25 per gallon.

Chicago Board of Trade (CBOT) wheat prices recorded the most significant annual decline among major grains, falling 32.3 percent year-over-year. This decline followed the extraordinary price spike of fiscal year 2022, which was driven by Russia's invasion of Ukraine in February 2022 and the subsequent disruption of Black Sea grain exports. The resumption of partial export flows under the Black Sea Grain Initiative, which remained in effect through July 17, 2023, contributed to price normalization.

Corn ending stocks for the 2022–2023 marketing year were estimated at 1.417 billion bushels by the USDA, up from 1.377 billion bushels the prior year. U.S. corn production for 2023 was estimated at 15.342 billion bushels, the third-largest crop on record.

2.2 Soft Commodities

Cocoa futures on the Intercontinental Exchange (ICE) averaged $3,247 per metric ton during fiscal year 2023, rising 28.4 percent from the prior year average of $2,528 per metric ton. Prices accelerated sharply in the final quarter of the fiscal year, reaching $3,891 per metric ton on September 29, 2023, the highest settlement price since November 1978. The price surge was attributed to consecutive years of crop shortfalls in Côte d'Ivoire and Ghana, which together account for approximately 60 percent of global cocoa production.

Orange juice futures (FCOJ-A contract, ICE) averaged $2.47 per pound during fiscal year 2023, an increase of 61.4 percent from the prior year average of $1.53 per pound. Florida's orange crop for the 2022–2023 season was estimated at 16.0 million boxes by the USDA, the lowest harvest since the 1936–1937 season, due to the combined effects of citrus greening disease and damage from Hurricane Ian, which made landfall on September 28, 2022.

Coffee (arabica, ICE) averaged $1.71 per pound, down 19.3 percent from $2.12 per pound in fiscal year 2022. Cotton averaged 82.4 cents per pound, a decline of 24.1 percent from 108.6 cents per pound in the prior year.

2.3 Livestock and Dairy

Live cattle futures averaged $1.7842 per pound during fiscal year 2023, an increase of 14.8 percent from the prior year average of $1.5541 per pound. Prices reached a record high of $1.9200 per pound on August 4, 2023, reflecting tight cattle supplies resulting from herd liquidation during the 2022 drought across the Southern Plains. The U.S. cattle inventory as of January 1, 2023 stood at 89.3 million head, the lowest since 1962, according to the USDA National Agricultural Statistics Service.

Lean hog futures averaged 78.6 cents per pound, down 11.2 percent from 88.5 cents per pound in fiscal year 2022.

Class III milk futures averaged $16.42 per hundredweight (cwt), a decline of 28.7 percent from the prior year average of $23.03 per cwt.

SECTION 3: METALS AND MINERALS

3.1 Precious Metals

Gold futures on the COMEX division of the New York Mercantile Exchange (NYMEX) averaged $1,941.60 per troy ounce during fiscal year 2023, an increase of 5.8 percent from the prior year average of $1,835.00 per troy ounce. Gold reached a fiscal year high of $2,009.40 per troy ounce on May 4, 2023, as investors sought safe-haven assets amid banking sector concerns and uncertainty regarding Federal Reserve monetary policy.

Silver averaged $23.42 per troy ounce, essentially flat compared to the prior year average of $23.17 per troy ounce, a change of 1.1 percent.

Nickel prices recorded the largest percentage decline among base metals tracked in this report, falling 35.1 percent year-over-year. The decline followed the extraordinary market disruption of March 2022, when nickel prices briefly exceeded $100,000 per metric ton on the London Metal Exchange (LME), triggering a trading halt and subsequent exchange rule changes. Prices normalized throughout fiscal year 2023 as Indonesian supply continued to expand, with Indonesia accounting for an estimated 49 percent of global nickel mine production.

SECTION 4: COMMODITY MARKET TRADING ACTIVITY

Total open interest across CFTC-regulated commodity futures and options markets averaged 14.8 million contracts during fiscal year 2023, compared to 15.3 million contracts in fiscal year 2022, a decline of 3.3 percent. The energy sector accounted for 38.2 percent of total open interest, followed by agricultural commodities at 29.7 percent, financial commodities at 22.4 percent, and metals at 9.7 percent.

The CFTC's Commitments of Traders (COT) reports indicated that managed money participants held net short positions in natural gas futures for 22 consecutive weeks between January and June 2023, the longest sustained net short position recorded in that category since the COT data series began in 2006.

SECTION 5: PRODUCER PRICE INDEX — COMMODITY COMPONENTS

The BLS Producer Price Index (PPI) for all commodities stood at 228.4 (1982=100) as of September 2023, compared to 239.6 in September 2022, representing a year-over-year decline of 4.7 percent. The index peaked at 254.8 in June 2022, which represented the highest reading in the series' history.

Key PPI sub-index movements for fiscal year 2023:
- Crude petroleum: -22.4 percent
- Natural gas: -57.8 percent
- Grains: -18.3 percent
- Livestock: +9.6 percent
- Metals and metal products: -8.1 percent
- Processed foods and feeds: -6.2 percent

SECTION 6: OUTLOOK AND RISK FACTORS

The CFTC and BLS jointly identify the following risk factors as material to commodity price developments in fiscal year 2024:

1. Continuation of OPEC+ production management policies and their effect on global crude oil supply balances.
2. El Niño weather pattern development, which NOAA forecasts to persist through the first quarter of calendar year 2024, with potential adverse effects on agricultural production in Southeast Asia, Australia, and parts of South America.
3. Ongoing geopolitical tensions affecting Black Sea shipping lanes and their implications for grain and oilseed trade flows.
4. Federal Reserve monetary policy trajectory and its effect on the U.S. dollar, which maintains a historically significant inverse correlation of approximately -0.62 with the Bloomberg Commodity Index over rolling 12-month periods.
5. Structural supply constraints in cocoa and orange juice markets, which may sustain elevated prices beyond the fiscal year 2023 levels already observed.

DATA SOURCES AND METHODOLOGY

Price data for energy commodities was sourced from the U.S. Energy Information Administration's weekly and monthly price surveys. Agricultural commodity prices reflect CBOT and ICE futures settlement prices as reported by the respective exchanges and compiled by the USDA Economic Research Service. Metals prices reflect COMEX and LME official settlement prices. All fiscal year averages are calculated as arithmetic means of daily settlement prices for the period October 1, 2022 through September 30, 2023. Producer Price Index data is sourced exclusively from the Bureau of Labor Statistics and reflects not-seasonally-adjusted figures unless otherwise noted.
