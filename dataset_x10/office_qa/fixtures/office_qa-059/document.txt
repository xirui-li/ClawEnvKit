EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This report presents comprehensive commodity price statistics for fiscal year 2023 (January 1 through December 31, 2023), covering energy, agricultural, metals, and soft commodity sectors. Data has been compiled from the CFTC's market surveillance division, the BLS Producer Price Index program, the U.S. Energy Information Administration (EIA), and the Chicago Mercantile Exchange (CME) Group's public trading records. The report is intended for use by policymakers, market participants, and the general public in assessing commodity market conditions during the reporting period.

Overall, the Producer Price Index for all commodities declined 4.7 percent on a year-over-year basis in 2023, following a historic 14.8 percent increase recorded in 2022. This moderation reflects easing supply chain pressures, reduced energy costs, and stabilizing agricultural markets following the disruptions caused by the Russia-Ukraine conflict.

SECTION 1: ENERGY COMMODITIES

1.1 Crude Oil

West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude oil averaged $77.58 per barrel across calendar year 2023, representing a decline of 17.4 percent from the 2022 annual average of $93.97 per barrel. The annual high of $93.68 per barrel was recorded on September 27, 2023, driven by OPEC+ production cuts announced by Saudi Arabia and Russia in June 2023. The annual low of $66.74 per barrel occurred on March 17, 2023, amid banking sector turbulence following the collapse of Silicon Valley Bank.

Brent crude, the international benchmark, averaged $82.17 per barrel in 2023, down 16.2 percent from $98.00 per barrel in 2022.

U.S. crude oil production reached a record 12.9 million barrels per day in December 2023, surpassing the previous record of 12.8 million barrels per day set in November 2019.

1.2 Natural Gas

Henry Hub natural gas spot prices averaged $2.74 per million British thermal units (MMBtu) in 2023, a dramatic decline of 59.7 percent from the 2022 average of $6.80 per MMBtu. This represents the lowest annual average since 2020, when prices averaged $2.13 per MMBtu. The price collapse was attributable to record domestic production levels, mild winter temperatures in the first quarter of 2023, and reduced European demand following aggressive liquefied natural gas (LNG) stockpiling in late 2022.

1.3 Gasoline and Distillates

The U.S. average retail price of regular unleaded gasoline was $3.53 per gallon in 2023, compared to $3.99 per gallon in 2022, a decrease of 11.5 percent. Ultra-low sulfur diesel averaged $4.05 per gallon, down 22.3 percent from $5.21 per gallon in 2022.

SECTION 2: AGRICULTURAL COMMODITIES

2.1 Grains

Corn futures on the Chicago Board of Trade (CBOT) averaged $4.84 per bushel in 2023, declining 24.6 percent from the 2022 average of $6.42 per bushel. The market peaked at $6.73 per bushel on April 28, 2023, before declining steadily through the remainder of the year as U.S. crop conditions improved and Brazilian production reached record levels.

Wheat (CBOT Soft Red Winter) averaged $6.22 per bushel in 2023, down 28.9 percent from $8.75 per bushel in 2022. The decline reflects improved global supply conditions and the continuation of the Black Sea Grain Initiative through July 2023, which allowed Ukrainian exports to resume.

Soybeans averaged $13.44 per bushel in 2023, a decrease of 8.1 percent from $14.62 per bushel in 2022. Soybean prices demonstrated greater resilience than other grains due to sustained Chinese import demand, which totaled an estimated 98.6 million metric tons for the marketing year.

2.3 Livestock

Live cattle futures averaged $1.6742 per pound in 2023, an increase of 14.7 percent from $1.4596 per pound in 2022, reaching an all-time record annual average. Tight domestic cattle supplies, driven by prolonged drought conditions in the Southern Plains and reduced herd sizes, supported elevated prices throughout the year. The December 2023 contract settled at $1.7480 per pound on November 30, 2023.

Lean hog futures averaged $0.7823 per pound in 2023, down 9.4 percent from $0.8635 per pound in 2022, reflecting increased domestic pork production and softer export demand.

SECTION 3: METALS

3.1 Precious Metals

Gold averaged $1,940.54 per troy ounce in 2023, an increase of 8.2 percent from $1,793.40 per troy ounce in 2022. Gold reached an all-time intraday high of $2,135.39 per troy ounce on December 4, 2023, driven by expectations of Federal Reserve interest rate cuts in 2024 and heightened geopolitical uncertainty following the outbreak of conflict in the Middle East in October 2023.

Silver averaged $23.35 per troy ounce in 2023, up 0.4 percent from $21.73 per troy ounce in 2022. The gold-to-silver ratio averaged 83.1:1 during the year, compared to 82.5:1 in 2022.

3.2 Base Metals

Copper futures on the COMEX division of the New York Mercantile Exchange averaged $3.85 per pound in 2023, a decline of 4.7 percent from $4.04 per pound in 2022. Copper prices were constrained by slower-than-expected Chinese economic recovery following the lifting of COVID-19 restrictions in January 2023, as well as concerns about the property sector's ongoing contraction.

Aluminum averaged $2,264 per metric ton on the London Metal Exchange (LME) in 2023, down 16.8 percent from $2,720 per metric ton in 2022.

SECTION 4: SOFT COMMODITIES

4.1 Coffee and Cocoa

Arabica coffee futures on the Intercontinental Exchange (ICE) averaged $1.6482 per pound in 2023, a decrease of 11.3 percent from $1.8584 per pound in 2022. Improved Brazilian harvest conditions and favorable weather in Colombia contributed to the price decline.

Cocoa futures recorded exceptional performance in 2023, averaging $3,392 per metric ton, an increase of 42.3 percent from $2,384 per metric ton in 2022. Severe crop failures in Ivory Coast and Ghana, which together account for approximately 60 percent of global cocoa production, drove prices to their highest levels since 1977. By December 31, 2023, the nearby cocoa contract had reached $4,215 per metric ton.

4.2 Sugar

ICE raw sugar No. 11 futures averaged 23.87 cents per pound in 2023, up 14.6 percent from 20.83 cents per pound in 2022. Indian export restrictions announced in October 2023 and reduced Brazilian output in the first half of the year contributed to price strength.

SECTION 5: PRODUCER PRICE INDEX — COMMODITY BREAKDOWN

The BLS Producer Price Index for Finished Goods declined 0.9 percent in 2023 on a December-to-December basis, following an increase of 7.4 percent in 2022. The energy component of the PPI fell 14.2 percent, while the food component declined 3.8 percent. The core PPI (excluding food and energy) rose 2.6 percent.

Total U.S. commodity trading volume on regulated exchanges reached $487.3 trillion in notional value during 2023, according to CFTC data, compared to $512.8 trillion in 2022, a decrease of 5.0 percent. Energy contracts accounted for 38.4 percent of total volume, agricultural contracts represented 12.7 percent, and metals contracts comprised 9.1 percent.

SECTION 6: OUTLOOK AND POLICY CONSIDERATIONS

The CFTC and BLS project continued moderation in broad commodity price indices through the first half of 2024, contingent on stable geopolitical conditions and normal weather patterns in key agricultural regions. Key risk factors include potential escalation of Middle East conflict affecting oil shipping lanes through the Strait of Hormuz, El Niño weather effects on South American and Southeast Asian agricultural production, and the pace of Chinese economic recovery affecting base metals demand.

The Federal Reserve's anticipated transition to an easing monetary policy cycle in 2024 may provide upward support for gold and other precious metals, while a stronger U.S. dollar scenario could exert downward pressure on dollar-denominated commodity prices broadly.

DATA SOURCES AND METHODOLOGY

All price data reflects settlement prices on regulated U.S. exchanges unless otherwise noted. LME data is sourced from official LME daily settlement records. Annual averages are calculated as arithmetic means of daily settlement prices for the nearest active contract month. Data for this report was compiled by the CFTC's Division of Market Oversight and the BLS Office of Prices and Living Conditions.
