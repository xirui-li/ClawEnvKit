EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This report presents a comparative analysis of appropriated and expended budgets across major Commonwealth departments for fiscal year 2023–2024 (July 1, 2023 through June 30, 2024) against the prior fiscal year 2022–2023. The analysis was compiled under the direction of Budget Secretary Uri Monson and reflects data drawn from the Commonwealth's Integrated Enterprise System (IES) as of August 31, 2024. Total general fund expenditures for FY 2023–2024 reached $42.6 billion, representing an increase of $1.84 billion, or 4.5 percent, over the FY 2022–2023 total of $40.76 billion.

The report identifies departments that exceeded their appropriated budgets, those that returned surplus funds, and areas where year-over-year spending growth was most pronounced. Findings are intended to inform the Governor's budget office, the General Assembly's Appropriations Committees, and the public.

SECTION 1: STATEWIDE BUDGET OVERVIEW

1.1 Total Appropriations and Expenditures

The General Assembly enacted a general fund budget of $45.4 billion for FY 2023–2024 on July 7, 2023, following a brief budget impasse that delayed final passage by seven days. Actual expenditures of $42.6 billion represent a utilization rate of 93.8 percent against total appropriations, leaving an unspent balance of approximately $2.8 billion. This compares favorably to FY 2022–2023, when the utilization rate was 91.2 percent against an enacted budget of $44.7 billion with actual expenditures of $40.76 billion.

Revenue collections for FY 2023–2024 totaled $43.1 billion, exceeding expenditures by $500 million and contributing to a general fund balance of $7.3 billion as of June 30, 2024.

1.2 Year-Over-Year Spending Change by Category

Personnel Services: $14.2 billion (FY24) vs. $13.6 billion (FY23) — increase of $600 million, or 4.4%
Employee Benefits: $5.8 billion (FY24) vs. $5.5 billion (FY23) — increase of $300 million, or 5.5%
Contracts and Services: $9.1 billion (FY24) vs. $8.7 billion (FY23) — increase of $400 million, or 4.6%
Grants and Subsidies: $10.3 billion (FY24) vs. $9.9 billion (FY23) — increase of $400 million, or 4.0%
Capital Expenditures: $2.1 billion (FY24) vs. $1.9 billion (FY23) — increase of $200 million, or 10.5%
Other Operating Expenses: $1.1 billion (FY24) vs. $1.16 billion (FY23) — decrease of $60 million, or 5.2%

SECTION 2: DEPARTMENT-BY-DEPARTMENT COMPARISON

2.1 Department of Human Services (DHS)

Secretary: Dr. Val Arkoosh
FY 2023–2024 Appropriation: $16.8 billion
FY 2023–2024 Expenditure: $16.74 billion
FY 2022–2023 Expenditure: $15.9 billion
Year-Over-Year Change: +$840 million (+5.3%)
Utilization Rate: 99.6%

DHS remained the single largest spending department in the Commonwealth, accounting for 39.3 percent of total general fund expenditures. The increase was driven primarily by a 6.1 percent rise in Medical Assistance (Medicaid) costs, which totaled $11.2 billion in FY 2023–2024 compared to $10.56 billion in FY 2022–2023. Enrollment in the HealthChoices managed care program reached 2.87 million individuals as of March 31, 2024, up from 2.71 million the prior year. The Child Care Works subsidy program saw expenditures increase by $112 million due to expanded eligibility thresholds enacted under Act 14 of 2023.

2.2 Department of Education (PDE)

Secretary: Dr. Khalid Mumin
FY 2023–2024 Appropriation: $8.1 billion
FY 2023–2024 Expenditure: $7.96 billion
FY 2022–2023 Expenditure: $7.44 billion
Year-Over-Year Change: +$520 million (+7.0%)
Utilization Rate: 98.3%

The Department of Education recorded the largest percentage increase among major departments. Basic Education Funding distributed to school districts totaled $7.07 billion, reflecting a $567 million increase over the prior year's $6.5 billion. This increase was the result of the landmark Pennsylvania Supreme Court ruling in William Penn School District v. Pennsylvania Department of Education, which prompted the legislature to revise the funding formula. Special Education funding rose to $1.27 billion from $1.19 billion, an increase of $80 million or 6.7 percent.

2.3 Department of Transportation (PennDOT)

Secretary: Michael Carroll
FY 2023–2024 Appropriation: $6.3 billion (combined general fund and Motor License Fund)
FY 2023–2024 Expenditure: $5.88 billion
FY 2022–2023 Expenditure: $5.71 billion
Year-Over-Year Change: +$170 million (+3.0%)
Utilization Rate: 93.3%

PennDOT's capital program expenditures totaled $3.4 billion, funding 847 active construction projects across the Commonwealth's 67 counties. The department completed 214 bridge replacement or rehabilitation projects during FY 2023–2024, compared to 198 in the prior year. Personnel costs declined slightly by $18 million due to a hiring freeze implemented from October 2023 through February 2024 in response to projected revenue shortfalls that did not ultimately materialize.

2.4 Department of Corrections (DOC)

Secretary: Laurel Harry
FY 2023–2024 Appropriation: $3.1 billion
FY 2023–2024 Expenditure: $3.07 billion
FY 2022–2023 Expenditure: $2.98 billion
Year-Over-Year Change: +$90 million (+3.0%)
Utilization Rate: 99.0%

The average daily inmate population across the Commonwealth's 23 state correctional institutions was 37,842 in FY 2023–2024, a decrease from 38,614 in FY 2022–2023. Despite the population decline, per-inmate daily cost rose from $211.47 to $222.18, an increase of 5.1 percent, driven by healthcare contract renegotiations with Wellpath LLC finalized in November 2023 and a 4.0 percent cost-of-living adjustment for correctional officers effective January 1, 2024.

2.5 Department of Environmental Protection (DEP)

Secretary: Jessica Shirley
FY 2023–2024 Appropriation: $242 million
FY 2023–2024 Expenditure: $228 million
FY 2022–2023 Expenditure: $219 million
Year-Over-Year Change: +$9 million (+4.1%)
Utilization Rate: 94.2%

DEP returned $14 million in unspent appropriations, the largest surplus among smaller agencies. The department attributed the unspent funds to delayed hiring for 47 environmental inspector positions, of which only 29 were filled by June 30, 2024. Federal reimbursements through the Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act supplemented DEP's budget by an additional $87 million in FY 2023–2024, a figure not reflected in the general fund totals above.

SECTION 3: DEPARTMENTS EXCEEDING APPROPRIATIONS

Three departments required supplemental appropriations during FY 2023–2024:

Department of Military and Veterans Affairs: Appropriation of $189 million; final expenditure of $196.4 million. Supplemental appropriation of $7.4 million approved by the General Assembly on March 6, 2024, to cover unanticipated costs associated with the activation of 1,200 National Guard personnel for flood response operations in Luzerne and Wyoming Counties in November 2023.

Office of the State Fire Commissioner: Appropriation of $31 million; final expenditure of $33.2 million. Supplemental appropriation of $2.2 million approved April 18, 2024, for emergency equipment procurement.

Department of Agriculture: Appropriation of $178 million; final expenditure of $181.6 million. Supplemental appropriation of $3.6 million approved February 22, 2024, related to avian influenza response and flock depopulation assistance programs.

SECTION 4: NOTABLE TRENDS AND FINDINGS

4.1 Personnel Cost Pressures

Statewide personnel costs, including salaries and benefits, totaled $20.0 billion in FY 2023–2024, representing 47.0 percent of total general fund expenditures. This is up from 47.0 percent in FY 2022–2023 ($19.1 billion), indicating that personnel costs are growing in line with overall spending. The Commonwealth employed 78,341 full-time equivalent positions as of June 30, 2024, compared to 77,908 in the prior year.

4.2 Pension Obligations

Employer contributions to the State Employees' Retirement System (SERS) totaled $1.47 billion in FY 2023–2024, and contributions to the Public School Employees' Retirement System (PSERS) totaled $2.84 billion. Combined pension contributions of $4.31 billion represent 10.1 percent of total general fund expenditures, a slight decrease from 10.4 percent in FY 2022–2023, reflecting the impact of investment gains recorded in calendar year 2023.

4.3 Federal Fund Leverage

Departments leveraged $18.3 billion in federal funds during FY 2023–2024, a ratio of approximately $0.43 in federal funds for every $1.00 in state general fund expenditure. This represents a modest decline from the $19.1 billion in federal funds received in FY 2022–2023, when pandemic-era relief programs were still partially active.

SECTION 5: RECOMMENDATIONS

The Bureau of Financial Management offers the following recommendations for consideration in the FY 2025–2026 budget development process:

1. DHS should conduct a comprehensive review of Medical Assistance managed care contracts, which are due for renewal by December 31, 2025, to identify cost containment opportunities given the 6.1 percent annual growth rate.

2. PennDOT should develop a multi-year capital spending plan that aligns project timelines with available Motor License Fund revenues to reduce the risk of mid-year budget adjustments.

3. DEP should accelerate recruitment for vacant inspector positions, as the 47-position vacancy rate is limiting the agency's regulatory capacity and contributing to unspent appropriations.

4. The Office of the Budget should establish a formal contingency reserve of no less than $150 million within the general fund to address emergency supplemental appropriation needs without requiring mid-year legislative action.
