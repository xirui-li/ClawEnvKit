EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The Commonwealth of Pennsylvania's Office of the Budget hereby presents the Second Quarter Financial Report for Fiscal Year 2023-2024, covering the period of October 1, 2023 through December 31, 2023. This report details General Fund revenues, expenditures, and fund balances, with particular emphasis on quarter-over-quarter changes relative to the First Quarter (July 1 – September 30, 2023).

Overall, the Commonwealth collected $10.84 billion in General Fund revenues during Q2 FY2023-24, representing a 6.3 percent increase over the $10.20 billion collected in Q1 FY2023-24. Total expenditures for Q2 reached $11.67 billion, compared to $10.91 billion in Q1, reflecting a 6.97 percent increase driven primarily by scheduled Medicaid disbursements and school district reimbursements. The General Fund ended Q2 with an unappropriated surplus of $1.23 billion, down from $1.47 billion at the close of Q1.

Budget Secretary Uri Monson noted that the revenue performance in Q2 reflects continued strength in personal income tax collections and a modest recovery in corporate net income tax receipts following softer-than-expected Q1 results.

1.2 Key Revenue Observations

Personal Income Tax (PIT): PIT collections of $4.60 billion in Q2 exceeded Q1 by $289.3 million. The increase is attributable to stronger employer withholding in October and November 2023, consistent with continued low unemployment in Pennsylvania, which stood at 3.6 percent as of November 2023 according to the Pennsylvania Department of Labor and Industry.

Sales and Use Tax (SUT): Q2 SUT collections of $3.10 billion reflect robust consumer spending during the October–December period, which historically captures pre-holiday and holiday retail activity. The $228.7 million quarter-over-quarter increase aligns with a 4.1 percent increase in Pennsylvania retail sales reported by the Pennsylvania Retailers' Association for the same period.

Corporate Net Income Tax (CNIT): The 15.32 percent quarter-over-quarter increase in CNIT, from $1.20 billion to $1.39 billion, partially reverses the underperformance observed in Q1. Q1 CNIT had come in $97.4 million below the official estimate published in the June 2023 revenue certification. The Q2 recovery reflects accelerated estimated payments from several large financial sector firms headquartered in Philadelphia, including payments processed between October 15 and November 15, 2023.

Realty Transfer Tax: The 12.49 percent decline in Realty Transfer Tax collections, from $187.3 million to $163.9 million, reflects continued softness in Pennsylvania's residential real estate market. The Pennsylvania Association of Realtors reported that existing home sales in the Commonwealth fell 8.7 percent year-over-year in Q2, consistent with elevated mortgage rates averaging 7.44 percent during the quarter.

2.2 Key Expenditure Observations

Medicaid: Human Services expenditures, dominated by the Medical Assistance (Medicaid) program administered by the Pennsylvania Department of Human Services, increased by $367.4 million or 9.55 percent quarter-over-quarter. This increase reflects the scheduled October 2023 rate adjustments for managed care organizations under the HealthChoices program, as well as a 2.1 percent increase in the number of enrolled beneficiaries compared to Q1. As of December 31, 2023, Pennsylvania's Medicaid enrollment stood at approximately 3.47 million individuals.

Basic Education Subsidy: The $238.4 million increase in Basic Education Subsidy payments reflects the Commonwealth's accelerated disbursement schedule adopted following the February 2023 Commonwealth Court ruling in William Penn School District v. Pennsylvania Department of Education. Under the revised schedule approved by the General Assembly in Act 55 of 2023, 52 percent of the annual Basic Education Subsidy appropriation of $8.02 billion is disbursed in Q2, compared to 40 percent in Q1.

Debt Service: Debt service payments declined by $14.4 million in Q2, as a series of general obligation bond maturities that fell due in August and September 2023 were not replicated in the October–December period. The Commonwealth's total outstanding general obligation debt as of December 31, 2023 was $10.87 billion, down from $11.14 billion at the close of Q1.

3.2 Budget Stabilization Reserve (Rainy Day Fund)

The Budget Stabilization Reserve Fund maintained a balance of $5.07 billion as of December 31, 2023, unchanged from the Q1 close. No transfers were made to or from the Rainy Day Fund during Q2. This balance represents approximately 10.8 percent of the FY2023-24 General Fund appropriation of $47.0 billion, which exceeds the 8.0 percent benchmark recommended by the Government Finance Officers Association (GFOA).

SECTION 4: YEAR-TO-DATE PERFORMANCE VERSUS ESTIMATE

Through the first six months of FY2023-24 (July 1 – December 31, 2023), total General Fund revenues of $21.04 billion are running $312.7 million, or 1.51 percent, above the official estimate of $20.73 billion. This outperformance is concentrated in Personal Income Tax (+$198.4 million above estimate) and Sales and Use Tax (+$144.6 million above estimate), partially offset by Realty Transfer Tax underperformance of $61.3 million below estimate.

Year-to-date expenditures of $22.58 billion are $88.3 million, or 0.39 percent, above the enacted budget plan, primarily due to higher-than-anticipated Medicaid costs.

SECTION 5: OUTLOOK FOR Q3 FY2023-24

The Office of the Budget projects Q3 (January 1 – March 31, 2024) General Fund revenues of approximately $9.87 billion, reflecting the historically lower revenue quarter associated with reduced consumer spending following the holiday season and the timing of corporate estimated payments. Q3 expenditures are projected at $11.34 billion, driven by the second major Medicaid managed care payment cycle and spring school district reimbursements.

The Office will continue to monitor developments in federal Medicaid policy, particularly proposed changes to the enhanced Federal Medical Assistance Percentage (eFMAP) rates, which currently provide Pennsylvania with a 6.2 percentage point enhancement above the standard 55.73 percent FMAP rate. Any reduction in federal matching funds would materially affect the Commonwealth's net Medicaid expenditure position in Q3 and Q4.
