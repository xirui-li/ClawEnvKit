EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The Commonwealth of Pennsylvania's Office of the Budget presents this Quarterly Financial Report for the third quarter of Fiscal Year 2023-2024, covering the period from October 1, 2023 through December 31, 2023. This report provides a comprehensive analysis of revenue collections, expenditure activity, and fund balances compared to the prior quarter (July 1, 2023 through September 30, 2023) and the corresponding quarter of the prior fiscal year.

Overall, the Commonwealth collected $10.84 billion in General Fund revenues during Q3 FY2023-24, representing a decrease of $1.23 billion, or 10.2 percent, from Q2 FY2023-24, which recorded $12.07 billion. However, Q3 revenues exceeded the same quarter of the prior fiscal year (Q3 FY2022-23: $10.61 billion) by $230 million, or 2.2 percent. Total General Fund expenditures for Q3 FY2023-24 reached $11.19 billion, an increase of $418 million, or 3.9 percent, over Q2 FY2023-24 expenditures of $10.77 billion.

Secretary of the Budget Uri Monson noted that the quarter-over-quarter revenue decline is consistent with seasonal patterns, as the second quarter historically benefits from estimated corporate tax payments due in September. The Commonwealth ended Q3 with a General Fund balance of $3.62 billion, down from $4.97 billion at the close of Q2.

1.2 Personal Income Tax

Personal Income Tax (PIT) collections of $3.89 billion in Q3 FY2023-24 declined $327.2 million, or 7.8 percent, from Q2. The decline is attributable primarily to lower withholding receipts in October and November, which reflected reduced bonus compensation activity compared to the September quarter. Quarterly estimated payments received in December totaled $612.4 million, down 4.1 percent from the $638.6 million received in September. The PIT rate remains at 3.07 percent, unchanged since 2004.

1.3 Sales and Use Tax

Sales and Use Tax (SUT) was the only major tax category to show quarter-over-quarter growth, increasing $183.7 million, or 5.9 percent, to $3.29 billion. This increase reflects stronger consumer spending during the October-December holiday shopping period. Online marketplace facilitator collections, which have been tracked separately since Act 13 of 2019, contributed $487.3 million in Q3, up from $401.6 million in Q2, a 21.3 percent increase.

1.4 Corporate Net Income Tax

The most significant quarter-over-quarter decline occurred in Corporate Net Income Tax (CNIT), which fell $897.5 million, or 34.0 percent, from $2.64 billion in Q2 to $1.74 billion in Q3. This decline was expected, as Q2 benefited from the September 15 estimated payment deadline, which is the largest corporate estimated payment date of the fiscal year. Pennsylvania's CNIT rate was reduced from 9.99 percent to 8.99 percent effective January 1, 2023, as part of the phased reduction schedule established under Act 53 of 2022, which will bring the rate to 4.99 percent by 2031.

2.2 Education Expenditures

PreK-12 education expenditures increased $265.6 million, or 6.9 percent, quarter-over-quarter, reaching $4.11 billion in Q3. This increase reflects the timing of Basic Education Funding subsidy payments to school districts, with the December payment cycle including a supplemental distribution of $187.4 million related to the Governor's Level Up initiative for the 76 lowest-funded school districts. The Pennsylvania Department of Education disbursed payments to all 500 school districts during the quarter.

2.3 Debt Service

Debt service expenditures rose sharply by $132.1 million, or 27.0 percent, from $489.3 million in Q2 to $621.4 million in Q3. This increase reflects the November 1 and December 1 semi-annual bond payment dates for several General Obligation bond series. The Commonwealth's total outstanding General Obligation debt as of December 31, 2023 stood at $10.84 billion, a reduction of $312.6 million from the June 30, 2023 balance of $11.15 billion, consistent with the Governor's debt reduction strategy.

SECTION 3: FUND BALANCE AND RESERVE POSITION

3.1 General Fund Balance

The General Fund unappropriated balance decreased from $4.97 billion at September 30, 2023 to $3.62 billion at December 31, 2023, a reduction of $1.35 billion. This reflects the net effect of Q3 revenues of $10.84 billion against expenditures of $11.19 billion, resulting in a quarterly deficit of $350 million.

3.2 Budget Stabilization Reserve Fund (Rainy Day Fund)

The Budget Stabilization Reserve Fund balance remained unchanged at $1.83 billion during Q3, as no deposits or withdrawals were made. This balance represents approximately 4.1 percent of the FY2023-24 enacted General Fund budget of $44.4 billion, below the 8 to 12 percent range recommended by the Government Finance Officers Association (GFOA).

3.3 Year-to-Date Position

Through the first three quarters of FY2023-24 (July 1 through December 31, 2023), the Commonwealth has collected $33.76 billion in General Fund revenues against a prorated annual estimate of $33.21 billion, placing collections $550 million, or 1.7 percent, ahead of estimate. Year-to-date expenditures of $33.13 billion are $284 million, or 0.9 percent, below the prorated appropriation level.

SECTION 4: SPECIAL FUNDS AND FEDERAL RECEIPTS

4.1 Motor License Fund

The Motor License Fund received $1.04 billion in Q3 FY2023-24, compared to $1.01 billion in Q2, an increase of $30.0 million, or 3.0 percent. Motor fuel tax collections of $618.4 million accounted for the majority of receipts. The Pennsylvania Department of Transportation (PennDOT) expended $1.17 billion from the Motor License Fund during Q3, primarily for highway maintenance contracts and capital projects.

4.2 Federal Funds

Federal reimbursements to the Commonwealth totaled $5.84 billion in Q3 FY2023-24, compared to $5.61 billion in Q2, an increase of $230 million, or 4.1 percent. The largest component was Medicaid Federal Financial Participation (FFP) at $2.98 billion, reflecting the 55.67 percent federal medical assistance percentage (FMAP) applicable to Pennsylvania for federal fiscal year 2024.

SECTION 5: OUTLOOK AND FOURTH QUARTER PROJECTIONS

The Office of the Budget projects Q4 FY2023-24 (January 1 through March 31, 2024) General Fund revenues of approximately $11.6 billion, driven by April 15 estimated payment activity that falls within the quarter and strong anticipated SUT collections. Q4 expenditures are projected at $11.1 billion, yielding an anticipated positive quarterly balance of $500 million.

The Independent Fiscal Office (IFO), in its December 2023 revenue update, projected full-year FY2023-24 General Fund revenues of $44.8 billion, $400 million above the enacted estimate of $44.4 billion. The Office of the Budget's internal projection of $44.9 billion is modestly more optimistic, contingent on continued labor market stability and consumer spending resilience through the spring filing season.
