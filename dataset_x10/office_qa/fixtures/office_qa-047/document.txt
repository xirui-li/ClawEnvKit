EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This report presents the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania's financial performance for the third quarter of fiscal year 2023-2024, covering the period January 1 through March 31, 2024, with direct comparison to the immediately preceding second quarter (October 1 through December 31, 2023). The data reflects General Fund activity unless otherwise noted. Overall, Q3 demonstrated a net improvement in the Commonwealth's fiscal position, driven primarily by stronger-than-anticipated personal income tax collections and a partial recovery in corporate net income tax receipts following a soft second quarter.

Total General Fund revenues for Q3 FY2023-24 reached $11.84 billion, representing an increase of $1.27 billion, or 12.0 percent, over Q2 revenues of $10.57 billion. Total General Fund expenditures for Q3 were $10.91 billion, compared to $11.38 billion in Q2, a decrease of $470 million, or 4.1 percent. The resulting Q3 surplus of $930 million compares favorably to the Q2 deficit of $810 million, reflecting the seasonal nature of both tax collection cycles and expenditure patterns.

1.2 Key Revenue Observations

Personal Income Tax (PIT): The $679 million quarter-over-quarter increase in PIT collections was the single largest driver of Q3 revenue growth. This improvement reflects the January 31, 2024 deadline for employer withholding reconciliation filings and a surge in estimated payments from self-employed individuals responding to strong 2023 capital gains realizations. The Department of Revenue processed approximately 1.4 million individual returns during Q3, compared to 340,000 in Q2.

Corporate Net Income Tax (CNIT): CNIT collections rose by $244 million (20.3%) from Q2 to Q3. The increase is attributable to the March 15, 2024 estimated payment deadline for calendar-year S-corporations and partnerships that elected to pay at the entity level under Act 53 of 2022. Additionally, audit assessments finalized during February 2024 contributed approximately $61 million in one-time collections.

Sales and Use Tax (SUT): SUT declined modestly by $68 million (-2.3%) in Q3. December 2023 holiday retail activity, which is captured in Q2 remittances due to the one-month lag in reporting, was not replicated in the January-March period. Consumer spending data from the Pennsylvania Department of Labor and Industry indicated a 1.8 percent decline in taxable retail transactions during January 2024 compared to December 2023.

Non-Tax Revenue: The $367 million increase in non-tax revenue was largely driven by $298 million in federal reimbursements received in February 2024 related to Medicaid administrative costs under the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services quarterly settlement process. An additional $52 million was received from the Pennsylvania Liquor Control Board's quarterly transfer on March 15, 2024, up from $44 million in the prior quarter.

2.2 Key Expenditure Observations

Education (PreK-12): The $264 million decline in education expenditures reflects the timing of the Basic Education Funding subsidy disbursements. Under the Commonwealth's payment schedule, the largest annual disbursement of $1.1 billion was made in October 2023 (Q2), while Q3 disbursements returned to the standard monthly allocation of approximately $614 million per month. The Q3 figure represents three standard monthly payments totaling $1,843 million.

Debt Service: The $278 million reduction in debt service expenditures is a direct result of payment scheduling. The Commonwealth made its largest semi-annual general obligation bond payment of $612 million on November 1, 2023, which fell within Q2. The next comparable payment is scheduled for May 1, 2024. Q3 debt service consisted primarily of interest-only payments on variable-rate obligations and lease financing arrangements.

Human Services: Human Services spending increased by $83 million (2.0%) in Q3, driven by a 3.4 percent increase in Medical Assistance enrollment. As of March 31, 2024, 3,412,000 Pennsylvanians were enrolled in Medical Assistance, compared to 3,301,000 as of December 31, 2023. The Department of Human Services attributed the enrollment growth to the conclusion of the federal continuous enrollment requirement and subsequent re-enrollment activity.

Corrections: The Department of Corrections expenditure of $748 million in Q3 reflects a daily average inmate population of 38,847, compared to 38,614 in Q2. The per-diem cost per inmate increased from $208.47 in Q2 to $210.12 in Q3, reflecting a January 2024 cost-of-living adjustment for corrections staff under the AFSCME Council 13 collective bargaining agreement ratified in December 2023.

SECTION 3: CASH POSITION AND FUND BALANCE

As of March 31, 2024, the General Fund unappropriated surplus stood at $2.14 billion, compared to $1.21 billion as of December 31, 2023, an increase of $930 million. The Budget Stabilization Reserve Fund (Rainy Day Fund) remained unchanged at $1.83 billion during Q3, as no transfers were authorized by the General Assembly during this period.

The Commonwealth's short-term borrowing activity decreased significantly in Q3. Tax Anticipation Notes (TANs) outstanding as of March 31, 2024 totaled $0, compared to $500 million outstanding as of December 31, 2023. The $500 million TAN issued on November 15, 2023 at an interest rate of 4.87 percent was retired on February 29, 2024 using Q3 tax receipts.

---

SECTION 4: YEAR-TO-DATE PERFORMANCE VERSUS ESTIMATE

Through the first three quarters of FY2023-24 (July 1, 2023 – March 31, 2024), total General Fund revenues of $32.16 billion represent 76.4 percent of the full-year estimate of $42.10 billion established in the Governor's Executive Budget presented February 6, 2024. This pace is 1.2 percentage points ahead of the historical three-quarter average of 75.2 percent, suggesting the Commonwealth is on track to meet or modestly exceed its full-year revenue estimate.

Year-to-date expenditures of $31.23 billion represent 74.4 percent of the full-year appropriation of $41.98 billion, consistent with historical norms.

---

SECTION 5: NOTABLE RISKS AND FORWARD OUTLOOK

The Office of the Budget identifies the following material risks for Q4 FY2023-24 (April 1 – June 30, 2024):

- Federal Medicaid matching rate: The enhanced Federal Medical Assistance Percentage (FMAP) of 6.2 percentage points, which provided Pennsylvania with an estimated $218 million in additional federal revenue during Q3, is scheduled to expire on June 30, 2024. No extension has been enacted as of the publication date of this report.

- Interest rate sensitivity: The Commonwealth holds $1.2 billion in variable-rate debt obligations. A 25-basis-point increase in the SOFR benchmark rate would increase annual debt service costs by approximately $3 million.

- Personal income tax refunds: As of April 15, 2024, the Department of Revenue had issued $1.87 billion in PIT refunds for tax year 2023, with an estimated $640 million in approved refunds pending disbursement in Q4.
