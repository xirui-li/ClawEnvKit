EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This report presents a comprehensive analysis of Commonwealth expenditure patterns across the five-year period from Fiscal Year 2019-2020 through Fiscal Year 2023-2024. Total general fund expenditures for FY 2023-2024 reached $47.6 billion, representing a 6.2 percent increase over the prior fiscal year's total of $44.8 billion. The analysis identifies sustained growth in human services and education spending, moderate increases in debt service obligations, and a notable deceleration in infrastructure investment relative to the post-pandemic recovery period of FY 2021-2022.

Secretary of the Budget Uri Monson authorized this report pursuant to Section 614 of the Administrative Code of 1929, as amended. Data were compiled from the Commonwealth's SAP-based financial management system, the Integrated Enterprise System (IES), with final reconciliation completed September 30, 2024.

SECTION 1: FIVE-YEAR GENERAL FUND EXPENDITURE OVERVIEW

Total general fund expenditures have grown by $9.1 billion, or 23.6 percent, over the five-year analysis window. The compound annual growth rate (CAGR) for this period stands at 4.3 percent, modestly exceeding the average Pennsylvania CPI increase of 3.8 percent over the same interval.

TABLE 1: GENERAL FUND EXPENDITURES BY FISCAL YEAR (in billions)

Fiscal Year     Total Expenditures     Year-over-Year Change     % Change
FY 2019-2020         $38.5                      —                    —
FY 2020-2021         $40.1                    +$1.6                +4.2%
FY 2021-2022         $43.0                    +$2.9                +7.2%
FY 2022-2023         $44.8                    +$1.8                +4.2%
FY 2023-2024         $47.6                    +$2.8                +6.2%

The spike observed in FY 2021-2022 reflects the drawdown of federal American Rescue Plan Act (ARPA) matching funds and accelerated capital project disbursements totaling $1.4 billion that were deferred from FY 2020-2021 due to COVID-19 procurement delays.

SECTION 2: EXPENDITURE BY MAJOR CATEGORY — FY 2023-2024

The $47.6 billion in total general fund expenditures for FY 2023-2024 is distributed across six primary functional categories. Human Services remains the dominant category, accounting for 39.4 percent of all general fund spending.

TABLE 2: FY 2023-2024 EXPENDITURES BY CATEGORY (in billions)

Category                          FY 2023-2024     FY 2022-2023     Change
Human Services                      $18.76           $17.41         +7.8%
Education (PreK-12)                 $13.22           $12.58         +5.1%
Debt Service                         $4.31            $3.97         +8.6%
Public Safety & Corrections          $3.18            $3.09         +2.9%
General Government Operations        $2.89            $2.74         +5.5%
Transportation & Infrastructure      $2.44            $2.63         -7.2%
Other/Miscellaneous                  $2.80            $2.38        +17.6%

Key observations from Table 2:

- Human Services spending of $18.76 billion is driven primarily by the Department of Human Services Medical Assistance program, which alone accounts for $11.3 billion, a 9.1 percent increase attributable to Medicaid enrollment growth and pharmaceutical cost escalation.

- Education spending reached $13.22 billion following the implementation of the Basic Education Funding Commission's recommendations, which Governor Josh Shapiro signed into law under Act 55 of 2023. The $645 million increase includes $200 million in new adequacy supplements directed to the 100 lowest-wealth school districts.

- Debt service obligations rose to $4.31 billion, the highest level in Commonwealth history, reflecting bond issuances from FY 2020-2021 and FY 2021-2022 now entering their principal repayment phases. The debt service ratio (debt service as a percentage of total expenditures) increased from 8.9 percent in FY 2022-2023 to 9.1 percent in FY 2023-2024.

- Transportation and Infrastructure spending declined by $190 million (-7.2 percent), reflecting the completion of 14 major bridge rehabilitation projects funded under the Multimodal Transportation Fund and the expiration of $312 million in federal infrastructure bridge formula program allocations that were fully expended by March 31, 2024.

SECTION 3: HUMAN SERVICES EXPENDITURE DEEP DIVE

Given that Human Services represents the largest and fastest-growing expenditure category, this section provides additional granularity on program-level trends.

The Department of Human Services (DHS), Secretary Dr. Val Arkoosh presiding, administered $18.76 billion in FY 2023-2024 across the following major programs:

Medical Assistance (Medicaid):          $11.30 billion     (+9.1% YoY)
Long-Term Living Services:               $3.42 billion     (+6.4% YoY)
Child Welfare Services:                  $1.87 billion     (+4.8% YoY)
Income Maintenance Programs:             $1.21 billion     (-2.3% YoY)
Behavioral Health Services:              $0.96 billion    (+11.2% YoY)

Medicaid enrollment as of June 30, 2024 stood at 3,487,000 individuals, compared to 3,312,000 on June 30, 2023, an increase of 175,000 enrollees (5.3 percent). The average per-enrollee cost rose from $3,120 to $3,241, a 3.9 percent increase, consistent with national trends reported by the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services.

Behavioral Health Services recorded the highest growth rate within DHS at 11.2 percent, reaching $960 million. This increase reflects the Commonwealth's $75 million investment in crisis stabilization centers announced by Governor Shapiro on February 14, 2024, and expanded substance use disorder treatment capacity under the Pennsylvania Opioid Misuse and Addiction Abatement Trust.

Income Maintenance Programs declined by 2.3 percent to $1.21 billion, consistent with declining SNAP and TANF caseloads as labor market conditions remained favorable, with Pennsylvania's unemployment rate averaging 3.7 percent during FY 2023-2024.

SECTION 4: PERSONNEL COSTS AND WORKFORCE EXPENDITURES

Personnel-related expenditures, encompassing salaries, wages, and employee benefits, totaled $14.8 billion in FY 2023-2024, representing 31.1 percent of total general fund expenditures. This compares to $13.9 billion (31.0 percent) in FY 2022-2023.

The 6.5 percent increase in personnel costs reflects:

1. A 3.25 percent general wage increase effective July 1, 2023, applicable to approximately 67,000 Commonwealth employees covered under AFSCME Council 13 and SEIU Healthcare Pennsylvania collective bargaining agreements ratified in May 2023.

2. A 12.4 percent increase in employer pension contributions to the State Employees' Retirement System (SERS), driven by the actuarially determined contribution rate rising from 34.1 percent to 36.8 percent of payroll. Total SERS contributions from the general fund reached $1.87 billion.

3. Health insurance premium costs for active employees increased by 7.1 percent, totaling $2.34 billion, reflecting claims experience and pharmaceutical benefit costs.

The Commonwealth's total authorized full-time equivalent (FTE) position count as of June 30, 2024 was 78,412, compared to 77,891 on June 30, 2023. Net new positions were concentrated in the Department of Corrections (214 new corrections officers) and the Department of Human Services (307 caseworkers added in response to child welfare caseload increases).

SECTION 5: EXPENDITURE PROJECTIONS — FY 2024-2025

Based on enacted appropriations under the FY 2024-2025 General Appropriations Act (Act 1A of 2024, signed July 11, 2024), total general fund expenditures are projected at $49.9 billion, representing a 4.8 percent increase over FY 2023-2024 actuals.

Projected growth drivers include:

- Medical Assistance: Projected at $12.1 billion (+7.1%), reflecting continued enrollment growth and the phase-out of enhanced federal matching funds
- Education: Projected at $13.85 billion (+4.8%), including the second year of adequacy supplement funding
- Debt Service: Projected at $4.58 billion (+6.3%), reflecting scheduled principal and interest payments on $2.1 billion in general obligation bonds issued in November 2023
- Behavioral Health: Projected at $1.05 billion (+9.4%), continuing the investment trajectory established in FY 2023-2024

The Office of the Budget projects that if current growth trends continue without structural reform, total general fund expenditures will reach $54.2 billion by FY 2026-2027, which would require either revenue growth averaging 5.1 percent annually or identification of $2.8 billion in expenditure reductions over the three-year period.

SECTION 6: CONCLUSIONS AND FISCAL SUSTAINABILITY CONSIDERATIONS

This analysis identifies three primary fiscal sustainability concerns warranting legislative and executive attention:

First, the debt service ratio of 9.1 percent, while below the Government Finance Officers Association (GFOA) recommended ceiling of 10 percent, has increased by 0.8 percentage points over five years. Continued bond issuance at current levels risks breaching this threshold by FY 2026-2027.

Second, Medicaid expenditure growth at 9.1 percent substantially exceeds overall budget growth of 6.2 percent, creating structural pressure that will intensify as federal enhanced matching rates normalize.

Third, the "Other/Miscellaneous" category growth of 17.6 percent ($420 million) warrants further programmatic review. The Bureau of Financial Management has initiated a categorical audit, with findings expected by January 31, 2025.
