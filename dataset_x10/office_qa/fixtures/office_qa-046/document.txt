EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This report presents a comprehensive Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR) analysis of the Federal Disability Insurance (DI) Trust Fund for the ten-year period spanning fiscal year 2014 through fiscal year 2024. The analysis was conducted pursuant to Section 201(f) of the Social Security Act, which mandates periodic financial performance reviews of all federal trust fund accounts. The findings contained herein are intended to inform congressional oversight committees, the Social Security Board of Trustees, and the Office of Management and Budget regarding the fund's long-term financial trajectory.

The DI Trust Fund experienced a CAGR of 3.47 percent in total asset reserves over the ten-year measurement period, recovering from a critically depleted position in 2016 following the Bipartisan Budget Act of 2015, which temporarily reallocated payroll tax revenues to stabilize the fund. This report details the year-by-year asset values, income streams, disbursement patterns, and the mathematical derivation of the CAGR figures presented.


SECTION 1: BACKGROUND AND STATUTORY CONTEXT

The Federal Disability Insurance Trust Fund was established under Title II of the Social Security Act and receives its primary funding from the 1.8 percent employee and employer payroll tax contributions allocated specifically to disability insurance under the Federal Insurance Contributions Act (FICA). As of December 31, 2024, the fund covers approximately 8.4 million disabled workers and 1.7 million dependents of disabled workers.

The Bipartisan Budget Act of 2015 (Public Law 114-74), signed into law on November 2, 2015, authorized a temporary reallocation of payroll tax revenues, shifting 0.57 percentage points from the Old-Age and Survivors Insurance (OASI) Trust Fund to the DI Trust Fund for the period 2016 through 2018. This intervention prevented the DI Trust Fund from exhausting its reserves, which the 2015 Trustees Report had projected would occur in the fourth quarter of 2016.


SECTION 2: METHODOLOGY FOR CAGR COMPUTATION

The Compound Annual Growth Rate is calculated using the following standard formula:

CAGR = (Ending Value / Beginning Value) ^ (1 / Number of Years) - 1

For the primary ten-year analysis in this report:
- Beginning Value (FY2014 year-end reserves): $60.4 billion
- Ending Value (FY2024 year-end reserves): $85.7 billion
- Number of Years: 10

CAGR = ($85.7 billion / $60.4 billion) ^ (1/10) - 1
CAGR = (1.4189) ^ (0.10) - 1
CAGR = 1.03474 - 1
CAGR = 0.03474, or approximately 3.47 percent

All reserve figures are reported in nominal dollars. A separate inflation-adjusted analysis using the GDP Implicit Price Deflator is presented in Section 4, yielding a real CAGR of 1.12 percent over the same period.

SECTION 4: INCOME AND DISBURSEMENT ANALYSIS

Total income to the DI Trust Fund in FY2024 was $196.3 billion, consisting of the following components:

- Payroll tax contributions (net): $181.4 billion
- Income from taxation of benefits: $9.7 billion
- Interest earned on invested assets: $3.8 billion
- Reimbursements from the General Fund: $1.4 billion

Total disbursements in FY2024 were $193.1 billion, consisting of:

- Benefit payments to disabled workers: $176.8 billion
- Benefit payments to dependents of disabled workers: $9.3 billion
- Administrative expenses (SSA): $4.2 billion
- Vocational rehabilitation reimbursements: $2.8 billion

The net increase in reserves for FY2024 was therefore $3.2 billion, consistent with the $3.1 billion increase reflected in the year-end balance table above (the minor discrepancy reflects rounding and timing adjustments in intergovernmental transfers).

The CAGR of total annual income over the ten-year period was 4.21 percent, slightly exceeding the reserve CAGR of 3.47 percent, indicating that income growth has modestly outpaced the rate at which reserves were being rebuilt from the 2015 low point.

The CAGR of total annual disbursements over the same period was 3.89 percent, reflecting the gradual increase in the average monthly disability benefit, which rose from $1,146 in January 2014 to $1,537 in January 2024, a ten-year CAGR of 2.98 percent for the average benefit amount.


SECTION 5: INFLATION-ADJUSTED CAGR ANALYSIS

To provide a more accurate picture of real purchasing power and fund sustainability, the Division of Actuarial and Financial Analysis computed inflation-adjusted reserve values using the Bureau of Economic Analysis GDP Implicit Price Deflator. Using FY2014 as the base year (index = 100.0), the FY2024 deflator index stands at 121.7, reflecting cumulative inflation of 21.7 percent over the measurement period.

Inflation-adjusted reserve values:
- FY2014 (base year): $60.4 billion
- FY2024 (real, 2014 dollars): $70.4 billion ($85.7 billion / 1.217)

Real CAGR = ($70.4 billion / $60.4 billion) ^ (1/10) - 1
Real CAGR = (1.1656) ^ (0.10) - 1
Real CAGR = 1.01554 - 1
Real CAGR = 0.01554, or approximately 1.12 percent (inflation-adjusted)

This real CAGR of 1.12 percent, while positive, underscores the relatively modest growth in the fund's real purchasing power and reinforces the long-term sustainability concerns raised in the 2024 Annual Report of the Board of Trustees, which projected DI Trust Fund reserve depletion by 2035 under intermediate assumptions if no legislative action is taken.


SECTION 6: COMPARATIVE ANALYSIS WITH OASI TRUST FUND

For context, the Old-Age and Survivors Insurance Trust Fund reported the following CAGR metrics over the same ten-year period:

- OASI nominal reserve CAGR (FY2014–FY2024): -2.14 percent
- OASI beginning reserves (FY2014): $2,764.4 billion
- OASI ending reserves (FY2024): $2,218.6 billion
- OASI real CAGR (inflation-adjusted): -3.19 percent

The contrasting trajectories of the two funds reflect fundamentally different demographic and economic pressures. The DI Trust Fund's positive CAGR is attributable in part to a 14.3 percent decline in the number of disability beneficiaries between 2014 and 2024, from approximately 10.9 million to 9.3 million, driven by increased continuing disability reviews, the aging of beneficiaries into retirement status, and reduced incidence rates among younger cohorts. The OASI Trust Fund, by contrast, has faced accelerating disbursement growth as the Baby Boom generation reaches full retirement age.


SECTION 7: SENSITIVITY ANALYSIS

The Division conducted sensitivity testing on the CAGR computation using three alternative scenarios for the beginning value, reflecting different methodological choices regarding how to treat the pre-reallocation reserve level:

Scenario A (Base Case): Beginning value = FY2014 actual year-end balance of $60.4 billion
Result: CAGR = 3.47 percent

Scenario B (Pre-Crisis Peak): Beginning value = FY2013 year-end balance of $88.1 billion
Result: CAGR = -0.27 percent (reflecting that reserves have not fully recovered to pre-crisis levels in nominal terms)

Scenario C (Post-Reallocation Trough): Beginning value = FY2015 year-end balance of $32.3 billion
Result: CAGR = 10.24 percent (nine-year period, FY2015–FY2024)

The base case (Scenario A) is the primary figure used throughout this report and in all official communications, as it represents the most complete ten-year measurement window aligned with the reporting mandate.


SECTION 8: CONCLUSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS

The Federal Disability Insurance Trust Fund has demonstrated a nominal CAGR of 3.47 percent over the ten-year period from FY2014 through FY2024, recovering from the near-depletion crisis of 2015–2016. However, the real CAGR of 1.12 percent and the projected reserve depletion date of 2035 under current law indicate that the fund's long-term financial health remains a matter requiring legislative attention.

The Office of Trust Fund Management recommends:

1. That the Social Security Subcommittees of the Senate Finance Committee and the House Ways and Means Committee initiate hearings no later than the third quarter of FY2025 to consider payroll tax rate adjustments or benefit modification proposals.

2. That the Office of Management and Budget incorporate the 3.47 percent nominal CAGR and 1.12 percent real CAGR figures into the FY2026 Budget Appendix for the Social Security Administration.

3. That the Board of Trustees' 2025 Annual Report include a dedicated section addressing the divergence between nominal and real CAGR metrics and their implications for the 75-year actuarial balance.
