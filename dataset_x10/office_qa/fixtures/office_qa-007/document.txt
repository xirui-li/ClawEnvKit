EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The Commonwealth of Pennsylvania's General Fund revenues and expenditures for the third quarter of Fiscal Year 2023-2024 (January 1, 2024 through March 31, 2024) reflect continued fiscal stability with modest revenue growth compared to the second quarter. Total General Fund revenues for Q3 FY2023-24 reached $11.84 billion, representing a 3.7 percent increase over Q2 FY2023-24 revenues of $11.42 billion. Total expenditures for the same period were $10.97 billion, up 2.1 percent from Q2 expenditures of $10.74 billion. The resulting quarterly surplus of $870 million contributes to a year-to-date General Fund balance of $2.31 billion as of March 31, 2024.

Secretary of the Budget Uri Monson noted that the quarter's performance reflects stronger-than-anticipated personal income tax collections driven by robust labor market conditions in the Philadelphia and Pittsburgh metropolitan areas, partially offset by softer corporate net income tax receipts.

1.2 Key Revenue Observations

Personal Income Tax (PIT): PIT collections of $4.491 billion in Q3 represent the strongest quarterly PIT performance in the current fiscal year. The 6.5 percent quarter-over-quarter increase is attributable to higher withholding receipts, which grew 5.2 percent to $3.84 billion, reflecting Pennsylvania's unemployment rate declining to 3.4 percent in February 2024, the lowest level since October 2019. Estimated payments also increased 9.1 percent compared to Q2, suggesting stronger self-employment and investment income activity.

Sales and Use Tax (SUT): SUT collections of $3.188 billion increased 2.7 percent from Q2. January and February collections were below the quarterly trend, consistent with typical post-holiday consumer spending patterns. March collections of $1.142 billion recovered strongly, up 4.8 percent from March 2023, driven by increased vehicle sales and online marketplace transactions subject to the state's 6 percent sales tax rate.

Corporate Net Income Tax (CNIT): CNIT receipts declined 4.9 percent quarter-over-quarter to $1.544 billion. This decline was anticipated, as Q2 typically captures larger estimated payments from calendar-year filers. The Q3 figure nonetheless exceeded the Governor's budget estimate of $1.487 billion by $57.1 million, or 3.8 percent. Pennsylvania's CNIT rate was reduced from 9.99 percent to 8.99 percent effective January 1, 2024, as part of the phased reduction schedule established under Act 53 of 2022.

Non-Tax Revenue: The significant 76.4 percent quarter-over-quarter increase in non-tax revenue, from $376.8 million to $664.8 million, reflects the receipt of $312.4 million in federal reimbursements related to Medicaid administrative costs that were delayed from Q2 due to federal processing timelines. Excluding this timing adjustment, non-tax revenue grew 2.7 percent quarter-over-quarter.

2.2 Key Expenditure Observations

Human Services: The Department of Human Services accounted for the largest dollar increase in expenditures, rising $176.6 million (4.1 percent) to $4.489 billion. The primary driver was Medical Assistance (Medicaid) spending, which increased $148.3 million quarter-over-quarter due to the completion of the federally mandated Medicaid redetermination process. Pennsylvania completed redeterminations for approximately 3.2 million enrollees between April 2023 and January 2024, and the Q3 caseload stabilized at 3.41 million individuals, compared to 3.38 million at the end of Q2.

Education: PreK-12 education expenditures of $2.204 billion reflect the second quarterly disbursement of Basic Education Funding to Pennsylvania's 500 school districts. The slight 0.8 percent increase reflects mid-year adjustments to special education funding allocations totaling $18.9 million, partially offset by lower administrative costs.

Debt Service: Debt service payments increased 2.4 percent to $912.6 million, consistent with the Commonwealth's debt service schedule. Pennsylvania's outstanding General Obligation debt as of March 31, 2024 stood at $10.87 billion, down from $11.14 billion at the same point in FY2022-23, reflecting the Commonwealth's continued commitment to debt reduction.

General Government: General government expenditures declined 4.5 percent, or $27.6 million, primarily due to the timing of information technology infrastructure payments that were accelerated into Q2 under a contract with Unisys Corporation for mainframe modernization services.

Through three quarters of FY2023-24, revenues are tracking 1.4 percent above the Governor's budget estimate on a prorated basis. The Bureau of Revenue Estimation projects full-year General Fund revenues will reach $44.61 billion, exceeding the enacted budget estimate by $240 million.

3.2 Rainy Day Fund Status

The Commonwealth's Budget Stabilization Reserve Fund (Rainy Day Fund) balance as of March 31, 2024 was $6.23 billion, unchanged from December 31, 2023. This represents approximately 14.1 percent of annual General Fund expenditures, well above the Government Finance Officers Association's recommended minimum of 16.7 percent of annual expenditures (equivalent to two months of operating costs). Pennsylvania's Rainy Day Fund balance ranks among the top ten largest state reserve funds in the nation as a percentage of general fund spending, according to the Pew Charitable Trusts' most recent state fiscal health assessment published in February 2024.

SECTION 4: SPECIAL FUNDS AND MOTOR LICENSE FUND

Motor License Fund revenues for Q3 FY2023-24 totaled $1.14 billion, a decrease of 1.8 percent from Q2's $1.16 billion. Motor fuel tax collections of $487.3 million declined 3.2 percent, reflecting lower gasoline consumption consistent with seasonal patterns and continued growth in electric vehicle registrations, which reached 87,400 registered EVs in Pennsylvania as of March 2024, a 22.6 percent increase from March 2023. Vehicle registration and licensing fees contributed $312.8 million, essentially flat with Q2.

The Pennsylvania Lottery Fund transferred $286.4 million to support senior programs in Q3, a 4.3 percent increase over Q2's $274.6 million transfer, driven by strong instant ticket sales following the introduction of the "$5 Million Jackpot" scratch-off game in January 2024.

SECTION 5: OUTLOOK FOR Q4 FY2023-24

The fourth quarter (April 1 – June 30, 2024) is historically the Commonwealth's strongest revenue quarter, driven by final personal income tax payments due April 15 and corporate estimated payments due in April and June. The Bureau of Revenue Estimation projects Q4 General Fund revenues of approximately $12.76 billion, which would represent a 7.8 percent increase over Q3 and would bring full-year revenues to $44.61 billion.

Key risks to the Q4 outlook include:
- Potential softening in capital gains realizations if equity market volatility persists
- Uncertainty regarding the timing of approximately $180 million in federal broadband infrastructure reimbursements under the BEAD Program
- The impact of the March 22, 2024 Francis Scott Key Bridge collapse in Baltimore on regional supply chains and commercial activity in southeastern Pennsylvania

The next quarterly financial report, covering Q4 FY2023-24, will be released on or about July 25, 2024.
