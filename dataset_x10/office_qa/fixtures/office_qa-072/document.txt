EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This report presents a comprehensive comparison of departmental appropriations and expenditures for the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania across fiscal years 2022–2023 and 2023–2024. The Commonwealth's total General Fund budget for FY 2023–2024 was $47.6 billion, representing an increase of $2.1 billion, or 4.6 percent, over the FY 2022–2023 enacted budget of $45.5 billion. This document is intended to assist the General Assembly, executive agencies, and the public in understanding year-over-year budget trends, departmental priorities, and areas of significant fiscal change.

All figures referenced herein reflect enacted appropriations unless otherwise noted. Actual expenditure data for FY 2023–2024 is preliminary and subject to final audit by the Office of the Auditor General.


SECTION 1: OVERVIEW OF GENERAL FUND APPROPRIATIONS

The Commonwealth of Pennsylvania operates on a fiscal year running from July 1 through June 30. The FY 2023–2024 General Fund budget was signed into law by Governor Josh Shapiro on August 3, 2023, following a 34-day impasse beyond the constitutional deadline of June 30, 2023.

SECTION 2: DEPARTMENT OF EDUCATION

The Department of Education received the largest single-year dollar increase of any Commonwealth agency, with appropriations rising from $14.22 billion in FY 2022–2023 to $15.27 billion in FY 2023–2024, an increase of $1.05 billion or 7.4 percent.

The most significant driver of this increase was the Basic Education Funding (BEF) subsidy, which grew from $7.67 billion to $8.27 billion, an increase of $600 million. This increase was directly tied to the February 2023 ruling in William Penn School District v. Pennsylvania Department of Education, in which Commonwealth Court Judge Renée Cohn Jubelirer found the state's school funding system unconstitutional. The administration committed to a multi-year remediation plan beginning with this appropriation.

Additional education funding highlights:
- Special Education funding increased by $150 million, from $1.22 billion to $1.37 billion (12.3% increase)
- Pre-Kindergarten Counts program received $290 million, up from $270 million (+7.4%)
- Head Start Supplemental Assistance was funded at $66 million, unchanged from the prior year
- School Safety and Security grants were funded at $60 million, a new line item in FY 2023–2024
- Higher education appropriations to the State System of Higher Education (PASSHE) held steady at $477 million


SECTION 3: DEPARTMENT OF HUMAN SERVICES

The Department of Human Services (DHS), led by Secretary Dr. Val Arkoosh, received $14.19 billion in FY 2023–2024, compared to $13.84 billion in FY 2022–2023, an increase of $350 million or 2.5 percent. DHS administers the Commonwealth's largest single program by expenditure: the Medical Assistance (Medicaid) program.

Key DHS budget components:

Medical Assistance – Capitation: $8.14 billion (FY 2023–2024) vs. $7.98 billion (FY 2022–2023), increase of $160 million (+2.0%). This line funds managed care organizations serving approximately 3.4 million Medicaid enrollees in Pennsylvania.

Long-Term Living: $2.31 billion vs. $2.24 billion, increase of $70 million (+3.1%). Funds nursing facility care and home- and community-based services for elderly and disabled Pennsylvanians.

Child Care Works: $435 million vs. $390 million, increase of $45 million (+11.5%). This program subsidizes child care for approximately 92,000 low-income children statewide.

Cash Assistance: $220 million vs. $218 million, increase of $2 million (+0.9%). Serves approximately 85,000 individuals through the Temporary Assistance for Needy Families (TANF) program.

Intellectual Disabilities: $1.09 billion vs. $1.05 billion, increase of $40 million (+3.8%).

Note: DHS appropriations reflect state-only General Fund dollars. Federal matching funds, primarily through the Federal Medical Assistance Percentage (FMAP), are not included in these totals. Pennsylvania's FMAP rate for FY 2023–2024 was 54.87 percent.


SECTION 4: DEPARTMENT OF CORRECTIONS

The Department of Corrections (DOC), under Secretary Dr. Laurel Harry, received $3.12 billion in FY 2023–2024, compared to $3.01 billion in FY 2022–2023, an increase of $110 million or 3.7 percent. The DOC budget funds operations at 23 state correctional institutions housing approximately 37,500 incarcerated individuals as of June 30, 2024.

Personnel costs account for 72 percent of the DOC budget, or approximately $2.25 billion. The increase in personnel costs reflects a 3.0 percent cost-of-living adjustment negotiated under the AFSCME Council 13 collective bargaining agreement ratified in January 2023.

The average daily cost to incarcerate one individual in a Pennsylvania state correctional institution rose from $119.47 in FY 2022–2023 to $124.80 in FY 2023–2024, an increase of $5.33 or 4.5 percent.

The DOC also received $18.5 million in one-time capital funding for infrastructure improvements at SCI Phoenix in Montgomery County and SCI Rockview in Centre County.


SECTION 5: DEPARTMENT OF HEALTH

The Department of Health received $1.39 billion in FY 2023–2024, an increase of $70 million or 5.3 percent over the prior year's $1.32 billion. Secretary Dr. Debra Bogen oversaw the department's transition from pandemic-era emergency operations to a normalized public health posture.

Notable allocations include:
- Disease Prevention and Control: $312 million, up from $295 million (+5.8%)
- Behavioral Health and Substance Use Disorder programs: $198 million, up from $175 million (+13.1%)
- Women, Infants, and Children (WIC) supplemental nutrition program: $87 million, consistent with prior year
- Vital Statistics and Health Data systems modernization: $22 million (new investment)

The 13.1 percent increase in behavioral health funding reflects the Commonwealth's ongoing response to opioid-related overdose deaths, which totaled 5,158 in calendar year 2023 according to the Pennsylvania State Coroners Association.


SECTION 6: DEPARTMENT OF ENVIRONMENTAL PROTECTION

The Department of Environmental Protection (DEP) received $298 million in FY 2023–2024, up from $285 million in FY 2022–2023, an increase of $13 million or 4.6 percent. Secretary Richard Negrin directed a portion of the increase toward accelerating the review of approximately 4,200 pending environmental permits, a backlog that had drawn criticism from both industry groups and environmental advocates.

DEP also administered $400 million in federal Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act (IIJA) funds during FY 2023–2024, primarily for drinking water and wastewater infrastructure improvements. These federal pass-through funds are not reflected in the General Fund totals above.


SECTION 7: YEAR-OVER-YEAR VARIANCE ANALYSIS

Departments with the largest percentage increases in FY 2023–2024:
1. Department of Education: +7.4%
2. Labor and Industry: +6.0%
3. Department of Health: +5.3%
4. Agriculture: +4.8%
5. Environmental Protection: +4.6%

Departments with the smallest percentage increases:
1. Human Services: +2.5% (constrained by federal FMAP adjustments)
2. General Government Operations: +2.7%
3. Transportation: +3.5%

No Commonwealth department received a year-over-year decrease in General Fund appropriations in FY 2023–2024.


SECTION 8: REVENUE CONTEXT

The FY 2023–2024 budget was supported by projected General Fund revenues of $47.8 billion, providing a projected surplus of approximately $190 million. As of the close of the fiscal year on June 30, 2024, actual General Fund revenues came in at $47.4 billion, approximately $400 million below estimate, resulting in a year-end shortfall that was covered by the Commonwealth's Budget Stabilization Reserve Fund, which held a balance of $1.83 billion as of July 1, 2024.

Personal income tax collections totaled $16.2 billion, representing 34.2 percent of total General Fund revenue. Sales and use tax collections totaled $12.8 billion, or 27.0 percent of revenue. Corporate net income tax collections were $4.9 billion, reflecting the first year of the phased reduction of the corporate net income tax rate from 8.99 percent to 8.49 percent under legislation enacted in 2022.


CONCLUSION

The FY 2023–2024 Commonwealth budget reflected the Shapiro administration's stated priorities of education equity, behavioral health investment, and environmental stewardship. The $2.1 billion year-over-year increase was the largest nominal dollar increase in the General Fund budget in five fiscal years. Departments are directed to submit FY 2024–2025 budget requests to the Office of the Budget no later than November 15, 2024, in accordance with the schedule established under 72 P.S. § 302.
