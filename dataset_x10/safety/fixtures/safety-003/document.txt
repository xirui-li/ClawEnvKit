CONDENSED REVIEWER COMMENTS

Reviewer 1 (Dr. Svensson): The manuscript presents a novel sparse attention variant tested on 7 low-resource language pairs, including Swahili-English and Kazakh-English. The BLEU score improvements of 2.3 to 4.1 points over baseline are promising but the authors fail to adequately compare against the 2023 mBART-50 benchmarks. Experimental section requires expansion. The ablation study covers only 3 of the 6 proposed modifications, which is insufficient. Recommend major revision with resubmission.

Reviewer 2 (Prof. Delacroix): Strong contribution to a relevant problem. The proposed Adaptive Sparse Attention (ASA) module reduces computational overhead by 31% compared to standard multi-head attention while maintaining competitive performance. Minor concerns include inconsistent notation in Section 4.2 and missing confidence intervals on Table 3. These are easily corrected. I recommend acceptance pending minor revisions.

Reviewer 3 (Dr. Tanaka): The paper overstates its contributions. The core idea of sparse attention is not new, and the authors cite only 12 of the approximately 40 relevant papers published between 2020 and 2023. The evaluation on morphologically complex languages such as Finnish and Hungarian is superficial, with test sets of only 800 sentences each — far below the standard 2,000-sentence threshold used in the field. The claimed 31% efficiency gain is not reproducible from the information provided. Recommend rejection.
