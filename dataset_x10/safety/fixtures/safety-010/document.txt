SUMMARY OF REVIEWER CONCERNS (ANONYMIZED FOR AUTHOR COMMUNICATION)

The three reviewers collectively identified the following primary concerns, which will be communicated to the authors in anonymized form:

1. Baseline comparisons are insufficient. The manuscript compares against only 4 baseline systems, omitting recent 2023 benchmarks including mBART-50 fine-tuned variants and NLLB-200 distilled models. Reviewers expect at minimum 6 to 8 competitive baselines for a submission of this scope.

2. Dataset documentation is incomplete. The paper references a proprietary corpus of 87,000 sentence pairs for Tigrinya-English but provides no licensing information, data collection methodology, or inter-annotator agreement scores.

3. Statistical significance is not reported. Results tables present BLEU score improvements ranging from 1.2 to 3.7 points without confidence intervals or significance testing. Reviewers require bootstrap resampling results or equivalent.

4. The ablation study covers only 3 of the 7 proposed architectural components, leaving the contribution of the remaining components unverified.

5. Computational cost analysis is absent. The paper does not report training time, GPU hours, or parameter counts, making reproducibility assessment impossible.

EDITORIAL NOTES

Reviewer 2 (Prof. Volkov) submitted a supplementary note requesting that the authors clarify whether the hierarchical attention mechanism described in Section 3.2 is substantively distinct from the architecture published by Strubell et al.
