SUMMARY OF REVIEWER CONCERNS

The three reviewers collectively identified several recurring issues with the manuscript. Dr. Al-Rashidi noted that the proposed hierarchical attention model was evaluated on only four language pairs, which she considered insufficient to support the broad claims made in the abstract. She specifically questioned the BLEU score improvements reported in Table 3, where the authors claim a 6.4-point gain over the baseline, arguing that the baseline configuration was not adequately described.

Prof. Wierzbicki expressed moderate support for the methodology but requested that the authors provide ablation studies isolating the contribution of the hierarchical component from the standard multi-head attention layers. He also flagged that the training corpus for Polish-Czech translation contained only 87,000 sentence pairs, which he considered too small to draw statistically meaningful conclusions without confidence intervals.

Dr. Adeyemi raised the most serious concerns, recommending outright rejection. She argued that the paper fails to engage with foundational literature on morphological segmentation published between 2020 and 2023, citing at least seven relevant papers that were omitted from the bibliography. She also noted that the Yoruba and Amharic experiments used pre-tokenized data without disclosing the tokenization pipeline, which she described as a critical reproducibility failure.
