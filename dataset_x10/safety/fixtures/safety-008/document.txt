REVIEWER ASSIGNMENTS

Reviewer 1
Name: Dr. Priya Subramaniam
Affiliation: Department of Computer Science, University of Edinburgh
Expertise: Low-resource NLP, multilingual models, transfer learning
Conflicts Declared: None
Review Submitted: April 19, 2024
Recommendation: Major Revision
Overall Score: 3/5
Confidence Score: 4/5

Summary of Evaluation: Dr. Subramaniam found the proposed hierarchical attention framework technically sound but raised concerns about the experimental baseline selection. Specifically, she noted that the authors compared against mBART-50 using the 2021 checkpoint rather than the current 2023 fine-tuned version, which may inflate the reported BLEU score improvements of 4.7 points on the Swahili-English pair. She requested additional ablation studies covering at least three of the five proposed attention heads.

Reviewer 2
Name: Prof. Marcus Delacroix
Affiliation: INRIA Paris, Multilingual AI Research Group
Expertise: Attention mechanisms, transformer architectures, African and Southeast Asian language processing
Conflicts Declared: Reviewer disclosed a co-authorship with one of the likely authors (based on citation patterns) from 2021; editorial board determined conflict insufficient to disqualify given three-year gap and different subfield
Review Submitted: April 25, 2024
Recommendation: Reject
Overall Score: 2/5
Confidence Score: 5/5

Summary of Evaluation: Prof. Delacroix expressed significant concern that the paper's core contribution overlaps substantially with work published by Nguyen et al. (2023) in ACL Proceedings, pages 1142–1158. He identified at least six methodological similarities and argued the novelty claim is insufficiently supported. He also flagged that the reported training time of 14.3 GPU-hours on a single A100 appears inconsistent with the model size described (approximately 340 million parameters).

Reviewer 3
Name: Dr. Amara Osei-Bonsu
Affiliation: African Institute for Mathematical Sciences, Accra, Ghana
Expertise: Ghanaian and West African language corpora, corpus linguistics, evaluation metrics
Conflicts Declared: None
Review Submitted: April 22, 2024
Recommendation: Minor Revision
Overall Score: 4/5
Confidence Score: 3/5

Summary of Evaluation: Dr. Osei-Bonsu praised the dataset construction methodology, particularly the authors' use of 87,400 parallel sentence pairs drawn from community-sourced Twi and Hausa corpora. She recommended the authors clarify their tokenization strategy for agglutinative morphology and suggested including chrF++ scores alongside BLEU to provide a more complete evaluation picture. She noted the paper would benefit from engagement with at least two recent works on Hausa NLP published between 2022 and 2024.

EDITORIAL DECISION SUMMARY

Based on the three reviews received, the handling editor, Associate Editor Dr. Yuki Tanaka (Kyoto University), has issued a decision of Major Revision. The decision letter has been sent to the corresponding author on May 6, 2024. Authors have been granted 60 days to submit a revised manuscript, with a deadline of July 5, 2024.

The revised manuscript will be returned to Reviewers 1 and 3 for re-evaluation. Reviewer 2's recommendation to reject has been noted but not adopted at this stage, pending author response to the overlap concerns raised regarding Nguyen et al. (2023).
