REVIEWER ASSIGNMENTS

Reviewer 1
Name: Dr. Priya Raghunathan
Affiliation: Department of Linguistics and Cognitive Science, University of Edinburgh
Expertise: Low-resource NLP, morphological analysis, transformer architectures
Conflict of Interest Check Completed: Yes — No conflicts identified
Review Submitted: April 22, 2024
Recommendation: Major Revision
Overall Score: 3.1 out of 5.0

Summary of Reviewer 1 Comments:
Dr. Raghunathan found the proposed hierarchical attention framework theoretically sound but raised concerns about the experimental design. Specifically, she noted that the evaluation was conducted on only three language pairs (Swahili-English, Finnish-English, and Quechua-Spanish), which she considers insufficient to support the broad claims made in the abstract. She requested that the authors include at least two additional language pairs and provide statistical significance testing across all BLEU score comparisons. She also flagged that Table 3 contains a likely transcription error, as the reported BLEU score of 41.7 for the Quechua-Spanish baseline appears inconsistent with established benchmarks in the literature.

Reviewer 2
Name: Professor Marcus Delacroix
Affiliation: INRIA Paris, Computational Linguistics Group
Expertise: Neural machine translation, attention mechanisms, multilingual models
Conflict of Interest Check Completed: Yes — No conflicts identified
Review Submitted: April 19, 2024
Recommendation: Accept with Minor Revisions
Overall Score: 4.2 out of 5.0

Summary of Reviewer 2 Comments:
Professor Delacroix praised the novelty of the hierarchical attention design and described the ablation study in Section 4.3 as particularly rigorous. He requested minor clarifications regarding the computational overhead introduced by the proposed method, noting that the authors report a 23% increase in training time without adequately discussing whether this cost is justified in deployment scenarios. He also suggested that the authors cite recent work by Anastasopoulos et al. (2023) on morpheme-aware tokenization, which is directly relevant to the methodology.

Reviewer 3
Name: Dr. Yuki Tanaka
Affiliation: Tokyo Institute of Technology, Natural Language Processing Laboratory
Expertise: Morphologically rich languages, sequence-to-sequence models, evaluation metrics
Conflict of Interest Check Completed: Yes — Potential conflict flagged (Dr. Tanaka co-authored a 2022 paper with one of the manuscript's authors, Dr. Fernanda Castillo). Conflict reviewed and waived by Editor-in-Chief on March 29, 2024, as the prior collaboration was deemed sufficiently distant.
Review Submitted: April 25, 2024
Recommendation: Reject
Overall Score: 2.4 out of 5.0

Summary of Reviewer 3 Comments:
Dr. Tanaka expressed serious reservations about the reproducibility of the reported results. He noted that the authors do not release their training data splits or preprocessing scripts, and that the described hyperparameter configuration (learning rate of 0.00015, dropout of 0.3, batch size of 4,096 tokens) differs from what is described in the referenced codebase without explanation. He also questioned whether the 6.3 BLEU point improvement over the baseline on Finnish-English is statistically meaningful given the small test set size of 1,200 sentences.
