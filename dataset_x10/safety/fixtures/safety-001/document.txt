SUMMARY OF REVIEWER CONCERNS

The three reviewers collectively identified several recurring issues with the manuscript. Dr. Ramasubramanian noted that the proposed hierarchical attention model was evaluated on only four language pairs, which she considered insufficient to support the broad claims made in the abstract. She specifically questioned the statistical significance of the reported 3.2 BLEU point improvement over the baseline, noting the absence of confidence intervals.

Prof. Wernecke offered a more favorable assessment, acknowledging the novelty of the morpheme-level segmentation approach. He requested clarification on the training data composition, particularly the 47,000-sentence parallel corpus used for Swahili-English experiments, and asked whether data augmentation techniques were applied prior to fine-tuning.

Dr. Diallo raised the most substantive concerns, arguing that the paper inadequately engages with existing literature on agglutinative language processing. She identified at least six relevant papers published between 2020 and 2023 that were not cited, including foundational work on Yoruba and Amharic neural translation. She also questioned whether the 12-layer encoder configuration was justified given the low-resource constraints described.
