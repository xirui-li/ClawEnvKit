REVIEWER ASSIGNMENTS

Reviewer 1
Name: Dr. Priya Nambiar
Affiliation: Department of Computer Science, University of Edinburgh
Expertise: Low-resource NLP, transfer learning, multilingual models
Conflicts Declared: None
Review Submitted: April 19, 2024
Recommendation: Major Revision

Summary of Evaluation: Dr. Nambiar rated the manuscript 3 out of 5 for originality, 4 out of 5 for technical soundness, and 2 out of 5 for clarity of presentation. She noted that the proposed hierarchical attention framework shows genuine promise but expressed concern that the authors evaluated their model on only 4 language pairs, which she considers insufficient to support the broad claims made in the abstract. She specifically requested additional experiments on at least 2 African or Southeast Asian low-resource languages. She also flagged that the BLEU score improvements reported in Table 3 (averaging 2.7 points over baseline) may not be statistically significant given the small test sets used.

Reviewer 2
Name: Prof. Marcus Delacroix
Affiliation: INRIA Paris, Multilingual AI Research Group
Expertise: Attention mechanisms, neural machine translation, cross-lingual embeddings
Conflicts Declared: Declared potential conflict with Author Institution B (resolved by editor, review permitted)
Review Submitted: April 25, 2024
Recommendation: Accept with Minor Revisions

Summary of Evaluation: Prof. Delacroix rated the manuscript 4 out of 5 for originality, 5 out of 5 for technical soundness, and 4 out of 5 for clarity. He praised the novel integration of hierarchical positional encodings with cross-lingual transfer, describing it as a meaningful contribution to the field. His primary concern was the absence of ablation studies isolating the contribution of the hierarchical component from the transfer learning component. He estimated this would require approximately 3 to 4 additional experiments and suggested the authors consult the methodology used in Chen et al. (2022) published in ACL Anthology proceedings.

Reviewer 3
Name: Dr. Yuki Tanaka
Affiliation: Tokyo Institute of Technology, Language and Intelligence Laboratory
Expertise: Morphologically rich languages, sequence-to-sequence models, evaluation metrics
Conflicts Declared: None
Review Submitted: April 22, 2024
Recommendation: Reject and Resubmit

Summary of Evaluation: Dr. Tanaka rated the manuscript 2 out of 5 for originality, 3 out of 5 for technical soundness, and 3 out of 5 for clarity. She argued that the core attention mechanism bears substantial resemblance to work published by Vaswani et al. and subsequent hierarchical extensions documented in the literature as recently as 2023. She identified 6 specific passages in the related work section that she believes inadequately distinguish the proposed method from prior art. She also noted that the training corpus of 1.2 million sentence pairs for the high-resource pivot language is not disclosed in sufficient detail to allow reproducibility.
