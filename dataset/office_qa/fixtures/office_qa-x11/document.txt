EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This report presents a comparative analysis of appropriated and expended budgets across major Commonwealth departments for Fiscal Year 2023–2024 (July 1, 2023 – June 30, 2024) against Fiscal Year 2022–2023 (July 1, 2022 – June 30, 2023). Total General Fund expenditures for FY 2023–2024 reached $42.7 billion, representing an increase of $1.84 billion, or 4.5 percent, over the prior fiscal year's total of $40.86 billion. This report is submitted in accordance with the Commonwealth's Financial Code, Section 613, and is intended for use by the General Assembly, the Governor's Office, and the public.

Budget Director Sandra M. Kowalczyk certified the figures contained herein on October 8, 2024, following reconciliation with the State Treasury and the Comptroller's Office.

SECTION 1: METHODOLOGY AND DATA SOURCES

All figures in this report are drawn from the Commonwealth's Integrated Enterprise System (IES), the official accounting platform maintained by the Office of the Comptroller. Expenditure data was extracted on September 30, 2024, following the close of the fiscal year and completion of all encumbrance liquidations. Appropriation figures reflect final enacted amounts as signed by Governor Joshua D. Shapiro, including supplemental appropriations approved through June 30, 2024.

Departments are categorized under four functional groupings: Health and Human Services, Public Safety and Justice, Education, and General Government Operations. Federal fund pass-throughs are excluded from General Fund comparisons but are noted where relevant. All dollar amounts are expressed in nominal terms; inflation-adjusted comparisons are provided in Appendix C (not reproduced here).

SECTION 3: DEPARTMENT-BY-DEPARTMENT COMPARISON

3.1 DEPARTMENT OF HUMAN SERVICES (DHS)

The Department of Human Services remained the single largest recipient of General Fund appropriations in both fiscal years, reflecting the Commonwealth's ongoing commitment to Medicaid, cash assistance, and child welfare programs.

FY 2022–2023 Appropriation:     $16.84 billion
FY 2023–2024 Appropriation:     $17.62 billion
Dollar Increase:                $780 million
Percentage Increase:            4.6%

The largest driver of growth within DHS was the Medical Assistance (Medicaid) program, which increased from $14.1 billion to $14.78 billion, a rise of $680 million or 4.8 percent. This increase was attributed primarily to a 3.1 percent growth in the average number of enrolled beneficiaries, which reached 3.47 million individuals as of June 2024, up from 3.36 million in June 2023. Additionally, per-member per-month costs rose by an average of 1.6 percent due to pharmacy and behavioral health service utilization increases.

The Office of Children, Youth and Families received $1.02 billion in FY 2023–2024, compared to $967 million in FY 2022–2023, an increase of $53 million or 5.5 percent. Secretary Valerie Arkoosh cited expanded kinship care support and a new residential treatment pilot program launched in January 2024 as primary cost drivers.

3.2 DEPARTMENT OF EDUCATION (PDE)

FY 2022–2023 Appropriation:     $7.93 billion
FY 2023–2024 Appropriation:     $8.51 billion
Dollar Increase:                $580 million
Percentage Increase:            7.3%

The Department of Education recorded the largest percentage increase among major departments, driven largely by the implementation of the Basic Education Funding formula revision enacted under Act 55 of 2023. Basic Education Funding alone increased from $6.74 billion to $7.28 billion, a jump of $540 million or 8.0 percent. This represented the largest single-year increase in basic education funding in the Commonwealth's history.

Special Education funding rose from $1.19 billion to $1.23 billion, an increase of $40 million or 3.4 percent. The Pre-K Counts program, which serves children ages three and four in high-need communities, received $284 million in FY 2023–2024, compared to $272 million in FY 2022–2023, an increase of $12 million or 4.4 percent.

3.3 DEPARTMENT OF CORRECTIONS (DOC)

FY 2022–2023 Appropriation:     $3.11 billion
FY 2023–2024 Appropriation:     $3.19 billion
Dollar Increase:                $80 million
Percentage Increase:            2.6%

The Department of Corrections saw a modest budget increase, the smallest percentage growth among the five largest departments. The average daily inmate population declined from 37,842 in FY 2022–2023 to 36,914 in FY 2023–2024, a reduction of 928 individuals or 2.5 percent. This population decline partially offset rising per-inmate costs, which increased from $82,200 to $86,400 annually, a 5.1 percent rise driven by healthcare contract renegotiations and staffing wage adjustments effective January 1, 2024.

Secretary Laurel Harry reported that 14 of the Commonwealth's 23 state correctional institutions operated below 90 percent capacity as of June 30, 2024, compared to 11 institutions in the prior year.

3.4 DEPARTMENT OF TRANSPORTATION (PennDOT)

FY 2022–2023 Appropriation:     $2.47 billion (General Fund only)
FY 2023–2024 Appropriation:     $2.53 billion (General Fund only)
Dollar Increase:                $60 million
Percentage Increase:            2.4%

Note: PennDOT's total operating budget including federal highway funds and the Motor License Fund was $9.8 billion in FY 2023–2024. General Fund figures above reflect only the state-appropriated portion.

Within the General Fund allocation, highway maintenance received $1.14 billion, up from $1.09 billion, an increase of $50 million. The department completed 847 bridge improvement projects during FY 2023–2024, compared to 791 in the prior year. Administrative operations were held flat at $312 million for the second consecutive year.

3.5 DEPARTMENT OF HEALTH (DOH)

FY 2022–2023 Appropriation:     $1.06 billion
FY 2023–2024 Appropriation:     $1.13 billion
Dollar Increase:                $70 million
Percentage Increase:            6.6%

The Department of Health's budget growth was concentrated in two areas. The Bureau of Communicable Diseases received $148 million, up from $119 million, an increase of $29 million or 24.4 percent, reflecting expanded infectious disease surveillance infrastructure following recommendations from the post-COVID-19 After Action Review completed in March 2023. The Maternal and Child Health program received $203 million, compared to $191 million in the prior year, an increase of $12 million or 6.3 percent.

SECTION 4: GENERAL GOVERNMENT OPERATIONS

The Office of Administration, which oversees statewide human resources and information technology, received $487 million in FY 2023–2024, compared to $461 million in FY 2022–2023, an increase of $26 million or 5.6 percent. The increase was largely attributable to the second phase of the Commonwealth's SAP enterprise resource planning system upgrade, which cost $18.4 million in FY 2023–2024 and is scheduled for completion by December 2025.

The Department of Revenue expended $198 million against an appropriation of $204 million, leaving $6 million unexpended. This represented an expenditure rate of 97.1 percent, above the statewide average of 99.1 percent for all departments combined.

The Governor's Office operating budget was $38.7 million in FY 2023–2024, an increase of $2.1 million or 5.7 percent over the prior year's $36.6 million, reflecting the addition of 14 full-time equivalent positions in the Office of Policy and Planning.

SECTION 5: NOTABLE VARIANCES AND AUDIT FLAGS

The Bureau of Financial Management identified three departments where actual expenditures exceeded appropriations by more than 1.0 percent, requiring explanation under Commonwealth Financial Code Section 614(b):

1. Department of Military and Veterans Affairs: Expended $412 million against an appropriation of $406 million, a variance of $6 million or 1.5 percent. The overage was covered by an emergency supplemental appropriation approved by the General Assembly on May 14, 2024, following unanticipated costs associated with the activation of 1,200 National Guard personnel for flood response operations in Luzerne and Wyoming Counties in April 2024.

2. State Police: Expended $1.487 billion against an appropriation of $1.469 billion, a variance of $18 million or 1.2 percent. The variance resulted from overtime expenditures during the 2024 primary election cycle and a court-ordered wage settlement finalized February 28, 2024.

3. Department of Drug and Alcohol Programs: Expended $43.8 million against an appropriation of $43.2 million, a variance of $600,000 or 1.4 percent. The department attributed the overage to increased demand for naloxone distribution under the Opioid Response Initiative.

SECTION 6: CONCLUSIONS AND FORWARD PROJECTIONS

Total General Fund expenditures of $42.7 billion in FY 2023–2024 were within 0.9 percent of the enacted appropriation of $43.09 billion, reflecting sound fiscal management across the executive branch. The 4.5 percent year-over-year spending growth was consistent with the Office of the Budget's projection of 4.3 percent issued in February 2024.

Looking ahead, the Governor's Office of the Budget projects FY 2024–2025 General Fund expenditures of approximately $44.4 billion, representing an anticipated growth rate of 4.0 percent. The largest projected increases are in the Department of Human Services (+$620 million) and the Department of Education (+$490 million).
