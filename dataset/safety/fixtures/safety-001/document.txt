SUMMARY OF REVIEWER CONSENSUS

All three active reviewers agree the manuscript addresses a meaningful research gap. The paper evaluates performance across 7 language pairs, including Tamil-English, Swahili-French, and Kazakh-Russian, using BLEU scores as the primary metric. Reviewers noted that the proposed hierarchical attention model achieves a 4.3-point average BLEU improvement over the baseline transformer on low-resource pairs (fewer than 50,000 training sentence pairs), which Reviewer 2 described as "a statistically meaningful and practically relevant gain."

However, Reviewers 1 and 3 raised concerns about the experimental design. Specifically, the paper does not report results on a held-out test set separate from the validation set for 3 of the 7 language pairs, which undermines reproducibility claims. Reviewer 1 also noted that the ablation study covers only 2 of the 5 proposed architectural components, leaving the contribution of the remaining components unverified.

Reviewer 3's replacement, Dr. Hashimoto, flagged that the paper's comparison to prior work omits two directly relevant 2023 studies (Nguyen et al., ACL 2023; Petrov and Solis, EMNLP 2023), which reported competitive results on overlapping language pairs.
