EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This report evaluates the current deployment of Salesforce Sales Cloud Enterprise Edition (v.58.0) and Microsoft Power BI Premium (Gen2) across Meridian Financial Partners, a mid-market wealth management firm headquartered in Charlotte, North Carolina, with 847 employees across 14 regional offices. The assessment was conducted between June 3 and August 29, 2024, encompassing 23 structured interviews, 4 departmental workshops, and analysis of 18 months of platform usage telemetry data.

Key findings indicate that Meridian is utilizing approximately 61% of licensed Salesforce functionality and 44% of Power BI Premium capabilities, representing a combined annual underutilization cost of $312,000 against a total platform spend of $1.84 million per year. Three critical capability gaps have been identified that are directly impacting revenue generation and client retention.

3. IDENTIFIED CAPABILITY GAPS

Gap 1: Absence of Automated Client Onboarding Workflows

Meridian's wealth management advisors currently spend an average of 6.4 hours per new client onboarding event, compared to an industry benchmark of 2.1 hours as reported by Celent Research in their 2023 Wealth Management Operations Survey. With 1,340 new clients onboarded in fiscal year 2023, this represents approximately 5,762 excess advisor hours annually — equivalent to 2.77 full-time employees at an average fully-loaded cost of $127,000 per advisor, totaling an estimated $351,000 in avoidable labor cost.

Salesforce Flow Builder and Einstein Document Automation, both included in Meridian's current Enterprise license, remain entirely unconfigured. Implementation of these tools is estimated to reduce onboarding time to 2.8 hours per client based on comparable deployments at Harrington Wealth Group (Phoenix, AZ) and Coastal Capital Advisors (Tampa, FL), which reported 56% and 61% reductions respectively following similar configurations completed in Q1 2024.

Gap 2: Disconnected Data Pipeline Between Salesforce and Power BI

Currently, Meridian's BI team exports Salesforce data via manual CSV downloads on a weekly schedule every Monday at 6:00 AM, performed by Marcus Tran or his backup, Priya Nambiar. This process introduces a maximum data latency of 7 days, meaning that sales leadership reviews pipeline reports that may be up to 168 hours out of date.

During the assessment, 3 of 14 regional office directors reported making resource allocation decisions based on stale pipeline data, contributing to what the VP of Sales, Robert Ecklund, described as "at least two misallocated territory expansions in Q4 2023." The Salesforce Connector for Power BI (available at no additional cost under Meridian's existing agreements) supports near-real-time DirectQuery refresh intervals of 15 minutes. Activation requires an estimated 40 hours of configuration work and has been formally scoped by the IT team but deprioritized for 14 consecutive months.

Gap 3: Underutilization of Pardot for Advisor-Led Marketing

Meridian's Marketing team manages 22 active Pardot users and executed 94 email campaigns in fiscal year 2023, generating 2,847 marketing-qualified leads. However, the firm's 214 wealth management advisors have zero access to Pardot's Engage module, which allows individual advisors to send pre-approved, compliance-reviewed email sequences directly from Salesforce without Marketing team involvement.

Competitor analysis indicates that firms deploying advisor-led marketing automation report a 19% to 34% increase in referral conversion rates (source: Broadridge Financial Solutions, "Advisor Technology Adoption Study," February 2024). Pardot Engage is included in Meridian's current Pardot license at no incremental cost. The primary barrier identified is a compliance review process that has not been initiated, despite a formal request submitted by Marketing Director Sandra Osei on January 8, 2024.

4. SECONDARY GAPS AND OBSERVATIONS

The following issues were noted as secondary priority items requiring monitoring but not immediate remediation:

- Power BI Row-Level Security (RLS) is configured on only 11 of 87 production reports, creating potential data governance exposure for 76 reports that display cross-regional financial data.
- Salesforce duplicate management rules are active for Account objects but not for Contact or Lead objects, resulting in an estimated 14,200 duplicate contact records identified during a data quality audit conducted August 5-9, 2024.
- Einstein Activity Capture, licensed as part of the Enterprise package, has not been enabled. Advisors manually log 100% of client interactions, and survey data indicates this consumes an average of 47 minutes per advisor per day across the wealth management team.
- The Salesforce mobile application has been downloaded by only 38 of 312 licensed users (12.2%), despite 67% of advisors reporting that they conduct client meetings off-site.

6. RECOMMENDATIONS AND PRIORITIZATION

Priority 1 (Immediate — Q4 2024):
Activate the Salesforce-to-Power BI DirectQuery connector. This is the highest-effort-to-value ratio item, requiring only 40 hours of work with zero additional licensing cost and immediate operational benefit for sales leadership.

Priority 2 (Q4 2024 – Q1 2025):
Configure Salesforce Flow Builder and Einstein Document Automation for client onboarding. Engage Salesforce Professional Services or certified partner Slalom Consulting (current preferred vendor under MSA signed April 2023) to lead the 320-hour implementation.

Priority 3 (Q1 2025):
Initiate Pardot Engage compliance review. Marketing Director Sandra Osei and Chief Compliance Officer Thomas Bergfeld should co-chair a working group targeting a 60-day review cycle with a target go-live of March 31, 2025.

Priority 4 (Q1–Q2 2025):
Enable Einstein Activity Capture and conduct a firm-wide mobile application adoption campaign targeting a minimum 60% download rate among licensed users by June 30, 2025.
