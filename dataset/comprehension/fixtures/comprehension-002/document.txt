EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This report evaluates the current software platform ecosystem deployed across Meridian Financial Services, Inc., a mid-market financial services firm headquartered in Columbus, Ohio, with approximately 1,340 full-time employees across seven regional offices. The assessment was conducted between June 3 and August 29, 2024, encompassing 14 distinct business units and drawing on 87 structured stakeholder interviews, 3 workflow observation sessions, and analysis of system utilization logs spanning 18 months (January 2023 through June 2024).

The primary objective was to identify active use cases, redundant capabilities, underutilized licenses, and functional gaps that represent either cost inefficiency or operational risk. Key findings indicate that Meridian is currently spending approximately $4.2 million annually on software licensing across its core platform stack, with an estimated 31% of licensed capacity going unused. Simultaneously, 6 critical workflow gaps were identified that are currently addressed through manual workarounds, spreadsheet-based processes, or entirely absent tooling.

SECTION 2: USE CASE MAPPING BY DEPARTMENT

2.1 Sales and Client Relationship Management

The Sales division (214 users) relies on Salesforce Sales Cloud as its primary CRM. Core use cases include opportunity tracking, contact management, and pipeline forecasting. However, 68% of interviewed sales representatives reported that they maintain parallel records in Microsoft Excel spreadsheets because Salesforce's reporting interface is considered "too slow" for daily use. Average Salesforce page load time measured during the observation period was 4.7 seconds, compared to the vendor's SLA benchmark of 1.5 seconds — a gap attributable to 47 custom Apex triggers installed between 2019 and 2021 that have not been reviewed or optimized.

Salesforce's Einstein Activity Capture feature, included in the Enterprise license, is activated for only 12 of 214 users. This represents a significant missed opportunity for automated activity logging.

2.2 IT Service Management

The IT department (43 active ServiceNow users) uses the platform exclusively for incident ticketing. The average monthly ticket volume is 1,840 incidents, with a mean resolution time of 6.2 hours. Industry benchmark for comparable organizations is 4.1 hours (HDI 2023 Support Center Practices Report). The gap is partially attributable to the absence of automated routing rules, which ServiceNow's Flow Designer could address but has not been implemented.

2.3 Finance and Analytics

The Finance team uses Tableau for executive dashboards and quarterly reporting. Of 88 active users, 61 hold Viewer licenses ($12 per user/month) and 27 hold Creator licenses ($70 per user/month). Stakeholder interviews revealed that 19 of the 61 Viewer-license holders have requested the ability to build their own reports — a capability requiring Creator access. Conversely, 8 Creator-license holders have not logged into Tableau in more than 90 days.

Snowflake is used by the Data Engineering team (18 users) for data warehousing and transformation pipelines. Monthly compute spend averages $56,700, with peak usage occurring during month-end close cycles (typically the 1st through 5th of each month), during which costs spike to approximately $94,000.

2.4 Human Resources

Workday HCM is the one platform achieving full seat utilization (100%). It supports payroll processing for all 1,340 employees, benefits administration, performance review cycles, and recruiting workflows. The most recent performance review cycle (Q1 2024) processed 1,287 individual reviews with a completion rate of 96.1%, up from 88.4% in Q1 2023.

SECTION 3: GAP ASSESSMENT

The following six functional gaps were identified as high-priority based on frequency of stakeholder mention, estimated productivity impact, and risk exposure.

Gap 1: Contract Lifecycle Management (CLM)
No dedicated CLM platform exists. Legal and Procurement teams manage approximately 340 active vendor contracts using a shared SharePoint folder and a manually maintained Excel tracker. Three contract renewals were missed in 2023 (February, July, and November), resulting in auto-renewal charges totaling $218,000 that were not budgeted. Recommended solution: Ironclad or Conga CLM, estimated implementation cost $85,000–$120,000, annual license $60,000–$90,000.

Gap 2: Customer Onboarding Workflow Automation
New client onboarding requires coordination across Sales, Compliance, Legal, and Operations. The current process involves 23 manual handoff steps documented in a Word document last updated on January 8, 2023. Average onboarding cycle time is 18 business days. Peer benchmarking against three comparable firms (conducted July 2024) shows an industry median of 9 business days. The gap represents an estimated $1.4 million in delayed revenue recognition annually based on average contract values and time-to-revenue calculations.

Gap 3: Advanced ITSM Capabilities
As noted in Section 2.2, ServiceNow's Change Management and Problem Management modules remain unconfigured. Implementing these modules within the existing license would cost approximately $45,000 in professional services and is projected to reduce mean incident resolution time by 28%, bringing Meridian to within 12% of the HDI benchmark.

Gap 4: Integrated Data Governance
No platform currently enforces data classification, lineage tracking, or access governance across Snowflake and the broader data ecosystem. This gap creates regulatory exposure under Ohio's data protection requirements and Meridian's obligations under SEC Rule 17a-4 for records retention. Collibra Data Intelligence Cloud has been evaluated and is estimated at $180,000 annually for the required tier.

Gap 5: Employee Learning Management
Meridian has no Learning Management System (LMS). Compliance training is tracked via a spreadsheet maintained by one HR coordinator. In 2023, the firm failed to document completion of mandatory anti-money laundering (AML) training for 34 employees prior to a regulatory examination, resulting in a corrective action notice issued by the Ohio Division of Financial Institutions on April 17, 2023. Workday Learning (an add-on module) is the recommended path given existing Workday investment, estimated at $4.20 per employee per month ($67,400 annually).

Gap 6: Unified Communications Analytics
Zoom is used for 876 users but no analytics layer exists to measure meeting effectiveness, collaboration patterns, or communication load distribution. Zoom's native analytics dashboard is not enabled. This gap limits the People Operations team's ability to assess remote work productivity, a stated priority in Meridian's 2024–2026 Strategic Plan published February 2024.

SECTION 4: COST OPTIMIZATION OPPORTUNITIES

Based on the utilization analysis, the following immediate actions are recommended:

1. Right-size Salesforce from 350 to 250 seats: Estimated annual savings of $174,857 (based on $1,749/seat/year Enterprise pricing).

2. Reduce ServiceNow from 120 to 60 seats pending module expansion: Estimated savings of $120,000 annually.

3. Realign Tableau licenses — convert 8 inactive Creator licenses to Viewer, upgrade 19 Viewer licenses to Creator: Net annual savings of approximately $4,944.

4. Renegotiate DocuSign from 150 to 80 seats: Estimated savings of $28,800 annually.

5. Implement Snowflake query optimization and workload scheduling to reduce off-peak compute waste: Projected 15% reduction in monthly spend, saving approximately $102,000 annually.

Total projected annual savings from optimization: $430,601
Total projected annual investment in gap remediation (priority items): $307,400
Net first-year benefit: $123,201
Net second-year benefit (full savings, annualized): $430,601
